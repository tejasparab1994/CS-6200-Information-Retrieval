african easterly jet  contents  formation and characteristicsedit impactedit the mid-level african easterly jet is considered to play a crucial role in the southwest monsoon of africa3 and helps form the tropical waves that move across the tropical atlantic and eastern pacific oceans during the warm season.4 the jet exhibits both barotropic and baroclinic instability which produces synoptic scale westward-propagating disturbances in the jet known as african easterly waves or tropical waves a small number of mesoscale storm systems embedded in these waves develop into tropical cyclones after they move from west africa into the tropical atlantic mainly during august and september when the jet is south of normal during the peak months of the atlantic hurricane season tropical cyclone formation is suppressed.5 see alsoedit referencesedit further readingedit navigation menu personal tools namespaces variants views more search navigation interaction tools print/export languages
