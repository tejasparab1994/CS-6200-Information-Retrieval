geometric terms of location geometric terms of location describe directions or positions relative to the shape of an object these terms are used in descriptions of engineering physics and other sciences as well as ordinary day to day discourse though these terms by themselves may be somewhat ambiguous they are usually used in a context in which their meaning is clear for example when referring to a drive shaft it is clear what is meant by axial or radial directions or in a free body diagram one may similarly infer a sense of orientation by the forces or other vectors represented examplesedit common geometric terms of location are referencesedit navigation menu personal tools namespaces variants views more search navigation interaction tools print/export languages
