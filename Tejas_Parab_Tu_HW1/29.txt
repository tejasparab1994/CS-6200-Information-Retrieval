title="National Weather Service">National Weather Service</a> defines a gale as 34\xe2\x80\x9347 <a href="/wiki/Knot_(unit)" title="Knot (unit)">knots</a> (<span class="nowrap">63\xe2\x80\x9387 km/h</span>, <span class="nowrap">17.5\xe2\x80\x9324.2 m/s</span> or <span class="nowrap">39\xe2\x80\x9354 miles/hour</span>) of sustained surface winds.<sup id="cite_ref-noaa_glossary_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-noaa_glossary-1">[1]</a></sup> Forecasters typically issue <a href="/wiki/Gale_warning" title="Gale warning">gale warnings</a> when winds of this strength are expected.</p>\n<p>Other sources use minima as low as 28 knots (52&#160;km/h, 32&#160;mph) and maxima as high as 90 knots (170&#160;km/h, 100&#160;mph). Through 1986, the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> used the term gale to refer to winds of <a href="/wiki/Tropical" class="mw-redirect" title="Tropical">tropical</a> force for coastal areas, between 33 knots (61&#160;km/h, 38&#160;mph) and 63 knots (117&#160;km/h, 72&#160;mph). The 90 knots (170&#160;km/h, 100&#160;mph) definition is very non-standard. A common alternative definition of the maximum is 55 knots (102&#160;km/h, 63&#160;mph).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The most common way of measuring winds is with the <a href="/wiki/Beaufort_scale" title="Beaufort scale">Beaufort scale</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> which defines a gale as wind from 50 to 102&#160;km/h. It is an empirical measure for describing wind speed based mainly on observed sea conditions. Its full name is the Beaufort Wind Force Scale.</p>\n<p>On the <a href="/wiki/Beaufort_Wind_Scale" class="mw-redirect" title="Beaufort Wind Scale">Beaufort Wind Scale</a>, a gale is classified as: 7: Moderate&#160;Gale (32\xe2\x80\x9338 miles per hour), 8: Fresh&#160;Gale (39-46&#160;mph), 9: Strong&#160;Gale (47-54&#160;mph) and 10: Storm/Whole&#160;Gale (55-63&#160;mph). A gale is a type of Wind Description preceded by 0: Calm, 1: Light Air, 2: Light Breeze, 3: Gentle Breeze, 4: Moderate Breeze, 5: Fresh Breeze, 6: Strong Breeze and succeeded by 11: Violent Storm and 12: Hurricane on a Beaufort Wind Scale. There is a unique Beaufort Scale number and a unique Arrow Indication for each type of Wind Description mentioned above.</p>\n<p>The word <i>gale</i> is derived from the older <i>gail</i>, but its origin is uncertain.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap">\n<ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-noaa_glossary-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-noaa_glossary_1-0">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.nws.noaa.gov/glossary/">National Weather Service Glossary</a>, s.v. <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.nws.noaa.gov/glossary/index.php?word=Gale">"gale"</a>.</span></li>\n<li id="cite_note-2"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-2">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.novalynx.com/glossary-g.html">Glossary of Meteorological Terms</a>, NovaLynx Corporation.</span></li>\n<li id="cite_note-3"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-3">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">see article for more on the traditional nautical use of the word "gale"</span></li>\n<li id="cite_note-4"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-4">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.etymonline.com/index.php?term=gale">"Online Etymology Dictionary"</a>.
