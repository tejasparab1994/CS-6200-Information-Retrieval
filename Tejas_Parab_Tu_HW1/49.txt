class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Fire_whirl_(FWS)_crop.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nA fire whirl with flames in the vortex</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>A <b>fire whirl</b> \xe2\x80\x93 also commonly known as a <b>fire devil</b>, or, (in many cases) erroneously, as a <b>fire tornado</b>, <b>firenado</b>, or <b>fire twister</b> \xe2\x80\x93 is a <a href="/wiki/Whirlwind" title="Whirlwind">whirlwind</a> induced by a <a href="/wiki/Fire" title="Fire">fire</a> and often made up of flame or ash. They usually start with a whirl of wind or smoke. Fire whirls may occur when intense rising heat and turbulent wind conditions combine to form whirling <a href="/wiki/Eddy_(fluid_dynamics)" title="Eddy (fluid dynamics)">eddies</a> of air. These eddies can contract into a <a href="/wiki/Tornado" title="Tornado">tornado</a>-like <a href="/wiki/Vortex" title="Vortex">vortex</a> that sucks in burning debris and combustible gases.</p>\n<p>Fire whirls are sometimes colloquially called fire tornadoes, but are not usually classifiable as tornadoes as the vortex in most cases does not extend from the surface to cloud base. Also, even in such cases, even those fire whirls are not classic tornadoes, in that their <a href="/wiki/Vorticity" title="Vorticity">vorticity</a> derives from surface <a href="/wiki/Wind" title="Wind">winds</a> and heat-induced lifting, rather than a tornadic <a href="/wiki/Mesocyclone" title="Mesocyclone">mesocyclone</a> aloft. The literal fire tornado is a rare phenomenon, much more powerful and destructive than the a typical fire whirl, in which the <a href="/wiki/Wildfire" title="Wildfire">wildfire</a> generates a <a href="/wiki/Cumulonimbus_flammagenitus_(cloud)" title="Cumulonimbus flammagenitus (cloud)">pyrocumulonimbus</a> cloud that then <a href="/wiki/Tornadogenesis" title="Tornadogenesis">spawns a tornado</a>. When this tornado forms in or enters the fire zone, it fans flames enormously, draws flame and burning debris into itself, and scatters burning debris over a wide area.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Formation"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Formation</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Examples"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Examples</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#Classification"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">Classification</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-2 tocsection-5"><a href="#Notes"><span class="tocnumber">4.1</span> <span class="toctext">Notes</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-2 tocsection-6"><a href="#Bibliography"><span class="tocnumber">4.2</span> <span class="toctext">Bibliography</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-7"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n<p></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation">Formation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fire_whirl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Formation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>A fire whirl consists of a core of ash, and an invisible rotating pocket of air. A fire whirl can reach up to 2,000&#160;\xc2\xb0F (1,090&#160;\xc2\xb0C), hot enough to potentially reignite <a href="/wiki/Wood_ash" title="Wood ash">ashes</a> sucked up from the ground. Often, fire whirls are created when a wildfire or firestorm creates its own wind, which can turn into a vortex of fire .This causes the tall and skinny appearance of a fire whirl\'s core.</p>\n<p>Fire whirls most often move fairly slowly. Fire whirls can set objects in their paths ablaze and can hurl burning debris out into their surroundings. The winds generated by a fire whirl can also be dangerous. Large fire whirls can create wind speeds of more than 100&#160;mph (160&#160;km/h) \xe2\x80\x93 strong enough to knock down trees \xe2\x80\x93 and people who get caught by a fire whirl can be burned alive.</p>\n<p>Fire whirls can last for an hour or more, and they cannot be extinguished directly.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Examples">Examples</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fire_whirl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Examples">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Fire-whirl.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Fire-whirl.jpg/300px-Fire-whirl.jpg" width="300" height="200" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Fire-whirl.jpg/450px-Fire-whirl.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Fire-whirl.jpg/600px-Fire-whirl.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="427" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Fire-whirl.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nAnother fire whirl</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>An extreme example of a fire tornado is the <a href="/wiki/1923_Great_Kant%C5%8D_earthquake" title="1923 Great Kant\xc5\x8d earthquake">1923 Great Kant\xc5\x8d earthquake</a> in Japan which ignited a large city-sized <a href="/wiki/Firestorm" title="Firestorm">firestorm</a> and produced a gigantic fire whirl that killed 38,000 people in fifteen minutes in the Hifukusho-Ato region of <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fire_behavior_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fire_behavior-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Another example is the numerous large fire whirls (some tornadic) that developed after <a href="/wiki/Lightning" title="Lightning">lightning</a> struck an oil storage facility near <a href="/wiki/San_Luis_Obispo,_California" title="San Luis Obispo, California">San Luis Obispo</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> on 7 April 1926, several of which produced significant structural damage well away from the fire, killing two. Many whirlwinds were produced by the four-day-long firestorm coincident with conditions that produced severe <a href="/wiki/Thunderstorm" title="Thunderstorm">thunderstorms</a>, in which the larger fire whirls carried debris 5 kilometers away.<sup id="cite_ref-San_Luis_Obispo_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-San_Luis_Obispo-3">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<p>A fire whirl, of reportedly uncommon size for New Zealand wildfires, formed on day three of the <a href="/wiki/2017_Port_Hills_fires" title="2017 Port Hills fires">2017 Port Hills fires</a> in <a href="/wiki/Christchurch" title="Christchurch">Christchurch</a>. Pilots estimated the fire column to be 100 metres (330&#160;ft) high.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Classification">Classification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fire_whirl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Classification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>There are currently three known types of fire whirls:<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<ul>\n<li>Type 1: Stable and centered over burning area.</li>\n<li>Type 2: Stable or transient, downwind of burning area.</li>\n<li>Type 3: Steady or transient, centered over an open area adjacent to an asymmetric burning area with wind.</li>\n</ul>\n<p>There is evidence suggesting that the fire whirl in the Hifukusho-ato area, during the Great Kanto Earthquake of 1923, was of type 3.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fire_whirl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fire_whirl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em;
