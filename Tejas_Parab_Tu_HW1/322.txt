colspan="2"></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Hurricane Paul</b> was a particularly deadly and destructive <a href="/wiki/Pacific_hurricane" title="Pacific hurricane">Pacific hurricane</a> which killed a total of 1,696 people and caused $1.156&#160;billion in damage. The sixteenth <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">named storm</a> and tenth hurricane of the <a href="/wiki/1982_Pacific_hurricane_season" title="1982 Pacific hurricane season">1982 Pacific hurricane season</a>, Paul developed as a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical depression</a> just offshore Central America on September 18. The depression briefly moved inland two days later just before heading westward out to sea. The storm changed little in strength for several days until September 25, when it slowly intensified into a tropical storm. Two days later, Paul attained hurricane status, and further strengthened to <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_Hurricane_Scale#Category_2" class="mw-redirect" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson Hurricane Scale">Category 2 intensity</a> after turning northward. The hurricane then accelerated toward the northeast, reaching peak winds of 110&#160;mph (175&#160;km/h). Paul made landfall over <a href="/wiki/Baja_California_Sur" title="Baja California Sur">Baja California Sur</a> on September 29, and subsequently moved ashore in <a href="/wiki/Sinaloa" title="Sinaloa">Sinaloa</a>, Mexico the next day.</p>\n<p>Prior to making landfall near the <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">El Salvador</a>\xe2\x80\x93<a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a> border as a tropical depression, the precursor disturbance dropped heavy rainfall over <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">Nicaragua</a>, which later spread into El Salvador and Guatemala. Many rivers in the region burst their banks after five days of rainfall, causing severe flooding and multiple mudslides. Throughout Central America, at least 1,432&#160;people were killed, with most of the fatalities occurring in El Salvador and Guatemala. Another 225&#160;deaths were attributed to floods from the depression in southern Mexico. In addition, Paul was responsible for moderate damage and 24&#160;fatalities in northwestern Mexico, where it made landfall at hurricane strength.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc">\n<div class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Paul_(1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Paul_1982_track.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Paul_1982_track.png/220px-Paul_1982_track.png" width="220" height="136" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Paul_1982_track.png/330px-Paul_1982_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Paul_1982_track.png/440px-Paul_1982_track.png 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="633" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Paul_1982_track.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nMap plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>The precursor disturbance to Paul originated from an area of <a href="/wiki/Low-pressure_area" title="Low-pressure area">low barometric pressure</a> and disorganized <a href="/wiki/Thunderstorm" title="Thunderstorm">thunderstorms</a>, which was first noted near the Pacific coast of Nicaragua on September&#160;15. Several days later, <a href="/wiki/Weather_satellite" title="Weather satellite">satellite imagery</a> indicated it had developed a center of <a href="/wiki/Cyclone" title="Cyclone">cyclonic circulation</a>; on 1800 <a href="/wiki/UTC" class="mw-redirect" title="UTC">UTC</a> September 20, the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Pacific_Hurricane_Center" title="Eastern Pacific Hurricane Center">Eastern Pacific Hurricane Center</a> initiated advisories on the system and classified it as Tropical Depression Twenty-Two. At that time, it was located 200&#160;mi (320&#160;km) west of <a href="/wiki/Managua,_Nicaragua" class="mw-redirect" title="Managua, Nicaragua">Managua, Nicaragua</a> and supported winds of 35&#160;mph (50&#160;km/h). The depression turned northward in response to a weak <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone#Steering_winds" title="Tropical cyclone">steering flow</a> between two <a href="/wiki/High_pressure" title="High pressure">high pressure</a> systems\xe2\x80\x94one near <a href="/wiki/Cabo_San_Lucas" title="Cabo San Lucas">Cabo San Lucas</a> and the other west of Central America. It then moved inland near the El Salvador\xe2\x80\x93Guatemala border, and dissipated overland.<sup id="cite_ref-MWR_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Under the influence of a persistent stationary <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a> near <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, the remains of the depression retraced westward back over the open waters of the Pacific. Advisories on the system were resumed late on September&#160;20. Though it was reconsidered a tropical cyclone, its wind circulation was poorly defined; the depression again degenerated into an open trough at 0000 UTC September 22. Its forward motion remained relatively unchanged for several days, and by September 24 the system was reclassified as a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a>. After briefly drifting northward, the system began tracking toward the west-northwest. It gradually organized into a tropical storm at 0000 UTC September 25. Since it was then situated over favorable <a href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperature" title="Sea surface temperature">sea surface temperatures</a> between 28&#160;\xc2\xb0C (82&#160;\xc2\xb0F) and 29&#160;\xc2\xb0C (84&#160;\xc2\xb0F), Paul underwent a phase of <a href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapid deepening">rapid intensification</a>. This allowed it to reach <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_Hurricane_Scale#Category_1" class="mw-redirect" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson Hurricane Scale">Category 1 hurricane</a> strength on the <a href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale" class="mw-redirect" title="Saffir-Simpson hurricane wind scale">Saffir-Simpson hurricane wind scale</a> (SSHWS) just two days after its naming.<sup id="cite_ref-MWR_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Upon becoming a hurricane, Paul turned to the north and continued to develop.<sup id="cite_ref-MWR_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-1">[1]</a></sup> As the storm neared <a href="/wiki/Baja_California_Sur" title="Baja California Sur">Baja California Sur</a>, it reached Category 2 intensity.<sup id="cite_ref-EPAC_HURDAT_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EPAC_HURDAT-2">[2]</a></sup> An <a href="/wiki/Tropical_upper_tropospheric_trough" title="Tropical upper tropospheric trough">upper-level trough</a> forced the hurricane to accelerate towards the northeast, at which point it had reached peak wind speeds of 110&#160;mph (180&#160;km/h). From 1800 to 2100 UTC on September 29, the <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a> of the hurricane made landfall along Baja California Sur, moving ashore less than 100&#160;mi (160&#160;km) south of <a href="/wiki/La_Paz,_Baja_California_Sur" title="La Paz, Baja California Sur">La Paz</a> near San Jos\xc3\xa9 del Cabo. After weakening slightly inland, Paul briefly reemerged over water and subsequently made its final landfall near <a href="/wiki/Los_Mochis" title="Los Mochis">Los Mochis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sinaloa" title="Sinaloa">Sinaloa</a> with winds of 100&#160;mph (165&#160;km/h).<sup id="cite_ref-MWR_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-1">[1]</a></sup> Tropical cyclone advisories were discontinued shortly thereafter,<sup id="cite_ref-EPAC_HURDAT_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EPAC_HURDAT-2">[2]</a></sup> though exact information on the storm after it moved inland is unavailable due to a lack of data completion in the <a href="/wiki/HURDAT" title="HURDAT">hurricane database</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-EPAC_Book_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EPAC_Book-3">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Paul_(1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Paul_1982.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" At least 85 homes in <a href="/wiki/La_Paz" title="La Paz">La Paz</a> sustained damage, and many telephone lines in the region were down at the height of the storm.<sup id="cite_ref-HHSCOM_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HHSCOM-7">[7]</a></sup> Wind gusts estimated at 120&#160;mph (195&#160;km/h) swept through <a href="/wiki/San_Jos%C3%A9_del_Cabo" title="San Jos\xc3\xa9 del Cabo">San Jos\xc3\xa9 del Cabo</a>, causing property damage and subsequently leaving 9,000 homeless.<sup id="cite_ref-MWR_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-1">[1]</a></sup> Despite the damage, no deaths were reported in the wake of Paul.<sup id="cite_ref-HHBC_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HHBC-42">[42]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Northwest_Mexico_and_southwest_United_States">Northwest Mexico and southwest United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Paul_(1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Northwest Mexico and southwest United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Upon making its final landfall in <a href="/wiki/Sinaloa" title="Sinaloa">Sinaloa</a>, Paul produced hurricane-force winds recorded at 100&#160;mph (160&#160;km/h) in <a href="/wiki/Los_Mochis" title="Los Mochis">Los Mochis</a>. The winds demolished numerous homes in the region, leaving 140,000 residents homeless and another 400,000 people isolated.<sup id="cite_ref-MS_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MS-43">[43]</a></sup> The greatest damage occurred 70&#160;miles (110&#160;km) south of Los Mochis in the city of <a href="/wiki/Guamuchil" class="mw-redirect" title="Guamuchil">Guamuchil</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-MWR_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-1">[1]</a></sup> some houses suffered total destruction, while many other had their roof blown off. A total of 24 people were killed by the storm, although it produced beneficial rains over the region.<sup id="cite_ref-24KFH_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24KFH-14">[14]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The worst flooding occurred near the Rio Sinaloa; 7.9&#160;in (200&#160;mm) to 12&#160;in (300&#160;mm) of rain fell in some locations. Over 25,000 homes were damaged.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> Agricultural damage was severe in the state of Sinaloa, with up to 40 percent of the <a href="/wiki/Soybean" title="Soybean">soybean</a> crop destroyed. Sugar cane, tomato, and rice crops also sustained damage from the hurricane, and in its wake the state\'s corn production was down by 26 percent from the previous year. Total storm damage in Mexico amounted to $4.5&#160;billion (1982 <a href="/wiki/Mexican_peso" title="Mexican peso">MXN</a>; $70 million <a href="/wiki/USD" class="mw-redirect" title="USD">USD</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-MWR_1-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The remnants of Paul moved into the United States, producing heavy rainfall in southern <a href="/wiki/New_Mexico" title="New Mexico">New Mexico</a> and extreme West <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>. Inclement weather was observed as far inland as the <a href="/wiki/Great_Plains" title="Great Plains">Great Plains</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SHT_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SHT-45">[45]</a></sup> A combination of rain and snow moved into <a href="/wiki/Colorado" title="Colorado">Colorado</a>; 6&#160;in (150&#160;mm) of snow was expected in <a href="/wiki/Wyoming" title="Wyoming">Wyoming</a>, thus <a href="/wiki/Winter_storm_warning" title="Winter storm warning">winter storm warnings</a> were required for parts of the state.<sup id="cite_ref-CWFBHP_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CWFBHP-46">[46]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Paul_(1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In the aftermath of the storm, the Government of El Salvador was criticized for failing to keep the public well informed. It provided over $300,000 in aid and declared a <a href="/wiki/State_of_emergency" title="State of emergency">state of emergency</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-ESDTH565AMNF_31-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ESDTH565AMNF-31">[31]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DTETH500ISFD_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DTETH500ISFD-35">[35]</a></sup> additionally, a state of mourning was declared.<sup id="cite_ref-ESIM_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ESIM-47">[47]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations World Food program</a> began distributing food set aside for victims of the <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador_Civil_War" class="mw-redirect" title="El Salvador Civil War">El Salvador Civil War</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DTFFIESR232_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DTFFIESR232-48">[48]</a></sup> The U.S. Embassy in Guatemala provided $25,000 in aid for the country.<sup id="cite_ref-LRUSDA_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LRUSDA-49">[49]</a></sup> Mexican authorities rushed to supply food and water to the homeless.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a
