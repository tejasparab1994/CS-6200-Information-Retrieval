border-top:#aaa 1px solid;">Part of the <b><a href="/wiki/2015_Pacific_hurricane_season" title="2015 Pacific hurricane season">2015 Pacific hurricane season</a></b></td>\n</tr>\n<tr style="display:none">\n<td colspan="2"></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Hurricane Patricia</b> was the second-most intense <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> on record worldwide, behind <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Tip" title="Typhoon Tip">Typhoon Tip</a> in 1979, with a minimum <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" title="Atmospheric pressure">atmospheric pressure</a> of 872&#160;mbar (hPa; 25.75&#160;inHg).<sup id="cite_ref-TCR_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Originating from a sprawling disturbance near the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Tehuantepec" title="Gulf of Tehuantepec">Gulf of Tehuantepec</a>, south of Mexico, in mid-October&#160;2015, Patricia was first classified a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_depression" class="mw-redirect" title="Tropical depression">tropical depression</a> on October&#160;20. Initial development was slow, with only modest strengthening within the first day of its classification. The system later became a tropical storm and was named Patricia, the twenty-fourth named storm of the <a href="/wiki/2015_Pacific_hurricane_season" title="2015 Pacific hurricane season">annual hurricane season</a>. Exceptionally favorable environmental conditions fueled <a href="/wiki/Rapid_intensification" title="Rapid intensification">explosive intensification</a> on October&#160;22. A well-defined <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a> developed within an intense <a href="/wiki/Central_dense_overcast" title="Central dense overcast">central dense overcast</a> and Patricia grew from a tropical storm to a <a href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_5" class="mw-redirect" title="SSHS">Category&#160;5 hurricane</a> in just 24&#160;hours\xe2\x80\x94a near-record pace. On October&#160;23, the hurricane achieved its record peak intensity with <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">maximum sustained winds</a> of 215&#160;mph (345&#160;km/h).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[nb 1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[nb 2]</a></sup> This made it the most intense tropical cyclone on record in the <a href="/wiki/Western_Hemisphere" title="Western Hemisphere">Western Hemisphere</a>, and the strongest globally in terms of 1-minute maximum sustained winds.</p>\n<p>Late on October&#160;23, dramatic weakening ensued and Patricia made <a href="/wiki/Landfall" title="Landfall">landfall</a> near <a href="/wiki/Cuixmala" title="Cuixmala">Cuixmala</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jalisco" title="Jalisco">Jalisco</a>, with winds of 150&#160;mph (240&#160;km/h). This made it the strongest landfalling hurricane on record along the Pacific coast of Mexico. Patricia continued to weaken extremely quickly, faster than it had intensified, as it interacted with the mountainous terrain of Mexico. Within 24&#160;hours of moving ashore, Patricia weakened into a tropical depression and dissipated soon thereafter, late on October&#160;24.</p>\n<p>The precursor to Patricia produced widespread flooding rains in <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a>. Hundreds of thousands of people were directly affected by the storm, mostly in <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a>. At least six fatalities were attributed to the event: four in <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">El Salvador</a>, one in Guatemala, and one in <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">Nicaragua</a>. Torrential rains extended into southeastern Mexico, with areas of <a href="/wiki/Quintana_Roo" title="Quintana Roo">Quintana Roo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Veracruz" title="Veracruz">Veracruz</a> reporting accumulations in excess of 19.7&#160;in (500&#160;mm). Damage in <a href="/wiki/Chetumal" title="Chetumal">Chetumal</a> reached 1.4&#160;billion <a href="/wiki/Mexican_peso" title="Mexican peso">pesos</a> (US$84.1&#160;million).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[nb 3]</a></sup></p>\n<p>As a tropical cyclone, Patricia\'s effects in Mexico were tremendous; however, the affected areas were predominantly rural, mitigating a potential large-scale disaster. Violent winds tore roofs from structures and stripped coastal areas of their vegetation. Preliminary assessments indicated hundreds of homes to be destroyed; seven fatalities were linked to the hurricane directly or indirectly, including one during evacuations. Total damage was estimated to be in excess of 5.4&#160;billion pesos (US$323.3&#160;million), with agriculture and infrastructure comprising the majority of losses. Flooding partially associated with remnant moisture from Patricia inflicted US$52.5&#160;million in damage across <a href="/wiki/South_Texas" title="South Texas">Southern Texas</a>.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Meteorological_history_of_Hurricane_Patricia" title="Meteorological history of Hurricane Patricia">Meteorological history of Hurricane Patricia</a></div>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Patricia_2015_track.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Patricia_2015_track.png/250px-Patricia_2015_track.png" width="250" height="155" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Patricia_2015_track.png/375px-Patricia_2015_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Patricia_2015_track.png/500px-Patricia_2015_track.png 2x" data-file-width="3000" data-file-height="1854" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Patricia_2015_track.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nMap plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>On October&#160;11, 2015, an area of disturbed weather traversed <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a> and emerged over the eastern Pacific Ocean. The disturbance moved slowly over the next few days, later merging with a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> on October&#160;15. The merger of these systems and the effects of a concurrent <a href="/wiki/Tehuantepecer" title="Tehuantepecer">Tehuantepec gap wind event</a> spurred the formation of a broad <a href="/wiki/Low-pressure_area" title="Low-pressure area">area of low pressure</a>. This feature gradually consolidated and became a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_depression" class="mw-redirect" title="Tropical depression">tropical depression</a> shortly after 00:00&#160;<a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a> on October&#160;20; at this time the depression was situated roughly 205&#160;mi (335&#160;km) south-southeast of <a href="/wiki/Salina_Cruz" title="Salina Cruz">Salina Cruz</a>, Mexico. A <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">mid-level ridge</a> to the north steered the depression generally west and later pulled it north along an arcing path.<sup id="cite_ref-TCR_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Development was initially slowed by locally cooler <a href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperature" title="Sea surface temperature">sea surface temperatures</a> and dry air, and the depression became Tropical Storm Patricia later that day.<sup id="cite_ref-TCR_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Once clear of the unfavorable region, Patricia traversed anomalously warm waters within an environment exceptionally conducive to <a href="/wiki/Rapid_intensification" title="Rapid intensification">rapid intensification</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-TCR_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[2]</a></sup> Dramatic strengthening began late on October&#160;21 and continued through October&#160;23. Patricia reached <a href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_1" class="mw-redirect" title="SSHS">hurricane strength</a> shortly after 00:00&#160;UTC on October&#160;22,<sup id="cite_ref-TCR_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> featuring prominent <a href="/wiki/Outflow_(meteorology)" title="Outflow (meteorology)">outflow</a>, well-defined banding features,<sup id="cite_ref-D8_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-D8-6">[3]</a></sup> and a developing <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-D7_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-D7-7">[4]</a></sup> Data from <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hunters" class="mw-redirect" title="Hurricane Hunters">Hurricane Hunters</a> investigating the cyclone indicated Patricia to have reached <a href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_4" class="mw-redirect" title="SSHS">Category&#160;4 status</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale" class="mw-redirect" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson hurricane wind scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson hurricane wind scale</a> by 18:00&#160;UTC.<sup id="cite_ref-TCR_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-D10_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-D10-8">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Patricia_October_23,_2015,_GOES-15_visible_animation.gif" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Hurricane_Patricia_October_23%2C_2015%2C_GOES-15_visible_animation.gif/250px-Hurricane_Patricia_October_23%2C_2015%2C_GOES-15_visible_animation.gif" width="250" height="187" class="thumbimage" 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Hurricane_Patricia_October_23%2C_2015%2C_GOES-15_visible_animation.gif/500px-Hurricane_Patricia_October_23%2C_2015%2C_GOES-15_visible_animation.gif class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nSatellite animation of Patricia making landfall on October&#160;23</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>By the early hours of October&#160;23, a clear northward turn took place followed by acceleration northeast.<sup id="cite_ref-D11_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-D11-9">[6]</a></sup> A solid ring of \xe2\x88\x92130&#160;\xc2\xb0F (\xe2\x88\x9290&#160;\xc2\xb0C) cloud tops surrounded the hurricane\'s 12&#160;mi (19&#160;km) wide eye and signaled its intensification into a <a href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_5" class="mw-redirect" title="SSHS">Category&#160;5 hurricane</a>. In a 24-hour time span, Patricia\'s <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">maximum sustained winds</a> increased by 120&#160;mph (195&#160;km/h), the fastest such intensification in any hurricane observed by the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> (NHC). The hurricane achieved its peak around 12:00&#160;UTC on October&#160;23, with estimated winds around 215&#160;mph (345&#160;km/h) and a <a href="/wiki/Barometric_pressure" class="mw-redirect" title="Barometric pressure">barometric pressure</a> of 872&#160;mbar (hPa; 25.75&#160;inHg); these values are based upon continued intensification after a <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hunter" class="mw-redirect" title="Hurricane Hunter">Hurricane Hunter</a> mission into the storm six hours prior. In the NHC\'s report on Patricia, it is noted that the hurricane may have surpassed <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Tip" title="Typhoon Tip">Typhoon Tip</a> as the strongest tropical cyclone ever observed, but lack of direct observations at the time of its peak prevent analysis of such.<sup id="cite_ref-TCR_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Later on October&#160;23, rapid weakening ensued as an <a href="/wiki/Eyewall_replacement_cycle" title="Eyewall replacement cycle">eyewall replacement cycle</a> took shape and wind shear increased. In the five hours up until <a href="/wiki/Landfall" title="Landfall">landfall</a> in Mexico, Patricia weakened at an unprecedented rate while still over water. However, it remained a violent hurricane and moved ashore around 23:00&#160;UTC near <a href="/wiki/Cuixmala" title="Cuixmala">Cuixmala</a>, Jalisco, with winds of 150&#160;mph (240&#160;km/h) and an analyzed pressure of 932&#160;mbar (hPa; 27.49&#160;inHg). This made it the most intense Pacific hurricane on record to strike Mexico. Once onshore, the high terrain of the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Madre_Occidental" title="Sierra Madre Occidental">Sierra Madre mountains</a> accelerated Patricia\'s weakening. The low- and mid-level circulations of the tropical cyclone decoupled, with the latter accelerating northeast, and Patricia dissipated on October&#160;24 over central Mexico, less than 18&#160;hours after moving ashore.<sup id="cite_ref-TCR_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Records">Records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Patricia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Meteorological_history_of_Hurricane_Patricia#Records" title="Meteorological history of Hurricane Patricia">Meteorological history of Hurricane Patricia \xc2\xa7&#160;Records</a></div>\n<p>Hurricane Patricia set multiple records for maximum strength, rate of intensification, and rate of weakening throughout its relatively short existence. With maximum sustained winds of 215&#160;mph (345&#160;km/h) and a minimum pressure of 872&#160;mbar (hPa; 25.75&#160;inHg), Patricia is the <a href="/wiki/List_of_tropical_cyclone_records" title="List of tropical cyclone records">most intense tropical cyclone</a> ever observed in the <a href="/wiki/Western_Hemisphere" title="Western Hemisphere">Western Hemisphere</a>. In terms of central pressure, it is also the second-most intense tropical cyclone ever recorded worldwide, just shy of <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Tip" title="Typhoon Tip">Typhoon Tip</a> in 1979 which had a minimum pressure of 870&#160;mbar (hPa; 25.69&#160;inHg).<sup id="cite_ref-TCR_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Patricia\'s one-minute maximum sustained winds ranked as the highest ever reliably observed or estimated in a tropical cyclone, surpassing <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Haiyan" title="Typhoon Haiyan">Typhoon Haiyan</a> of 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-StrongestWHem_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StrongestWHem-10">[7]</a></sup> The magnitude of Patricia\'s rapid intensification is among the fastest ever observed. In a 24-hour period, 06:00\xe2\x80\x9306:00&#160;UTC October&#160;22\xe2\x80\x9323, its maximum sustained winds increased from 85&#160;mph (140&#160;km/h) to 205&#160;mph (335&#160;km/h), a record increase of 120&#160;mph (195&#160;km/h). During the same period, Patricia\'s central pressure fell by 95&#160;mbar (hPa; 2.81&#160;inHg). Despite record over-water weakening prior to striking Mexico, Patricia became the most intense Pacific hurricane to make landfall, with a pressure of 932&#160;mbar (hPa; 27.52&#160;inHg).<sup class="mw-redirect" title="Government of Mexico">Government of Mexico</a> issued multiple <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">watches and warnings</a> for coastal communities. A <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_watch" class="mw-redirect" title="Hurricane watch">hurricane watch</a> was first raised at 09:00&#160;UTC on October&#160;21, encompassing areas of <a href="/wiki/Michoac%C3%A1n" title="Michoac\xc3\xa1n">Michoac\xc3\xa1n</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colima" title="Colima">Colima</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jalisco" title="Jalisco">Jalisco</a>. A <a href="/wiki/Tropical_storm_watch" class="mw-redirect" title="Tropical storm watch">tropical storm watch</a> also covered portions of <a href="/wiki/Guerrero" title="Guerrero">Guerrero</a>. As Patricia intensified, the government issued a <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_warning" class="mw-redirect" title="Hurricane warning">hurricane warning</a> for areas between <a href="/wiki/Cabo_Corrientes,_Jalisco" title="Cabo Corrientes, Jalisco">Cabo Corrientes</a>, Jalisco, and Punta San Telmo, Michoac\xc3\xa1n; a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_storm_warning" class="mw-redirect" title="Tropical storm warning">tropical storm warning</a> supplemented this for areas farther north and east. The hurricane warning was extended northward to include areas south of <a href="/wiki/San_Blas,_Nayarit" title="San Blas, Nayarit">San Blas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nayarit" title="Nayarit">Nayarit</a>, on October&#160;23. Once the storm moved inland and the threat of damaging winds diminished, these warnings were gradually discontinued on October&#160;24.<sup id="cite_ref-TCR_1-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Patricia_(2015)_Laslovarga1.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Hurricane_Patricia_%282015%29_Laslovarga1.JPG/220px-Hurricane_Patricia_%282015%29_Laslovarga1.JPG" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Hurricane_Patricia_%282015%29_Laslovarga1.JPG/330px-Hurricane_Patricia_%282015%29_Laslovarga1.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Hurricane_Patricia_%282015%29_Laslovarga1.JPG/440px-Hurricane_Patricia_%282015%29_Laslovarga1.JPG 2x" data-file-width="5184" data-file-height="3456" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Patricia_(2015)_Laslovarga1.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nEvacuation from hotel Riu Vallarta on morning before Hurricane Patricia made landfall</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Following Patricia\'s upgrade to Category&#160;5 status, the National Hurricane Center called the storm "potentially catastrophic",<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[8]</a></sup> a source of criticism and praise from various media.<sup id="cite_ref-Forbes1_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes1-12">[9]</a></sup> Citing the relatively limited damage and loss of life, some outlets, including the <a href="/wiki/Associated_Press" title="Associated Press">Associated Press</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[10]</a></sup> claimed the agency was exaggerating the danger posed by the storm. However, most outlets praised them for effectively communicating the dire threat and potentially saving lives from an exceptionally powerful hurricane.<sup id="cite_ref-Forbes1_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes1-12">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[11]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Across Michoac\xc3\xa1n, Colima, Jalisco, and Nayarit, 1,782&#160;shelters were opened on October&#160;22 with a collective capacity of 258,000&#160;people.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[12]</a></sup> Officials in <a href="/wiki/Manzanillo,_Colima" title="Manzanillo, Colima">Manzanillo</a> began distributing sandbags during the afternoon of October&#160;22.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[13]</a></sup> Schools across <a href="/wiki/Guerrero" title="Guerrero">Guerrero</a> and Jalisco suspended activities for October&#160;22 and 23, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[15]</a></sup> Civil protection officials planned to evacuate roughly 50,000&#160;people from Colima, Jalisco, and Nayarit.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[16]</a></sup> To expedite the process, 2&#160;aircraft and 600&#160;buses were used to shuttle evacuees.<sup id="cite_ref-Cabo_Corrientes_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cabo_Corrientes-20">[17]</a></sup> Ultimately, 8,500&#160;people evacuated prior to the storm\'s arrival, including 2,600 in Cabo Corrientes.<sup id="cite_ref-Cabo_Corrientes_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cabo_Corrientes-20">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ElUniversalDamage_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ElUniversalDamage-21">[18]</a></sup> One person died during an evacuation in Jalisco.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[19]</a></sup> Roughly 25,000&#160;personnel from the <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Army" title="Mexican Army">Mexican Army</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Navy" title="Mexican Navy">Navy</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Federal_Police" class="mw-redirect" title="Mexican Federal Police">Federal Police</a> were deployed preemptively.<sup id="cite_ref-UnivisionOct26_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UnivisionOct26-23">[20]</a></sup> More than 500&#160;<a href="/wiki/Red_Cross" class="mw-redirect" title="Red Cross">Red Cross</a> volunteers were on standby.<sup id="cite_ref-RW2_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RW2-24">[21]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Red_Cross" title="Mexican Red Cross">Mexican Red Cross</a> prepositioned food for 3,500&#160;families.<sup id="cite_ref-RW1_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RW1-25">[22]</a></sup> Approximately 30&#160;kg (66&#160;lb) of aid was prepositioned in Colima for distribution after the hurricane\'s passage. The Electric Federal Commission dispatched 2,500&#160;crewmen, 152&#160;cranes, 15&#160;all-terrain vehicles, 4&#160;helicopters, and 84&#160;generators to cope with potential power outages.<sup id="cite_ref-RW2_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RW2-24">[21]</a></sup> Mexican authorities received praise for effectively carrying out emergency preparations.<sup id="cite_ref-NYTOct24_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTOct24-26">[23]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact_from_precursor_disturbance">Impact from precursor href="/wiki/File:Pre-Patricia_2015-10-17_1930Z.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/Pre-Patricia_2015-10-17_1930Z.jpg/220px-Pre-Patricia_2015-10-17_1930Z.jpg" width="220" height="274" class="thumbimage" Central America on October&#160;17</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>The precursor of Patricia was a large and sprawling system that affected much of <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a> with heavy rain for several days.<sup id="cite_ref-TCR_1-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Guat1_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guat1-27">[24]</a></sup> Rainfall was enhanced by the presence of multiple systems, including a tropical wave and a second disturbance over the Caribbean Sea.<sup id="cite_ref-TCR_1-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> One person was killed in the <a href="/wiki/Alta_Verapaz_Department" title="Alta Verapaz Department">Alta Verapaz Department</a> and approximately 2,100&#160;people required evacuation across Guatemala.<sup id="cite_ref-Guat1_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guat1-27">[24]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Guat2_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guat2-28">[25]</a></sup> A total of 442&#160;homes and 28,200&#160;ha (70,000 acres) of crops were damaged while roughly 223,000&#160;people were affected by flooding.<sup id="cite_ref-Guat3_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guat3-29">[26]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[27]</a></sup> Government officials deployed emergency teams and 40&#160;million <a href="/wiki/Guatemalan_quetzal" title="Guatemalan quetzal">quetzal</a> (US$5.4&#160;million) was available for relief operations.<sup id="cite_ref-Guat3_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guat3-29">[26]</a></sup> In nearby <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">El Dozens of homes were affected and four people were killed.<sup id="cite_ref-ElSalv1_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ElSalv1-31">[28]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ElSalv2_32-0" days, inundating surrounding communities.<sup id="cite_ref-ElSalv1_31-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ElSalv1-31">[28]</a></sup> Owing to widespread flooding, authorities suspended school activities across the country on October&#160;19.<sup id="cite_ref-ElSalv2_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ElSalv2-32">[29]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" Region">Bonanza</a>; one died while the others were rescued.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[30]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Ul%C3%BAa_River" title="Ul\xc3\xbaa River">Ul\xc3\xbaa River</a> in <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduras</a> over-topped its banks for the first time in 17&#160;years on October&#160;18, prompting the evacuation of more than 200&#160;people.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[31]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[32]</a></sup> Furthermore, flooding damaged 10&#160;homes in <a href="/wiki/Jac%C3%B3,_Costa_Rica" title="Jac\xc3\xb3, Costa Rica">Jac\xc3\xb3, Costa Rica</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[33]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The aforementioned rains extended into Southwestern and Northeastern Mexico, falling from October&#160;18\xe2\x80\x9320; precipitation was enhanced by another tropical disturbance over the northwestern Caribbean Sea. Portions of <a href="/wiki/Quintana_Roo" title="Quintana Roo">Quintana Roo</a> saw their most intense rainfall event on record, with 19.8&#160;in (502&#160;mm) observed in <a href="/wiki/Chetumal" title="Chetumal">Chetumal</a>. This surpassed the previous record of 18&#160;in (450&#160;mm) during Hurricane Wilma in 2005. Flooding affected approximately 1,500&#160;homes in the city, with 150&#160;people seeking refuge in public shelters.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[34]</a></sup> Damage in Chetumal was pegged at 1.4&#160;billion <a href="/wiki/Mexican_peso" title="Mexican peso">pesos</a> (US$84.1&#160;million).<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[35]</a></sup> Seven municipalities were declared <a href="/wiki/Disaster_areas" class="mw-redirect" title="Disaster areas">disaster areas</a> accordingly.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[36]</a></sup> In nearby <a href="/wiki/Veracruz" title="Veracruz">Veracruz</a>, more than 24&#160;in (600&#160;mm) of rain caused widespread flooding; at least 50&#160;municipalities reported damage from the event as multiple rivers over-topped their banks.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[37]</a></sup> Flooding in <a href="/wiki/Tabasco" title="Tabasco">Tabasco</a> affected 7,500&#160;ha (19,000 acres) of crops.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[38]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Oaxaca" title="Oaxaca">Oaxaca</a>, several roads were washed out by the accompanying floods and mudslides, particularly in the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Norte_de_Oaxaca" title="Sierra Norte de Oaxaca">Sierra Norte region</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[39]</a></sup> Widespread flooding affected large areas of <a href="/wiki/Chiapas" title="Chiapas">Chiapas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guerrero" title="Guerrero">Guerrero</a>, prompting dozens of evacuations.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[40]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Tapachula" title="Tapachula">Tapachula</a>, Chiapas, 6.6&#160;in (168&#160;mm) of rain fell in just 90&#160;minutes, triggering flash floods that caught residents by surprise. Hundreds of families were affected and 64&#160;homes were damaged or destroyed.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[41]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact_as_a_tropical_cyclone">Impact as a tropical cyclone</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Patricia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Impact as a tropical cyclone">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mexico">Mexico</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Patricia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Mexico">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Hurricane Patricia made landfall in Jalisco as a Category&#160;4 hurricane during the evening of October&#160;23. The 15&#160;mi (24&#160;km) wide core of Patricia largely avoided populated centers, passing through areas with a population density less than 30&#160;people per square mile. This, alongside effective evacuations, is credited for the relatively low death toll, despite the hurricane\'s intensity. Several small communities along the path sustained tremendous damage, however.<sup id="cite_ref-WUOct26_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WUOct26-45">[42]</a></sup> Had the hurricane moved farther east or west, the densely populated areas surrounding <a href="/wiki/Manzanillo,_Colima" title="Manzanillo, Colima">Manzanillo</a> or <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Vallarta" title="Puerto Vallarta">Puerto Vallarta</a> would have suffered a direct hit.<sup id="cite_ref-NYTOct24_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTOct24-26">[23]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Throughout the affected region, the storm\'s powerful winds left 261,989&#160;people without electricity.<sup id="cite_ref-ElUniversalDamage_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ElUniversalDamage-21">[18]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-UnivisionOct26_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UnivisionOct26-23">[20]</a></sup> Twenty-one of the <a href="/wiki/States_of_Mexico" title="States of Mexico">nation\'s thirty-one states</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Federal_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Mexican Federal District">Federal District</a>, were affected by the hurricane\'s rain.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[43]</a></sup> Accumulations in Jalisco peaked at 15.09&#160;in (383.2&#160;mm) on <a href="/wiki/Volc%C3%A1n_de_Colima" title="Volc\xc3\xa1n de Colima">Nevado de Colima</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[44]</a></sup> Roughly 42,000&#160;ha (100,000 acres) of crops were affected across Colima, Jalisco, Michoac\xc3\xa1n, and Nayarit, with 15,000&#160;ha (37,000 acres) deemed a total loss and 27,000&#160;ha (67,000 acres) partially damaged.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[45]</a></sup> Six deaths occurred due to direct or indirect effects of the hurricane, all in Jalisco.<sup id="cite_ref-Fox_News_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fox_News-49">[46]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Zocalo_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zocalo-50">[47]</a></sup> Damage assessments indicate total losses around 5.4&#160;billion pesos (US$323.3&#160;million), primarily from agriculture and infrastructure.<sup id="cite_ref-ElUniversalDamage_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ElUniversalDamage-21">[18]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-JaliscoTotal_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JaliscoTotal-51">[48]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ColimaTotal_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ColimaTotal-52">[49]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MichoacanDamage1_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MichoacanDamage1-53">[50]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MichoacanDamage2_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MichoacanDamage2-54">[51]</a></sup></p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Jalisco">Jalisco</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Patricia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Jalisco">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Patricia_2015-10-23_2035Z_EVL_(crop).png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Patricia_2015-10-23_2035Z_EVL_%28crop%29.png/220px-Patricia_2015-10-23_2035Z_EVL_%28crop%29.png" width="220" height="129" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Patricia_2015-10-23_2035Z_EVL_%28crop%29.png/330px-Patricia_2015-10-23_2035Z_EVL_%28crop%29.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Patricia_2015-10-23_2035Z_EVL_%28crop%29.png/440px-Patricia_2015-10-23_2035Z_EVL_%28crop%29.png 2x" data-file-width="1959" data-file-height="1149" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Patricia_2015-10-23_2035Z_EVL_(crop).png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nHurricane Patricia hours before making landfall in Jalisco</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Striking Jalisco as a strong Category&#160;4 hurricane, Patricia wrought tremendous damage, though the most extreme effects were relatively localized. In the small community of Emiliano Zapata, the hurricane\'s violent winds tore roofs off homes and business. Countless trees were defoliated, stripped of their branches, snapped, or uprooted. Hillsides along the immediate landfall area were largely stripped of vegetation. Storm chaser Josh Morgerman described the aftermath: "... the tropical landscape was transformed into something barren and wintry."<sup id="cite_ref-iCycloneReport_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iCycloneReport-55">[52]</a></sup> Furthermore, the winds toppled concrete power poles and crumpled <a href="/wiki/Transmission_tower" title="Transmission tower">transmission towers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-iCycloneReport_55-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iCycloneReport-55">[52]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Throughout Jalisco, approximately 9,000&#160;homes were damaged or destroyed.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">[53]</a></sup> The coastal <a href="/wiki/Hamlet_(place)" title="Hamlet (place)">hamlet</a> of <a href="/wiki/Chamela" title="Chamela">Chamela</a>, home to 40&#160;families, was completely flattened.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">[54]</a></sup> More than 24,000&#160;ha (59,000 acres) of crops were affected across the state: 10,684&#160;ha (26,400 acres) sustained total losses while 13,943&#160;ha (34,450 acres) were partially so. Damage from agriculture amounted to roughly 168&#160;million pesos (US$10.1&#160;million).<sup id="cite_ref-JaliscoCrops_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JaliscoCrops-58">[55]</a></sup> Total damage reached to 1.139&#160;billion pesos (US$68.7&#160;million).<sup id="cite_ref-JaliscoTotal_51-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JaliscoTotal-51">[48]</a></sup></p>\n<p>A total of six deaths in incidents related to Patricia were reported in the state.<sup id="cite_ref-Fox_News_49-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fox_News-49">[46]</a></sup> Two of these deaths occurred when a tree toppled over in the storm\'s powerful winds.<sup id="cite_ref-Zocalo_50-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zocalo-50">[47]</a></sup> A woman was also hospitalized after sustaining injuries in the same incident. Another four people were killed in an <a href="/wiki/Automobile_accident" class="mw-redirect" title="Automobile accident">automobile accident</a> in southern Jalisco. Two of the victims died in the wreckage and the other two died after being brought to a hospital.<sup id="cite_ref-Fox_News_49-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fox_News-49">[46]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-JSA_59-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JSA-59">[56]</a></sup></p>\n<p>A cargo ship\xe2\x80\x94the 735&#160;ft (224&#160;m) <a href="/wiki/Bulk_carrier" title="Bulk carrier">bulk carrier</a> <i>Los Llanitos</i><sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">[57]</a></sup>\xe2\x80\x94was shifted off-course by the hurricane and grounded near <a href="/wiki/Barra_de_Navidad" title="Barra de Navidad">Barra de Navidad</a>, Jalisco. Her 27&#160;crew were unharmed but required rescue by a military helicopter.<sup id="cite_ref-9news_61-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9news-61">[58]</a></sup> The ship was subsequently deemed a total loss and crews began <a href="/wiki/Ship_breaking" title="Ship breaking">scrapping</a> the vessel on-site in February&#160;2016.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">[59]</a></sup></p>\n<h4><span id="Colima_and_Michoac\xc3\xa1n"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Colima_and_Michoac.C3.A1n">Colima and Michoac\xc3\xa1n</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Patricia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Colima and Michoac\xc3\xa1n">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Damage_from_Hurricane_Patricia_2015_in_Colima,_Mexico.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/Damage_from_Hurricane_Patricia_2015_in_Colima%2C_Mexico.jpg/300px-Damage_from_Hurricane_Patricia_2015_in_Colima%2C_Mexico.jpg" width="300" height="200" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/Damage_from_Hurricane_Patricia_2015_in_Colima%2C_Mexico.jpg/450px-Damage_from_Hurricane_Patricia_2015_in_Colima%2C_Mexico.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/Damage_from_Hurricane_Patricia_2015_in_Colima%2C_Mexico.jpg/600px-Damage_from_Hurricane_Patricia_2015_in_Colima%2C_Mexico.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4928" data-file-height="3280" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Damage_from_Hurricane_Patricia_2015_in_Colima,_Mexico.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nCoastal damage from Patricia in Colima</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>In nearby Colima, preliminary surveys indicated localized but severe damage. A total of 200&#160;schools, 107&#160;health facilities, 34&#160;sport facilities, and 11,645&#160;ha (28,780 acres) of agriculture was affected by the storm.<sup id="cite_ref-LaJordanaColima_63-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LaJordanaColima-63">[60]</a></sup> Severe damage was incurred by the banana crops, with losses from the fruit alone estimated at 500&#160;million pesos (US$30.2&#160;million).<sup id="cite_ref-ElUniversalDamage_21-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ElUniversalDamage-21">[18]</a></sup> Total damage in the state reached 1.7&#160;billion pesos (US$102.6&#160;million).<sup id="cite_ref-ColimaTotal_52-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ColimaTotal-52">[49]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Although Patricia made landfall farther west, damage across Michoac\xc3\xa1n was severe. Numerous communities were temporarily isolated as roads were either rendered impassible or washed away altogether by floods.<sup id="cite_ref-Michoac.C3.A1n1_64-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Michoac.C3.A1n1-64">[61]</a></sup> A total of 1,512&#160;homes were damaged and another 127 were destroyed across the state,<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">[62]</a></sup> including 600 in <a href="/wiki/Coahuayana" title="Coahuayana">Coahuayana</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Michoac.C3.A1n2_66-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Michoac.C3.A1n2-66">[63]</a></sup> The storm severely damaged 150&#160;homes in the <a href="/wiki/Arteaga_Municipality,_Michoac%C3%A1n" title="Arteaga Municipality, Michoac\xc3\xa1n">Arteaga Municipality</a>. A portion of <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Federal_Highway_200" title="Mexican Federal Highway 200">Highway 200</a> was shut down in <a href="/wiki/Aquila,_Michoac%C3%A1n" title="Aquila, Michoac\xc3\xa1n">Aquila</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Michoac.C3.A1n1_64-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Michoac.C3.A1n1-64">[61]</a></sup> Agriculture sustained extensive damage with 10,000&#160;ha (25,000 acres) of crops destroyed; losses in the sector amounted to 2.5&#160;billion pesos (US$151.2&#160;million). In Coahuayana alone, 5,600&#160;ha (14,000 acres) of bananas were ruined, the greatest loss of crops in the municipality\'s history;<sup id="cite_ref-MichoacanDamage1_53-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MichoacanDamage1-53">[50]</a></sup> thousands of residents lost their jobs as a result of the damage.<sup id="cite_ref-Michoac.C3.A1n2_66-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Michoac.C3.A1n2-66">[63]</a></sup> Losses to health facilities in the state reached 13.5&#160;million pesos (US$815,000).<sup id="cite_ref-MichoacanDamage2_54-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MichoacanDamage2-54">[51]</a></sup></p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_states">Other states</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Patricia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Other states">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>The hurricane produced large swells along the shores of Guerrero on October&#160;22, causing damage to coastal structures.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">[64]</a></sup> The remnants of Patricia brought heavy rain to much of northern Mexico on October&#160;24. Up to 7.6&#160;in (193&#160;mm) of rain was observed in <a href="/wiki/Tamaulipas" title="Tamaulipas">Tamaulipas</a>, resulting in flooding. Dozens of structures sustained damage and hundreds were left without power, with areas in <a href="/wiki/Reynosa" title="Reynosa">Reynosa</a> and <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Bravo,_Tamaulipas" title="R\xc3\xado Bravo, Tamaulipas">R\xc3\xado Bravo</a> particularly affected.<sup id="cite_ref-InformadorTamaulipas_68-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-InformadorTamaulipas-68">[65]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Patricia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Patricia\'s direct effects in Texas were limited to <a href="/wiki/South_Texas" title="South Texas">southern areas of the state</a>. Moisture associated with the hurricane streamed ahead of its circulation and interacted with a frontal boundary over the region.<sup id="cite_ref-WUOct24_69-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WUOct24-69">[66]</a></sup> Antecedent rains saturated the region and produced severe flooding, with cars flooded and a train derailed.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">[67]</a></sup> Fears arose that the collective effects of these storms would produce deadly floods, similar to those in <a href="/wiki/2015_Texas%E2%80%93Oklahoma_flood_and_tornado_outbreak" title="2015 Texas\xe2\x80\x93Oklahoma flood and tornado outbreak">May earlier in the year</a>; however, no deaths resulted.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">[68]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Houston,_Texas" class="mw-redirect" title="Houston, Texas">Houston</a> received 9.38&#160;in (238&#160;mm) of rain during a 24-hour span from October&#160;24\xe2\x80\x9325,<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">[69]</a></sup> and a brief <a href="/wiki/Tornado" title="Tornado">tornado</a> caused minor damage near the city.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">[70]</a></sup> Rain fell at rates of 1 to 2&#160;in (25 to 51&#160;mm) per hour in southern <a href="/wiki/Hidalgo_County,_Texas" title="Hidalgo County, Texas">Hidalgo County</a>, leading to damaging flash floods. An estimated 10 to 12&#160;in (250 to 300&#160;mm), or more, accumulated across the <a href="/wiki/Progreso,_Texas" title="Progreso, Texas">Progreso</a>\xe2\x80\x93<a href="/wiki/Weslaco,_Texas" title="Weslaco, Texas">Weslaco</a> area. Several feet of water rendered roads impassible, stranded cars, and affected more than 500&#160;homes. Twenty-six water rescues were conducted in the area. Damage was estimated at $50&#160;million.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">[71]</a></sup> Several roads in and around <a href="/wiki/Corpus_Christi,_Texas" title="Corpus Christi, Texas">Corpus Christi</a> were inundated,<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">[72]</a></sup> including part of <a href="/wiki/Interstate_37" title="Interstate 37">Interstate 37</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">[73]</a></sup> Two hours of "blinding rains" flooded the majority of eastern <a href="/wiki/Willacy_County,_Texas" title="Willacy County, Texas">Willacy County</a>, prompting multiple water rescues, and leaving $2.5&#160;million in damage.<sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">[74]</a></sup> Remnant moisture then moved north and northwest through the Great Lakes, Appalachia and Mid-Atlantic states, and Florida Pandhandle before exiting the nation.<sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78">[75]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">[76]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Patricia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In the aftermath of the hurricane, 5,791&#160;Marines from Mexico\'s <a href="/wiki/Naval_Infantry_Force" title="Naval Infantry Force">Naval Infantry Force</a> were deployed to assist with recovery and rescue efforts.<sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">[77]</a></sup> Rescuers reached some of the hardest-hit areas within a day of Patricia\'s landfall.<sup id="cite_ref-NYTOct24_26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTOct24-26">[23]</a></sup> Red Cross volunteers began needs assessments on October&#160;24 and distribution of humanitarian aid started the following day.<sup id="cite_ref-RW1_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RW1-25">[22]</a></sup> Power was restored to 88&#160;percent of those affected within two days of the storm.<sup id="cite_ref-UnivisionOct26_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UnivisionOct26-23">[20]</a></sup> Through the nation\'s livestock catastrophe fund, 150&#160;million pesos (US$9&#160;million) were allocated for agricultural areas affected by the storm on October&#160;29.
