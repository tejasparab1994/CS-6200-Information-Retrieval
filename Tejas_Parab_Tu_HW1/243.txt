season</a></b></td>\n</tr>\n<tr style="display:none">\n<td colspan="2"></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Hurricane Debbie</b> was an intense and long-lived hurricane that formed during August 1969. The fifth <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a>, fourth named storm, third hurricane and second major hurricane of the <a href="/wiki/1969_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1969 Atlantic hurricane season">1969 Atlantic hurricane season</a>, Debbie formed on August 14 in the southern Atlantic Ocean and took a general northwesterly path until turning northward into the central Atlantic. The storm was characterized by numerous fluctuations in intensity, and it reached winds corresponding to Category 3 status on the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" class="mw-redirect" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson Hurricane Scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson Hurricane Scale</a> on four separate occasions. The hurricane bypassed the island of Bermuda to the southeast on August 22, before ultimately brushing southeastern <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_(island)" title="Newfoundland (island)">Newfoundland</a> with strong winds. It dissipated over the cold waters east of <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a>. Although Debbie had little effect on land, it was extensively researched and was subject to a <a href="/wiki/Weather_modification" title="Weather modification">weather modification</a> experiment by <a href="/wiki/Project_Stormfury" title="Project Stormfury">Project Stormfury</a>, in which it was <a href="/wiki/Cloud_seeding" title="Cloud seeding">seeded</a> with <a href="/wiki/Silver_iodide" title="Silver iodide">silver iodide</a>.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Debbie_(1969)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Debbie_1969_track.png" class="image"><img alt="" class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Debbie_1969_track.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nMap plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>A disturbance associated with a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> strengthened into a tropical depression on August 14.<sup id="cite_ref-hurdat_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hurdat-1">[1]</a></sup> The system had significantly organized by August 15,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and it intensified into a tropical storm at 1200 UTC that day.<sup id="cite_ref-hurdat_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hurdat-1">[1]</a></sup> Upon its designation, Debbie was moving west-northwestward at approximately 15&#160;mph (24&#160;km/h) and it was predicted to gradually gain power.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It attained <a href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale#Category_1" class="mw-redirect" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Category 1</a> hurricane strength on August 16 as it turned toward the northwest. It continued to mature, and at around 1200 UTC the next day, it achieved winds corresponding to Category 2 on the <a href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" class="mw-redirect" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale</a>. On August 18, Debbie further intensified to Category 3 status, making it a major hurricane.<sup id="cite_ref-hurdat_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hurdat-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>However, the storm quickly downgraded, and by August 19 it was once again at minimal hurricane force. At roughly the same time, it turned more to the west, although it maintained a general northwesterly path.<sup id="cite_ref-hurdat_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hurdat-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The abrupt weakening may have been the result of a <a href="/wiki/Project_Stormfury" title="Project Stormfury">seeding experiment</a> carried out on the storm in an attempt to deteriorate it.<sup id="cite_ref-MWR_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-5">[5]</a></sup> By later in the day, Debbie had begun to restrengthen. It resumed Category 3 intensity on August 20, despite a minor oscillation in magnitude during the day. At this point, the cyclone acquired peak winds of 120&#160;mph (195&#160;km/h); shortly thereafter, its lowest recorded barometric pressure fell to 951 <a href="/wiki/Millibars" class="mw-redirect" title="Millibars">millibars</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-hurdat_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hurdat-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The storm turned northward on August 21,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and eventually curved northeastward. Debbie weakened to Category 2 strength but, for the fourth time, restrengthened to major hurricane intensity.<sup id="cite_ref-hurdat_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hurdat-1">[1]</a></sup> The hurricane then passed well to the southeast of Bermuda,<sup id="cite_ref-hurdat_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hurdat-1">[1]</a></sup> although it is believed that if not for the presence of nearby <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Camille" title="Hurricane Camille">Hurricane Camille</a> which emerged into the Atlantic from the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> on August 20, Debbie would have likely ended up further west, closer to the island.<sup id="cite_ref-MWR_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-5">[5]</a></sup> It maintained its severity through August 22 as it continued generally toward the northeast.<sup id="cite_ref-hurdat_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hurdat-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Eye_of_hurricane_debbie_(1969).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Eye_of_hurricane_debbie_%281969%29.jpg/330px-Eye_of_hurricane_debbie_%281969%29.jpg 1.5x, 23, the storm began a weakening trend and it turned towards the north.<sup id="cite_ref-hurdat_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hurdat-1">[1]</a></sup> The next day, the storm\xe2\x80\x94having weakened to Category 1 status\xe2\x80\x94skirted the southeastern tip of <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_(island)" title="Newfoundland (island)">Newfoundland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Debbie began to lose its tropical characteristics as it accelerated towards the northeast,<sup id="cite_ref-disc38_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-disc38-8">[8]</a></sup> and it weakened into a tropical storm early on August 25.<sup id="cite_ref-hurdat_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hurdat-1">[1]</a></sup> As it moved over increasingly cold waters, it dissipated east of <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-MWR_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-5">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact_and_Project_Stormfury">Impact and Project Stormfury</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Debbie_(1969)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Impact and Project Stormfury">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:DebbieAug2019691519zNavyRadar.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/DebbieAug2019691519zNavyRadar.jpg/220px-DebbieAug2019691519zNavyRadar.jpg" width="220" height="270" class="thumbimage" Hurricane Debbie was taken on August 20, 1969 around 1519 UTC.</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Project_Stormfury" title="Project Stormfury">Project Stormfury</a></div>\n<p>Debbie was subject to an experiment called <a href="/wiki/Project_Stormfury" title="Project Stormfury">Project Stormfury</a>, which attempted to weaken tropical cyclones by <a href="/wiki/Cloud_seeding" title="Cloud seeding">seeding</a> them with <a href="/wiki/Silver_iodide" title="Silver iodide">silver iodide</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-MWR_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-5">[5]</a></sup> The storm provided an excellent opportunity to test the underpinnings of Project Stormfury. In many ways it was the perfect storm for seeding: it did not threaten any land; it passed within range of seeding aircraft; and was intense with a distinct eye.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On August 18 and again on August 20, thirteen planes flew out to the storm to monitor and seed it. On the first day, windspeeds fell by 31%. On the second day, windspeeds fell by 18%.<sup id="cite_ref-MWR_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Both changes were consistent with Stormfury\'s working hypothesis. The results were so encouraging that "a greatly expanded research program was planned."<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Among other conclusions was the need for frequent seeding at close to hourly intervals.<sup id="cite_ref-MWR_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-5">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Debbie remained predominately at sea throughout its 3,000&#160;mi (4,800&#160;km) path, and as a result, it caused little damage.<sup id="cite_ref-MWR_5-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-5">[5]</a></sup> The storm had little or no impact on the island of Bermuda as it passed to the south.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Later, winds of 50 to 65&#160;mph (80 to 105&#160;km/h) were recorded
