Isidore was the fifth of eight named storms to occur in September.<sup id="cite_ref-HURDAT_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-1">[1]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> peaked as a <a href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" class="mw-redirect" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Category 3 hurricane</a>, causing damage as well as four fatalities in <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, and the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-LA_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LA-2">[2]</a></sup> Isidore is noted for threatening to strike the northern <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Coast" class="mw-redirect" title="Gulf Coast">Gulf Coast</a> as a Category&#160;4 hurricane, but instead striking as a moderate tropical storm due to a track change that brought the storm over the <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula">Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula</a> for over a day, which significantly weakened the <a href="/wiki/Cyclone" title="Cyclone">cyclone</a>. Its primary impact was the heavy rainfall which fell across southeast Mexico and from the central United States Gulf coast into the <a href="/wiki/Ohio_Valley" class="mw-redirect" title="Ohio Valley">Ohio Valley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DR_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DR-3">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<p></p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc">\n<div id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Isidore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Isidore_2002_track.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Isidore_2002_track.png/220px-Isidore_2002_track.png" width="220" height="191" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Isidore_2002_track.png/330px-Isidore_2002_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Isidore_2002_track.png/440px-Isidore_2002_track.png 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="890" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Isidore_2002_track.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nMap plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>On September 9, a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> moved off the coast of Africa. Despite moving through a dry environment, its convection became organized as an anticyclone developed over the wave. On September 14, convection was well-organized enough around a small closed wind circulation to classify the system as Tropical Depression Ten near <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago" title="Trinidad and Tobago">Trinidad and Tobago</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-LA_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LA-2">[2]</a></sup> Land interaction from <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a> later weakened the depression back to a tropical wave. The wave continued west-northwestward through the Caribbean, and redeveloped into a tropical depression on September 17 while 140 miles (230&#160;km) south of Jamaica.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Embedded within a weak steering current, it drifted to the northwest, and strengthened into <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_scales" title="Tropical cyclone scales">Tropical Storm</a> Isidore on September 18 near Jamaica.<sup id="cite_ref-TCR_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-5">[5]</a></sup> After grazing the island, it quickly intensified to a hurricane late on September 19 while south of Cuba. Isidore\'s <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">maximum sustained winds</a> reached 100 miles per hour (160&#160;km/h) near the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Youth" class="mw-redirect" title="Isle of Youth">Isle of Youth</a>, and weakened slightly to an 85 miles per hour (137&#160;km/h) hurricane as it made landfall on Cabo Frances late on September 20.<sup id="cite_ref-HURDAT_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-1">[1]</a></sup> The hurricane crossed the island, then slowed as it moved westward across the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a>. Favorable conditions aloft with warm water temperatures allowed Isidore to quickly intensify to a peak of 125 miles per hour (201&#160;km/h) winds on September 21.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Despite <a href="/wiki/Dvorak_technique" title="Dvorak technique">Dvorak</a> satellite estimates of 145 miles per hour (233&#160;km/h), <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hunters" class="mw-redirect" title="Hurricane Hunters">reconnaissance aircraft</a> indicated Isidore remained a 125 miles per hour (201&#160;km/h) Category&#160;3 hurricane, with pressures dropping to 934&#160;<a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">mbar</a> (27.6&#160;<a href="/wiki/Inch_of_mercury" title="Inch of mercury">inHg</a>), typical of a Category&#160;4 hurricane.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> It is the lowest minimum pressure ever observed for an Atlantic hurricane which did not exceed Category&#160;3 intensity.<sup id="cite_ref-HURDAT_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-1">[1]</a></sup> With nearly ideal conditions for development, Isidore was forecast to reach winds of 150 miles per hour (240&#160;km/h) over the northern Gulf of Mexico.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> High pressures to its north forced it southward, and Isidore made landfall at <a href="/wiki/Telchac_Puerto" title="Telchac Puerto">Telchac Puerto</a> in <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_(state)" class="mw-redirect" title="Yucat\xc3\xa1n (state)">Yucat\xc3\xa1n</a> as a major hurricane on September 22. It weakened rapidly as it nearly stalled over Yucat\xc3\xa1n, and was only a minimal tropical storm after being inland for 30&#160;hours.</p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Isidore25092002_1445.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Isidore25092002_1445.png/220px-Isidore25092002_1445.png" width="220" height="130" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Isidore25092002_1445.png/330px-Isidore25092002_1445.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Isidore25092002_1445.png/440px-Isidore25092002_1445.png 2x" data-file-width="722" data-file-height="426" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Isidore25092002_1445.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nTropical Storm Isidore (left), <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Lili" title="Hurricane Lili">Lili</a> (middle) and <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Kyle_(2002)" title="Hurricane Kyle (2002)">Kyle</a> on September 25</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>The inner core of convection collapsed while over southeast Mexico, and upon moving northward and reaching the Gulf of Mexico again, it was a large but weak tropical storm. Conditions favored significant strengthening, but Isidore did not redevelop central convection until reaching the northern Gulf of Mexico. The strengthening system hit <a href="/wiki/Grand_Isle,_Louisiana" title="Grand Isle, Louisiana">Grand Isle, Louisiana</a> on September 26 as a 65 miles per hour (105&#160;km/h) tropical storm, but weakened quickly into a tropical depression after moving inland. The system raced to the northeast, and became an <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical cyclone</a> on September 27 before becoming absorbed in a frontal zone over <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a> that night.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Isidore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Tropical_Storm_Isidore_(2002).gif" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Tropical_Storm_Isidore_%282002%29.gif/220px-Tropical_Storm_Isidore_%282002%29.gif" width="220" height="161" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Tropical_Storm_Isidore_%282002%29.gif/330px-Tropical_Storm_Isidore_%282002%29.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Tropical_Storm_Isidore_%282002%29.gif/440px-Tropical_Storm_Isidore_%282002%29.gif 2x" data-file-width="720" data-file-height="527" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Tropical_Storm_Isidore_(2002).gif" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nTropical Storm Isidore making landfall in <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a> on September 26</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Before Isidore became a hurricane, there were fears that the storm would end up being a significant threat.<sup id="cite_ref-LA_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LA-2">[2]</a></sup> The upper level environment ahead of the storm was very favorable, and the oceanic heat content was very high.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Just ten months after the destructive <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Michelle" title="Hurricane Michelle">Hurricane Michelle</a>, Hurricane Isidore threatened to cause similar effects in Cuba. In preparation for the storm, about 292,000&#160;people and thousands of farm animals were evacuated in the <a href="/wiki/Pinar_del_R%C3%ADo" title="Pinar del R\xc3\xado">Pinar del R\xc3\xado</a> province.<sup id="cite_ref-WFP_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WFP-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Cuba_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cuba-12">[12]</a></sup> Hurricane Warnings were posted about 48&#160;hours before landfall, leaving ample time to prepare for the storm.</p>\n<p>Once in the Gulf of Mexico, Hurricane Isidore became a major <a href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" class="mw-redirect" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Category 3</a> storm with winds of 125 miles per hour (201&#160;km/h).<sup id="cite_ref-HURDAT_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-1">[1]</a></sup> The forecasts then predicted Isidore to move westward in the Gulf of Mexico for a few days, before turning northward towards the Gulf Coast as what was predicted by then to be a strong <a href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" class="mw-redirect" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Category 4</a> hurricane.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Initially expected to remain north of the peninsula, hurricane warnings were issued just over a day prior to landfall, with no watch preceding Isidore\'s passage.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> More than 70,000&#160;people were evacuated,<sup id="cite_ref-MASON_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MASON-16">[16]</a></sup> and ports on the Gulf coast of Yucat\xc3\xa1n were closed. Mexican authorities declared a state of emergency prior to the arrival of the storm.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Due to Isidore\'s unexpected southward turn into the Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula, Isidore weakened significantly to a tropical storm which limited potential damage along the United States Gulf Coast. In preparation for the hurricane, the <a href="/wiki/Red_Cross" class="mw-redirect" title="Red Cross">Red Cross</a> mobilized its members across <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">Alabama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">Mississippi</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a> days before Isidore\'s expected landfall.<sup id="cite_ref-MASON_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MASON-16">[16]</a></sup> Volunteers were sent in the days prior to ensure families had a family disaster plan and a disaster supplies kit in their household. Hurricane watches raised for the United States Gulf coast between <a href="/wiki/Cameron,_Louisiana" title="Cameron, Louisiana">Cameron, Louisiana</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pascagoula,_Mississippi" title="Pascagoula, Mississippi">Pascagoula, Mississippi</a> late on the morning of September 24 were discontinued early on the morning of September 25, when the storm was no longer expected to restrengthen into a hurricane.<sup id="cite_ref-TCR_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-5">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Isidore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Caribbean_islands">Caribbean islands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Isidore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Caribbean islands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Windward_Islands" title="Windward Islands">Windward Islands</a>, Isidore was a weak tropical depression that caused no reported damage or casualties. The Cayman Islands reported tropical storm force winds, and likely experienced flooding to some degree, but Isidore caused no reported damage or casualties. Isidore brought heavy rains to Jamaica, totalling 27.2 inches (690&#160;mm) at Cotton Tree Gully.<sup id="cite_ref-TCR_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-5">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cuba">Cuba</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Isidore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Cuba">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Isidore_19_sept_2002_1845Z.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Hurricane_Isidore_19_sept_2002_1845Z.jpg/220px-Hurricane_Isidore_19_sept_2002_1845Z.jpg" width="220" height="293" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Hurricane_Isidore_19_sept_2002_1845Z.jpg/330px-Hurricane_Isidore_19_sept_2002_1845Z.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Hurricane_Isidore_19_sept_2002_1845Z.jpg/440px-Hurricane_Isidore_19_sept_2002_1845Z.jpg 2x" data-file-width="7200" data-file-height="9600" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Isidore_19_sept_2002_1845Z.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nHurricane Isidore approaching <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a> on September 19</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Hurricane Isidore brought torrential rainfall, with many areas reporting over 1 foot (300&#160;mm) of rain. The maximum reported was 21.7 inches (550&#160;mm) at Isabel Rubio in western Cuba.<sup id="cite_ref-TCR_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-5">[5]</a></sup> The rainfall, combined with a 12 feet (3.7&#160;m) storm surge, destroyed 77&#160;houses,<sup id="cite_ref-WFP_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WFP-11">[11]</a></sup> caused power outages, uprooted trees and blew off roofs.<sup id="cite_ref-RW_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RW-18">[18]</a></sup> Over 130 tobacco drying houses were affected to some degree, damaging valuable tobacco stock.<sup id="cite_ref-Cuba_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cuba-12">[12]</a></sup> A total of 24,000 <a href="/wiki/Metric_ton" class="mw-redirect" title="Metric ton">metric tons</a> of <a href="/wiki/Citrus" title="Citrus">citrus</a> and 132 metric tons of <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a> were lost from the storm. Thousands of livestock perished.<sup id="cite_ref-RW_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RW-18">[18]</a></sup> No deaths were reported.</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Southeast_Mexico">Southeast Mexico</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Isidore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Southeast Mexico">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Isidore_2002_rainfall.gif" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Isidore_2002_rainfall.gif/220px-Isidore_2002_rainfall.gif" width="220" height="234" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Isidore_2002_rainfall.gif/330px-Isidore_2002_rainfall.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Isidore_2002_rainfall.gif/440px-Isidore_2002_rainfall.gif 2x" data-file-width="861" data-file-height="915" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Isidore_2002_rainfall.gif" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nRainfall totals in Mexico and the United States</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Hurricane Isidore was one of only four storms to hit the north side of the Yucat\xc3\xa1n since records have been kept. It was the first tropical cyclone to hit the area since Tropical Depression Greta in <a href="/wiki/1970_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1970 Atlantic hurricane season">1970</a>, the first tropical storm since <a href="/wiki/1935_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1935 Atlantic hurricane season">1935</a>, the first hurricane since <a href="/wiki/1888_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1888 Atlantic hurricane season">1888</a>, and only major hurricane to ever hit the area.<sup id="cite_ref-HURDAT_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The storm shut down oil production of the Mexican state-run oil company, <a href="/wiki/PEMEX" class="mw-redirect" title="PEMEX">PEMEX</a>. Drilling operations, as well as exports from Mexico, stopped for one week which cost the oil company US$108.7&#160;million.<sup id="cite_ref-SourceMex_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SourceMex-19">[19]</a></sup> The Gulf Coast island of Isla Arena was nearly covered by Isidore\'s storm surge. Its residents evacuated prior to the storm\'s passage.<sup id="cite_ref-USA_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USA-20">[20]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Hurricane Isidore hit near <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A9rida,_Yucat%C3%A1n" title="M\xc3\xa9rida, Yucat\xc3\xa1n">M\xc3\xa9rida, Yucat\xc3\xa1n</a>, as a 125 miles per hour (201&#160;km/h) hurricane, and remained over the area for around 36&#160;hours. Over 30 inches (760&#160;mm) of rainfall fell, with the maximum recorded near Campeche.<sup id="cite_ref-DR_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DR-3">[3]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a> reached as high as 6 metres (20&#160;ft) over southern portions of Yucat\xc3\xa1n state. Nearly one-third of all fishing vessels were damaged by Isidore.<sup id="cite_ref-MWH_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MWH-21">[21]</a></sup> Nearly 36,500&#160;houses were destroyed in Yucat\xc3\xa1n from the intense winds,<sup id="cite_ref-MWH_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MWH-21">[21]</a></sup> 83,000 homes damaged, and 500,000 were left homeless.<sup id="cite_ref-SourceMex_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SourceMex-19">[19]</a></sup> Downed trees and power lines were common over much of the northern Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula,<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> and 75% of barns and warehouses were either severely damaged or destroyed. Damage to crops and livestock was extreme; 2,000&#160;square kilometres of maize and 400&#160;square kilometres of fruit trees were damaged across the country.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> A total of 70% of the poultry production and livestock were lost during the storm in Yucat\xc3\xa1n. Damage to Mexico\'s power grid reached US$19.76&#160;million. Over one-fourth of the roads across the peninsula were moderately or severely damaged, which resulted in losses of US$9.88&#160;million to rural areas.<sup id="cite_ref-SourceMex_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SourceMex-19">[19]</a></sup> Farming in Campeche experienced significant damage, with 30,000&#160;cattle and 100,000&#160;acres (400&#160;km\xc2\xb2) of agricultural land lost.</p>\n<p>Throughout the Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula, there were 17 deaths were attributed to Hurricane Isidore, two of which were indirectly related to the storm. Damage in the country totaled $950&#160;million (2002&#160;USD).<sup id="cite_ref-USA_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USA-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Central_America">Central America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Isidore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Central America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Isidore caused heavy rains in <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a> due to upslope flow from the Pacific Ocean, which led to landslides and flooding across southern and western portions of the country. The rains led to the drowning of two people when they tried to cross a rain-swollen river. Three hundred and fifty households reported losses.<sup id="cite_ref-RW_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RW-18">[18]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-USA_20-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USA-20">[20]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Eastern_United_States">Eastern United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Isidore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Eastern United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In the open Gulf of Mexico, Hurricane Isidore and later <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Lili" title="Hurricane Lili">Hurricane Lili</a> contributed to the shutdown of offshore oil and gas platforms. The effects of back-to-back storms resulted in the loss of production of 14.4 million barrels (2,290,000&#160;m<sup>3</sup>) of oil and 88.9&#160;billion cubic feet (2.52<span style="margin:0 .15em 0 .25em">\xc3\x97</span>10<sup>9</sup>&#160;m<sup>3</sup>) of natural gas. A storm surge of 8.3 feet (2.5&#160;m) was measured at Rigoletes, Louisiana and at <a href="/wiki/Gulfport,_Mississippi" title="Gulfport, Mississippi">Gulfport Harbor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">Mississippi</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-TCR_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-5">[5]</a></sup> Hurricane Isidore brought widespread heavy rainfall from the central Gulf coast into the Ohio Valley, with a maximum of 15.97 inches (406&#160;mm) at <a href="/wiki/Metairie,_Louisiana" title="Metairie, Louisiana">Metairie, Louisiana</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DR_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DR-3">[3]</a></sup> The flooding was responsible for moderate crop damage, with a total of $330&#160;million in damage (2002&#160;USD, 360&#160;million 2006&#160;USD).<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Isidore claimed five lives in the U.S.: four direct and one indirect. The indirect death was from a man that went into <a href="/wiki/Cardiac_arrest" title="Cardiac arrest">cardiac arrest</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">Mississippi</a>, whereas the other four were drowning deaths.<sup id="cite_ref-TCR_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-5">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement">Retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Isidore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">List of retired Atlantic hurricane names</a></div>\n<p>The name Isidore was retired in the spring of 2003 and will never be used for an <a href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_tropical_cyclone" class="mw-redirect" title="North Atlantic tropical cyclone">Atlantic hurricane</a> again.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> It was replaced with <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Ike" title="Hurricane Ike">Ike</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2008_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2008 Atlantic hurricane season">2008 season</a>, which was itself retired after the latter season.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
