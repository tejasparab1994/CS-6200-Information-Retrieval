href="http://www.jma.go.jp/jma/indexe.html">www.jma.go.jp</a></span></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p>The <b>Japan Meteorological Agency</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span lang="ja">\xe6\xb0\x97\xe8\xb1\xa1\xe5\xba\x81</span><span style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kish\xc5\x8d-ch\xc5\x8d</i>)</span>, <b>JMA</b>, is an <a href="/wiki/Government_agency" title="Government agency">agency</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Land,_Infrastructure,_Transport_and_Tourism_(Japan)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ministry of Land, Infrastructure, Transport and Tourism (Japan)">Ministry of Land, Infrastructure, Transport and Tourism</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-H11HO100_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-H11HO100-4">[4]</a></sup> It is charged with gathering and providing results for the public in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, that are obtained from data based on daily scientific observation and research into <a href="/wiki/Natural_phenomenon" class="mw-redirect" title="Natural phenomenon">natural phenomena</a> in the fields of <a href="/wiki/Meteorology" title="Meteorology">meteorology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hydrology" title="Hydrology">hydrology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seismology" title="Seismology">seismology</a> and <a href="/wiki/Volcanology" title="Volcanology">volcanology</a>, among other related scientific fields. Its headquarters is located in <a href="/wiki/Chiyoda,_Tokyo" title="Chiyoda, Tokyo">Chiyoda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>.</p>\n<p>JMA is responsible for gathering and reporting weather data and <a href="/wiki/Weather_forecasting" title="Weather forecasting">forecasts</a> for the general public, as well as providing <a href="/wiki/METAR" title="METAR">aviation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marine_weather_forecasting" title="Marine weather forecasting">marine weather</a>. JMA other responsibilities include issuing warnings for <a href="/wiki/Volcanic_eruption" class="mw-redirect" title="Volcanic eruption">volcanic eruptions</a>, and the nationwide issuance of earthquake warnings of the <a href="/wiki/Earthquake_Early_Warning_(Japan)" title="Earthquake Early Warning (Japan)">Earthquake Early Warning</a> (EEW) system. JMA is also designated one of the <a href="/wiki/Regional_Specialized_Meteorological_Center" title="Regional Specialized Meteorological Center">Regional Specialized Meteorological Centers</a> of the <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">World Meteorological Organization</a> (WMO). It is responsible for <a href="/wiki/Weather_forecasting" title="Weather forecasting">forecasting</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">naming</a>, and distributing warnings for <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclones</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Northwestern Pacific</a> region, including the <a href="/wiki/Celebes_Sea" title="Celebes Sea">Celebes Sea</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Sulu_Sea" title="Sulu Sea">Sulu Sea</a>, the <a href="/wiki/South_China_Sea" title="South China Sea">South China Sea</a>, the <a href="/wiki/East_China_Sea" title="East China Sea">East China Sea</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Yellow_Sea" title="Yellow Sea">Yellow Sea</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Japan" title="Sea of Japan">Sea of Japan</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Okhotsk" title="Sea of Okhotsk">Sea of Okhotsk</a>.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc">\n<div Marine Observatories, five auxiliary facilities, four Aviation Weather Service Centers and 47 local offices composed of the <a href="/wiki/Local_Meteorological_Observatory" title="Local Meteorological Observatory">LMOs</a>. These are also used to gather data, supplemented by <a href="/wiki/Weather_satellite" title="Weather satellite">weather satellites</a> such as <i><a href="/wiki/Himawari_(satellite)" title="Himawari href="/wiki/Regional_Specialized_Meteorological_Centre" class="mw-redirect" title="Regional Specialized Meteorological Centre">Regional Specialized Meteorological Centre</a> (RSMC) for Asia.<sup id="cite_ref-intl-coop_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-intl-coop-8">[8]</a></sup> In June&#160;1988, the WMO also assigned the JMA as a RSMC for the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Northwestern Pacific</a> under its Tropical Cyclone programme.<sup id="cite_ref-intl-coop_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-intl-coop-8">[8]</a></sup> In July&#160;1989, the RSMC Tokyo \xe2\x80\x93 Typhoon Center was established within the headquarters office, which dealt with the forecasting and dissemination of active <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical class="mw-headline" id="Land_weather">Land weather</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Japan_Meteorological_Agency&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Land weather">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>\n<p>Each <a href="/wiki/District_Meteorological_Observatory" title="District Meteorological Observatory">DMO</a> and <a href="/wiki/Local_Meteorological_Observatory" title="Local Meteorological Observatory">LMO</a> issues weather forecasts and warnings or advisories to the general public live in its own area. Weather data used to these forecasts are acquired from the Surface Observation (represented by the <a href="/wiki/Automated_Meteorological_Data_Acquisition_System" title="Automated Meteorological Data Acquisition System">AMeDAS</a>), the <a href="/wiki/Weather_radar" title="Weather radar">Radar Observation</a>, the Upper-air Observation and the <a href="/wiki/Weather_satellite" title="Weather satellite">Satellite Observation</a> mainly using the <i><a href="/wiki/Himawari_(satellite)" title="Himawari (satellite)">Himawari</a></i> series.</p>\n<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Marine_weather">Marine weather</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Japan_Meteorological_Agency&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Marine weather">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Marine_weather_forecasting" title="Marine weather forecasting">Marine weather forecasting</a></div>\n<p>The Marine Observatories are seated in <a href="/wiki/Hakodate,_Hokkaid%C5%8D" class="mw-redirect" title="Hakodate, Hokkaid\xc5\x8d">Hakodate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maizuru,_Kyoto" class="mw-redirect" title="Maizuru, Kyoto">Maizuru</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kobe" title="Kobe">Kobe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nagasaki" title="Nagasaki">Nagasaki</a>. These stations observe <a href="/wiki/Wind_wave" title="Wind wave">ocean waves</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tide" title="Tide">tide levels</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperature" title="Sea surface temperature">sea surface temperature</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ocean_current" title="Ocean current">ocean current</a> etc. in the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Northwestern Pacific</a> basin, as well as the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Japan" title="Sea of Japan">Sea of Japan</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Okhotsk" title="Sea of Okhotsk">Sea of Okhotsk</a> basin, and provide <a href="/wiki/Marine_weather_forecasting" title="Marine weather forecasting">marine meteorological forecasts</a> resulted from them, in cooperation with the Hydrographic and Oceanographic Department, <a href="/wiki/Japan_Coast_Guard" title="Japan Coast Guard">Japan Coast Guard</a>.</p>\n<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Aviation_weather">Aviation weather</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span Tourism</a> set up the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Air_Traffic_Management_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Air Traffic Management Center (page does not exist)">Air Traffic Additionally, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Aviation_Weather_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Aviation Weather Station (page does not exist)">Aviation Weather Stations</a> (beside the airports of <a href="/wiki/New_Chitose_Airport" title="New Chitose Airport">New Chitose</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sendai_Airport" title="Sendai Airport">Sendai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Osaka_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Osaka International Airport">Osaka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fukuoka_Airport" title="Fukuoka Airport">Fukuoka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kagoshima_Airport" title="Kagoshima Airport">Kagoshima</a> and <a href="/wiki/Naha_Airport" title="Naha Airport">Naha</a>) deal with the similar tasks as these.</p>\n<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Tropical_cyclones">Tropical cyclones</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Japan_Meteorological_Agency&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Tropical cyclones">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Typhoon" title="Typhoon">Typhoon</a> and <a href="/wiki/2017_Pacific_typhoon_season" title="2017 Pacific typhoon season">2017 Pacific typhoon season</a></div>\n<p>In the Northwestern Pacific area, the typhoon season ordinarily comes almost from May to November. The JMA forecasts and warns or advises on tropical cyclones to the public in Japan and its surrounding countries as well because it also works as the <a href="/wiki/Regional_Specialized_Meteorological_Centre" class="mw-redirect" title="Regional Specialized Meteorological Centre">RSMC</a> Tokyo \xe2\x80\x93 Typhoon Center.<sup id="cite_ref-rsmc-tc_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rsmc-tc-10">[10]</a></sup></p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Earthquakes">Earthquakes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Japan_Meteorological_Agency&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Earthquakes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Japan_Meteorological_Agency_seismic_intensity_scale" title="Japan Meteorological Agency seismic intensity scale">Japan Meteorological Agency seismic intensity scale</a> and <a href="/wiki/Earthquake_Early_Warning_(Japan)" title="Earthquake Early Warning (Japan)">Earthquake Early Warning (Japan)</a></div>\n<p>The JMA has its own 624 observation stations across the country<sup id="cite_ref-observ-seismo_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-observ-seismo-11">[11]</a></sup> that set up at intervals of 20&#160;km approximately<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> in order to measure <a href="/wiki/Japan_Meteorological_Agency_seismic_intensity_scale" title="Japan Meteorological Agency seismic intensity scale">seismic intensity</a> of earthquakes precisely. The agency also utilize about 2,900 more seismographs<sup id="cite_ref-observ-seismo_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-observ-seismo-11">[11]</a></sup> owned by the <a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Research_Institute_for_Earth_Science_and_Disaster_Prevention&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="National Research Institute for Earth Science and Disaster Prevention (page does not exist)">National Research Institute for Earth Science and Disaster Prevention</a> (NIED) and <a href="/wiki/Prefectures_of_Japan" title="Prefectures of Japan">local governments</a>. A 24-hour office has been housed within the JMA headquarters in Tokyo, for monitoring and tracking seismic events in the vicinity of Japan to collect and process their data, which issues observed earthquake\'s information on its <a href="/wiki/Hypocenter" title="Hypocenter">hypocenter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Richter_magnitude_scale" title="Richter magnitude scale">magnitude</a>, seismic intensity and possibility of <a href="/wiki/Tsunami" title="Tsunami">tsunami</a> occurrence after quakes quickly to the public through the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Earthquake_Phenomena_Observation_System&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Earthquake Phenomena Observation System (page does not exist)">Earthquake Phenomena Observation System</a> (EPOS).<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Earthquake_Early_Warning_(Japan)" title="Earthquake Early Warning (Japan)">Earthquake Early Warning</a> (EEW) system began to work fully for the general public on October 1, 2007.</p>\n<p>The agency is one of the representatives of the national <a href="/wiki/Coordinating_Committee_for_Earthquake_Prediction" title="Coordinating Committee for Earthquake Prediction">Coordinating Committee for Earthquake Prediction</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ccep-orgs_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ccep-orgs-14">[14]</a></sup></p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Tsunamis">Tsunamis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Tsunami_warning_system" title="Tsunami warning system">Tsunami In case of there is a possibility of <a href="/wiki/Tsunami" title="Tsunami">tsunami</a> after an earthquake, JMA issues Tsunami Warning or Advisory for each region in Japan with information of estimated tsunami heights and arrival times within 2 to 3 minutes of the quake.</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Volcanos">Volcanos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Japan_Meteorological_Agency&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Volcanos">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Volcanic_Ash_Advisory_Center" title="Volcanic Ash Advisory Center">Volcanic Ash Advisory Center</a></div>\n<p>The agency set up four <a href="/w/index.php?title=Volcanic_Observations_and_Information_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Volcanic Observations and Information Center (page does not exist)">Volcanic Observations and Information Centers</a> within DMOs in <a href="/wiki/Sapporo" title="Sapporo">Sapporo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sendai" title="Sendai">Sendai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fukuoka" title="Fukuoka">Fukuoka</a>. They are monitoring volcanic events on 110 active volcanos in Japan and 47 of these volcanos selected by the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Coordinating_Committee_for_Prediction_of_Volcanic_Eruption&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Coordinating Committee for Prediction of Volcanic Eruption (page does not exist)">Coordinating Committee for Prediction of Volcanic Eruption</a> are under the 24-hour observation with <a href="/wiki/Seismograph" class="mw-redirect" title="Seismograph">seismographs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Accelerometer" title="Accelerometer">accelerometers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Global_Positioning_System" title="Global Positioning System">GPS</a>, air-shock recorders, fixed point observation cameras and other equipment.
