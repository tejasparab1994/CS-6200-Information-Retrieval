id="cite_ref-BoxOfficeMojo_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BoxOfficeMojo-1">[1]</a></sup></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><i><b>The Day After Tomorrow</b></i> is a 2004 American science-fiction disaster film co-written, directed, and produced by <a href="/wiki/Roland_Emmerich" title="Roland Emmerich">Roland Emmerich</a> and starring <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Quaid" title="Dennis Quaid">Dennis Quaid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jake_Gyllenhaal" title="Jake Gyllenhaal">Jake Gyllenhaal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ian_Holm" title="Ian Holm">Ian Holm</a>, <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Rossum" title="Emmy Rossum">Emmy Rossum</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sela_Ward" title="Sela Ward">Sela Ward</a>. The film depicts catastrophic climatic effects following the <a href="/wiki/Shutdown_of_thermohaline_circulation" title="Shutdown of thermohaline circulation">disruption of the North Atlantic Ocean circulation</a> in a series of <a href="/wiki/Extreme_weather" title="Extreme weather">extreme weather</a> events that usher in <a href="/wiki/Global_cooling" title="Global cooling">global cooling</a> and lead to a new <a href="/wiki/Ice_age" title="Ice age">ice age</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Filmed in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>, it is the highest-grossing Hollywood film made in Canada (adjusted for <a href="/wiki/Inflation" title="Inflation">inflation</a>).</p>\n<p>Originally slated for release in the summer of 2003, <i>The Day After Tomorrow</i> opened in <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">Mexico City</a> on May 17, 2004, and was released worldwide from May 26 to May 28 (except in South Korea and Japan, where it was released on June 4 and 5). A major commercial success, the film became the <a href="/wiki/2004_in_film#Highest-grossing_films" title="2004 in film">sixth highest-grossing film of 2004</a>. It received mixed reviews upon release, with critics highly praising the film\'s special effects but criticizing numerous scientific inaccuracies.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc">\n<div id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Day_After_Tomorrow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Jack Hall, an American <a href="/wiki/Paleoclimatology" title="Paleoclimatology">paleoclimatologist</a>, and his colleagues Frank and Jason, drill for ice-core samples in the <a href="/wiki/Larsen_Ice_Shelf" title="Larsen Ice Shelf">Larsen Ice Shelf</a> for the <a href="/wiki/NOAA" class="mw-redirect" title="NOAA">NOAA</a>, when the shelf breaks apart. Jack warns of <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">global warming</a> to a <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">UN</a> conference in <a href="/wiki/New_Delhi" title="New Delhi">New Delhi</a>, but <a href="/wiki/US_Vice_President" class="mw-redirect" title="US Vice President">US Vice President</a> Raymond Becker dismisses his concerns. Professor Terry Rapson of the Hedland Centre in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> shares Jack\xe2\x80\x99s views of an inevitable climate shift and they become friends. Several <a href="/wiki/Buoys" class="mw-redirect" title="Buoys">buoys</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> show a severe ocean temperature drop, Rapson concluding Jack\xe2\x80\x99s theories are correct. Jack and Rapson\xe2\x80\x99s teams, along with <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a> <a href="/wiki/Meteorologist" class="mw-redirect" title="Meteorologist">meteorologist</a> Janet Tokada, build a forecast model based on Jack\xe2\x80\x99s research.</p>\n<p>Eventually, a <a href="/wiki/Storm" title="Storm">storm</a> system develops in the <a href="/wiki/Northern_hemisphere" class="mw-redirect" title="Northern hemisphere">northern hemisphere</a>, splitting into three superstorms above <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, Scotland, and <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a>. The subzero temperatures of the superstorms\' <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eyes</a> cause <a href="/wiki/Flash_freezing" title="Flash freezing">flash freezing</a>. Meanwhile, the weather becomes increasingly bad around the world, <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a> is struck by a giant hail storm and <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hollywood" title="Hollywood">Hollywood</a> are devastated by a tornado outbreak.</p>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>, Jack\xe2\x80\x99s son Sam, and his friends Brian, J.D., and Laura Chapman participate in an <a href="/wiki/Academic_decathlon" class="mw-redirect" title="Academic decathlon">academic decathlon</a>. The city begins to <a href="/wiki/Flood" title="Flood">flood</a> with rainfall, followed by an enormous <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a>, trapping Sam\xe2\x80\x99s group in the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Public_Library" title="New York Public Library">New York Public Library</a>. Sam contacts Jack and his mother Lucy, a <a href="/wiki/Physician" title="Physician">doctor</a>, Jack advising him to stay inside, and promises to rescue him. Rapson and his team perish in the European storm, while Lucy remains in a hospital caring for bed-ridden children, until saved by the authorities.</p>\n<p>Upon Jack\xe2\x80\x99s suggestion, <a href="/wiki/US_President" class="mw-redirect" title="US President">US President</a> Blake orders the southern states to be evacuated into <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, the northern half doomed to be hit by the superstorm. Blake is caught in it and dies, making Becker the <a href="/wiki/United_States_presidential_line_of_succession" title="United States presidential line of succession">new President</a>. Jack, Jason, and Frank make their way Manhattan, but Frank falls through the <a href="/wiki/Skylight" title="Skylight">skylight</a> of a <a href="/wiki/Shopping_mall" title="Shopping mall">mall</a>, cutting his rope to prevent his friends from falling in after him. In the library, most survivors decide to head south against Sam\xe2\x80\x99s warnings, and later freeze to death. Before and during the film\'s release, members of environmental and political advocacy groups distributed pamphlets to moviegoers describing the possible effects of global warming. Although the film depicts effects of global warming predicted by scientists (such as rising sea levels, more destructive storms, and disruption of ocean currents and weather patterns), it depicts their occurrence more rapidly and severely than what is considered scientifically plausible; the theory that a superstorm could create rapid worldwide <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a> does not appear in the scientific literature.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. The movie features 416 visual effects shots, with nine effects houses and over 1,000 artists working on the film for over a year to bring its disaster sequences to life.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Day_After_Tomorrow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Day_After_Tomorrow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The film ranked #2 at the box office (behind <i><a href="/wiki/Shrek_2" title="Shrek 2">Shrek 2</a></i>) over its four-day <a href="/wiki/Memorial_Day" title="Memorial Day">Memorial Day</a> opening, grossing $85,807,341. led the per-theater average, with a four-day average of $25,053 (compared to <i>Shrek 2</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">\'</span>s four-day average of $22,633). Schrag">Daniel P. Schrag</a> said, "On the one hand, I\'m glad that there\'s a big-budget movie about something as critical as climate change. On the other, I\'m concerned that people will see these over-the-top effects and think the whole thing is a joke ... We are indeed experimenting with the Earth in a way that hasn\'t been done for millions of years. But you\'re not going to see another <a href="/wiki/Ice_age" title="Ice age">ice age</a> \xe2\x80\x93 at least not like that."<sup id="cite_ref-USAToday_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USAToday-8">[8]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/J._Marshall_Shepherd" title="J. Michaels">Patrick J. But the film presents an opportunity to explain that some of the basic background is right: humans are indeed increasingly changing the climate and this is quite a dangerous experiment, including some risk of abrupt and unforeseen changes ... Luckily it is extremely unlikely that we will see major ocean circulation changes in the next couple of decades (I\xe2\x80\x99d be just as surprised as Jack Hall if they did occur); at least most scientists think this will only become a more serious risk towards the end of the century. And the consequences would certainly not be as dramatic as the \'superstorm\' depicted in the movie. Nevertheless, a major change in ocean circulation is a risk with serious and partly unpredictable consequences, which we should avoid. href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></dd>\n</dl>\n<p>Environmental activist and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">Guardian</a></i> columnist <a href="/wiki/George_Monbiot"
