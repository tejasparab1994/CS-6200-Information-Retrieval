Unsourced material may be challenged and removed.</span> <small><i>(July 2009)</i></small> <small class="hide-when-compact"><i>(<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div>\n</td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Slush</b>, also called <b>slush ice</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Slurry" title="Slurry">slurry</a> mixture of small ice crystals (e.g., snow) and <a href="/wiki/Liquid" title="Liquid">liquid</a> <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">water</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In the natural environment, slush forms when <a href="/wiki/Ice" title="Ice">ice</a> or snow melts. This often mixes with dirt and other materials, resulting in a gray or muddy brown color. Often, solid ice or snow will block the drainage of fluid water from slushy areas, so slush often goes through multiple freeze/thaw cycles before completely disappearing. In areas where <a href="/wiki/Road_salt" class="mw-redirect" title="Road salt">road salt</a> is used to clear <a href="/wiki/Road" title="Road">roadways</a>, slush forms at lower temperatures than it would ordinarily, and only in salted areas; this can produce a number of different consistencies over the same geographical area.</p>\n<p>Slushfall or slushing is the action of a wet snow falling from the sky.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hazards">Hazards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Slush&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Hazards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:FrozenTireMarks@Sunrise(byPeterBesser).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/FrozenTireMarks%40Sunrise%28byPeterBesser%29.jpg/220px-FrozenTireMarks%40Sunrise%28byPeterBesser%29.jpg" width="220" height="141" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/FrozenTireMarks%40Sunrise%28byPeterBesser%29.jpg/330px-FrozenTireMarks%40Sunrise%28byPeterBesser%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/FrozenTireMarks%40Sunrise%28byPeterBesser%29.jpg/440px-FrozenTireMarks%40Sunrise%28byPeterBesser%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1558" data-file-height="1002" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:FrozenTireMarks@Sunrise(byPeterBesser).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nRoadside puddle slush refrozen during the night.</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Slush can be a problem on an <a href="/wiki/Aircraft" title="Aircraft">aircraft</a> <a href="/wiki/Runway" title="Runway">runway</a> since the effect of excess slush acting on the aircraft\'s wheels can have a resisting effect during take off, which can cause an accident such as the <a href="/wiki/Munich_air_disaster" title="Munich air disaster">Munich air disaster</a>. Slush on <a href="/wiki/Road" title="Road">roads</a> can also increase the <a href="/wiki/Braking_distance" title="Braking distance">braking distances</a> for <a href="/wiki/Automobile" class="mw-redirect" title="Automobile">cars</a> and <a href="/wiki/Truck" title="Truck">trucks</a>, increasing the possibility of rear end <a href="/wiki/Car_accident" class="mw-redirect" title="Car accident">crashes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Collision#Traffic" title="Collision">other accidents</a>.</p>\n<p>Slush refreezing in overnight <a href="/wiki/Frost" title="Frost">frost</a> can turn to dangerous slippery ice underfoot.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
