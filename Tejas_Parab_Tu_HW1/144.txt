colspan="2"></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Typhoon Nabi</b> (<small>pronounced&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Korean" title="Help:IPA/Korean">[na.bi]</a></span>), known in the Philippines as <b>Typhoon Jolina</b>, was a powerful <a href="/wiki/Typhoon" title="Typhoon">typhoon</a> that struck southwestern <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> in September 2005. The 14th <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">named storm</a> of the <a href="/wiki/2005_Pacific_typhoon_season" title="2005 Pacific typhoon season">2005 Pacific typhoon season</a>, Nabi formed on August&#160;29 to the east of the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Mariana_Islands" title="Northern Mariana Islands">Northern Mariana Islands</a>. It moved westward and passed about 55&#160;km (35&#160;mi) north of <a href="/wiki/Saipan" title="Saipan">Saipan</a> on August&#160;31 as an intensifying typhoon. On the next day, the <a href="/wiki/Joint_Typhoon_Warning_Center" title="Joint Typhoon Warning Center">Joint Typhoon Warning Center</a> upgraded the storm to <a href="/wiki/Typhoon#Intensity_classifications" title="Typhoon">super typhoon</a> status, with winds equivalent to that of a Category&#160;5 hurricane on the <a href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" class="mw-redirect" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Japan_Meteorological_Agency" title="Japan Meteorological Agency">Japan Meteorological Agency</a> estimated peak ten-minute winds of 175&#160;km/h (110&#160;mph) on September&#160;2. Nabi weakened while curving to the north, striking the Japanese island of <a href="/wiki/Kyushu" title="Kyushu">Kyushu</a> on September&#160;6. After brushing <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>, the storm turned to the northeast, passing over <a href="/wiki/Hokkaido" title="Hokkaido">Hokkaido</a> before becoming <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical</a> on September&#160;8.</p>\n<p>The typhoon first affected the Northern Marianas Islands, where it left <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>2.5&#160;million in damage,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[nb 1]</a></sup> while damaging or destroying 114&#160;homes. The damage was enough to warrant a disaster declaration from the United States government. While passing near <a href="/wiki/Okinawa" class="mw-redirect" title="Okinawa">Okinawa</a>, Nabi produced gusty winds and caused minor damage. Later, the western fringe of the storm caused several traffic accidents in <a href="/wiki/Busan" title="Busan">Busan</a>, South Korea, and throughout the country Nabi killed six people and caused US$115.4&#160;million in damage. About 250,000&#160;people evacuated along the Japanese island of Kyushu ahead of the storm, and there were disruptions to train, ferry, and airline services. In Kyushu, the storm left <a href="/wiki/Japanese_yen" title="Japanese yen">\xc2\xa5</a>4.08&#160;billion<sup id="cite_ref-jpy_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jpy-3">[nb 2]</a></sup> (US$36.9&#160;million) in crop damage after dropping 1,322&#160;mm (52.0&#160;in) of rain over three days. During the storm\'s passage, there were 61&#160;daily rainfall records broken by Nabi\'s precipitation. The rains caused flooding and landslides, forcing people to evacuate their homes and for businesses to close. Across Japan, Nabi killed 29&#160;people and caused \xc2\xa594.9&#160;billion (US$854&#160;million) in damage. Soldiers, local governments, and insurance companies helped residents recover from the storm damage. After affecting Japan, the typhoon affected the <a href="/wiki/Kuril_Islands" title="Kuril Islands">Kuril Islands</a> of Russia, where it dropped the equivalent of the monthly precipitation, while also causing road damage due to high waves. Overall, Nabi killed 35&#160;people, and its effects were significant enough for the name to be <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Pacific_typhoon_names" title="List of retired Pacific typhoon names">retired</a>.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Meteorological_history"><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Nabi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nabi_2005_track.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Nabi_2005_track.png/220px-Nabi_2005_track.png" width="220" height="196" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Nabi_2005_track.png/330px-Nabi_2005_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Nabi_2005_track.png/440px-Nabi_2005_track.png 2x" data-file-width="2000" data-file-height="1786" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nabi_2005_track.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nMap plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>On August&#160;28, a large area of <a href="/wiki/Convection_(meteorology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Convection (meteorology)">convection</a> persisted about 1,035&#160;km (645&#160;mi) east of <a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">Guam</a>. Located within an area of moderate <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a>, the system quickly organized while moving westward, its track influenced by a <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">ridge</a> to the north.<sup id="cite_ref-gp_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-4">[2]</a></sup> At 00:00&#160;<a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a> on August&#160;29, a tropical depression formed from the system,<sup id="cite_ref-bt_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bt-5">[3]</a></sup> classified by the <a href="/wiki/Joint_Typhoon_Warning_Center" title="Joint Typhoon Warning Center">Joint Typhoon Warning Center</a> (JTWC)<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[nb 3]</a></sup> as Tropical Depression 14W. In initial forecasts, the agency anticipated steady strengthening,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[5]</a></sup> due to warm <a href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperature" title="Sea surface temperature">sea surface temperatures</a> in the area.<sup id="cite_ref-aug29_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aug29-9">[6]</a></sup> At 12:00&#160;UTC on August&#160;29, the <a href="/wiki/Japan_Meteorological_Agency" title="Japan Meteorological Agency">Japan Meteorological Agency</a> (JMA)<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[nb 4]</a></sup> upgraded it to a tropical storm.<sup id="cite_ref-jma_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jma-10">[7]</a></sup> As such, the JMA <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">named</a> the storm <i>Nabi</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[nb 5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gp_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-4">[2]</a></sup> About 12&#160;hours later, the JMA upgraded Nabi further to a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_scales#Western_Pacific" title="Tropical cyclone scales">severe tropical storm</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-bt_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bt-5">[3]</a></sup> after the convection organized into spiral <a href="/wiki/Rainband" title="Rainband">rainbands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-aug29_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aug29-9">[6]</a></sup> At 18:00&#160;UTC, Nabi intensified to typhoon status, reaching ten-minute <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">sustained winds</a> of 120&#160;km/h (75&#160;mph).<sup id="cite_ref-bt_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bt-5">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<p>On August&#160;31, Nabi passed about 55&#160;km (35&#160;mi) north-northeast of <a href="/wiki/Saipan" title="Saipan">Saipan</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Mariana_Islands" title="Northern Mariana Islands">Northern Mariana Islands</a> during its closest approach.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[8]</a></sup> The typhoon continued to intensify quickly as it moved to the west-northwest. On September&#160;1, the JTWC upgraded the storm to a <a href="/wiki/Typhoon#Intensity_classifications" title="Typhoon">super typhoon</a> and later estimated peak one-minute winds of 260&#160;km/h (160&#160;mph); this is the equivalent of a Category&#160;5 on the <a href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" class="mw-redirect" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Saffir-Simpson scale</a>. By contrast, the JMA estimated peak ten-minute winds of 175&#160;km/h (110&#160;mph) on September&#160;2, with a <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" title="Atmospheric pressure">barometric pressure</a> of 925&#160;mbar (27.3&#160;inHg).<sup id="cite_ref-gp_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-4">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bt_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bt-5">[3]</a></sup> While at peak intensity, the typhoon developed a large 95&#160;km (60&#160;mi) wide <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[9]</a></sup> For about 36&#160;hours, Nabi maintained its peak winds, during which it crossed into the area of responsibility of <a href="/wiki/PAGASA" title="PAGASA">PAGASA</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-gp_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-4">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bt_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bt-5">[3]</a></sup> the Philippine-based agency gave it the local name "Jolina", although the storm remained away from the country.<sup id="cite_ref-gp_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-4">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:272px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Typhooon_Nabi_as_seen_from_the_ISS.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Typhooon_Nabi_as_seen_from_the_ISS.jpg/270px-Typhooon_Nabi_as_seen_from_the_ISS.jpg" width="270" height="163" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Typhooon_Nabi_as_seen_from_the_ISS.jpg/405px-Typhooon_Nabi_as_seen_from_the_ISS.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Typhooon_Nabi_as_seen_from_the_ISS.jpg/540px-Typhooon_Nabi_as_seen_from_the_ISS.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2864" data-file-height="1728" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Typhooon_Nabi_as_seen_from_the_ISS.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nTyphoon Nabi on September 3, 2005, as seen from the International Space Station</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>On September&#160;3, Nabi began weakening as it turned more to the north, the result of an approaching <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a> weakening the ridge.<sup id="cite_ref-gp_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-4">[2]</a></sup> Later that day, the winds leveled off at 155&#160;km/h (100&#160;mph), according to the JMA. On September&#160;5, Nabi passed near <a href="/wiki/Kitadait%C5%8Djima" title="Kitadait\xc5\x8djima">Kitadait\xc5\x8djima</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yakushima" title="Yakushima">Yakushima</a>, part of the <a href="/wiki/Dait%C5%8D_Islands" title="Dait\xc5\x8d Islands">Dait\xc5\x8d</a> and <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Csumi_Islands" title="\xc5\x8csumi Islands">\xc5\x8csumi</a> island groups offshore southern Japan.<sup id="cite_ref-jma_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jma-10">[7]</a></sup> Around that time, the JTWC estimated that the typhoon reintensified slightly to a secondary peak of 215&#160;km/h (130&#160;mph).<sup id="cite_ref-bt_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bt-5">[3]</a></sup> After turning due north, Nabi made <a href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> near <a href="/wiki/Isahaya,_Nagasaki" title="Isahaya, Nagasaki">Isahaya, Nagasaki</a> around 05:00&#160;UTC on September&#160;6, after passing through the <a href="/wiki/Amakusa" title="Amakusa">Amakusa</a> of Kumamoto.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[10]</a></sup> Shortly thereafter, the storm entered the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Japan" title="Sea of Japan">Sea of Japan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-jma_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jma-10">[7]</a></sup> The typhoon turned to the northeast into the <a href="/wiki/Westerlies" title="Westerlies">mid-latitude flow</a>, influenced by a low over the <a href="/wiki/Kamchatka_Peninsula" title="Kamchatka Peninsula">Kamchatka Peninsula</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[11]</a></sup> At 18:00&#160;UTC on September&#160;6, the JTWC discontinued advisories on Nabi, declaring it <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[12]</a></sup> although the JMA continued tracking the storm. On the next day, Nabi moved across northern <a href="/wiki/Hokkaido" title="Hokkaido">Hokkaido</a> into the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Okhotsk" title="Sea of Okhotsk">Sea of Okhotsk</a>. The JMA declared the storm as extratropical on September&#160;8, which continued eastward until dissipating on September&#160;10 south of the <a href="/wiki/Aleutian_Islands" title="Aleutian Islands">Aleutian Islands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-jma_10-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jma-10">[7]</a></sup> The remnants of Nabi weakened and later moved into southwestern <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a> on September&#160;12.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[13]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Nabi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Typhoon_Nabi_06_sep_2005_0205Z.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Typhoon_Nabi_06_sep_2005_0205Z.jpg/220px-Typhoon_Nabi_06_sep_2005_0205Z.jpg" width="220" height="220" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Typhoon_Nabi_06_sep_2005_0205Z.jpg/330px-Typhoon_Nabi_06_sep_2005_0205Z.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Typhoon_Nabi_06_sep_2005_0205Z.jpg/440px-Typhoon_Nabi_06_sep_2005_0205Z.jpg 2x" data-file-width="7500" data-file-height="7500" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Typhoon_Nabi_06_sep_2005_0205Z.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nTyphoon Nabi making landfall in <a href="/wiki/Kyushu" title="Kyushu">Kyushu</a> on September 6</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>After Nabi formed as a tropical depression on August&#160;29, the local <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">National Weather Service</a> office on Guam issued a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">tropical storm watch</a> for the islands <a href="/wiki/Tinian" title="Tinian">Tinian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rota_(island)" title="Rota (island)">Rota</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sapian" title="Sapian">Sapian</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Agrihan" title="Agrihan">Agrihan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[14]</a></sup> On August&#160;30, the watch was upgraded to a tropical storm warning for Rota and Agrihan, while a typhoon warning was issued for Tinian and Saipan.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[15]</a></sup> On all four islands, a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches#Tropical_Cyclone_Condition_of_Readiness" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">Condition of Readiness 1</a> was declared. The government of the Northern Mariana Islands advised Tinian and Saipan residents along the coast and in poorly-built buildings to evacuate, and several schools operated as shelters.<sup id="cite_ref-u_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-u-21">[16]</a></sup> About 700&#160;people evacuated on Saipan,<sup id="cite_ref-ncdc_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc-22">[17]</a></sup> and the airport was closed, stranding about 1,000&#160;travelers.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[18]</a></sup> As a precaution, schools were closed on Guam on August&#160;31, after a tropical storm warning was issued for the island the night prior. The island\'s governor, <a href="/wiki/Felix_Perez_Camacho" title="Felix Perez Camacho">Felix Perez Camacho</a>, also declared a condition of readiness 2,<sup id="cite_ref-u_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-u-21">[16]</a></sup> as well as a state of emergency.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[19]</a></sup> Due to the typhoon, several flights were canceled or delayed at <a href="/wiki/Antonio_B._Won_Pat_International_Airport" title="Antonio B. Won Pat International Airport">Antonio B. Won Pat International Airport</a> on Guam.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[20]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Ahead of the storm, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Forces_Japan" title="United States Forces Japan">United States Forces Japan</a> evacuated planes from Okinawa to either Guam or mainland Japan to prevent damage.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[21]</a></sup> Officials at the military bases on Okinawa advised residents to remain inside during the storm\'s passage. While Nabi was turning to the north, the island was placed under a Condition of Readiness 2.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[22]</a></sup> At the military base in <a href="/wiki/United_States_Fleet_Activities_Sasebo" title="United States Fleet Activities Sasebo">Sasebo</a>, ships also evacuated,<sup id="cite_ref-sas_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sas-28">[23]</a></sup> and several buildings were closed after a Condition of Readiness 1 was declared.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[24]</a></sup></p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2005Typhoon14-Shimanto.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/2005Typhoon14-Shimanto.jpg/220px-2005Typhoon14-Shimanto.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/2005Typhoon14-Shimanto.jpg/330px-2005Typhoon14-Shimanto.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/2005Typhoon14-Shimanto.jpg/440px-2005Typhoon14-Shimanto.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2592" data-file-height="1944" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2005Typhoon14-Shimanto.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nFlood warnings posted along the <a href="/wiki/Shimanto_River" title="Shimanto River">Shimanto River</a> in advance of Nabi in Japan</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>In Kyushu, officials evacuated over a quarter of a million people in fear of Typhoon Nabi affecting Japan.<sup id="cite_ref-BO2_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BO2-30">[25]</a></sup> These continued after the storm made landfall to protect residents from flood waters and landslides. The first order during the storm took place in the <a href="/wiki/Arita,_Saga" title="Arita, Saga">Arita district</a>. In <a href="/wiki/Miyazaki_City" class="mw-redirect" title="Miyazaki City">Miyazaki City</a>, 21,483&#160;households were evacuated following reports of significant overflow on the nearby river. Another 10,000&#160;residences were vacated in <a href="/wiki/Nobeoka" class="mw-redirect" title="Nobeoka">Nobeoka</a> following similar reports.<sup id="cite_ref-warningsMP_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-warningsMP-31">[26]</a></sup> The entirety of the <a href="/wiki/West_Japan_Railway_Company" title="West Japan Railway Company">West Japan Railway Company</a> was shut down.<sup id="cite_ref-gp_4-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-4">[2]</a></sup> Canceled train services affected 77,800&#160;people on <a href="/wiki/Shikoku" title="Shikoku">Shikoku</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[27]</a></sup> Ferry service was also shut down, cutting off transportation for tens of thousands of people. In addition, at least 723&#160;flights were cancelled because of the storm.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[28]</a></sup> Japan\'s second-largest refinery, <a href="/wiki/Idemitsu_Kosan" title="Idemitsu Kosan">Idemitsu Kosan</a>, stopped shipments to other refineries across the area, and Japan\'s largest refinery, <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Oil" class="mw-redirect" title="Nippon Oil">Nippon Oil</a>, stopped all sea shipments. The <a href="/wiki/Cosmo_Oil_Company" title="Cosmo Oil Company">Cosmo Oil Company</a>, Japan\'s fourth largest refinery, stopped all shipments to <a href="/wiki/Yokkaichi" class="mw-redirect" title="Yokkaichi">Yokkaichi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sakaide" class="mw-redirect" title="Sakaide">Sakaide</a> refineries.<sup id="cite_ref-bn95_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bn95-34">[29]</a></sup> About 700&#160;schools in the country were closed. Approximately 1,500&#160;soldiers were dispatched to <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a> to help coastal areas prepare for Typhoon Nabi\'s arrival, and to clean up after the storm.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[30]</a></sup> Officials in the <a href="/wiki/Miyazaki_Prefecture" title="Miyazaki Prefecture">Miyazaki Prefecture</a> issued a <a href="/wiki/Flood_warning" title="Flood warning">flood warning</a> for expected heavy rains in the area.<sup id="cite_ref-warningsMP_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-warningsMP-31">[26]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In South Korea, the government issued a typhoon warning for the southern portion of the country along the coast, prompting the airport at <a href="/wiki/Pohang" title="Pohang">Pohang</a> to close,<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[31]</a></sup> and forcing 162&#160;flights to be canceled.<sup id="cite_ref-xin_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xin-37">[32]</a></sup> Ferry service was also disrupted,<sup id="cite_ref-xin_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xin-37">[32]</a></sup> and thousands of boats returned to port.<sup id="cite_ref-kh97_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kh97-38">[33]</a></sup> The storm also prompted 138&#160;schools to close in the region.<sup id="cite_ref-xin_37-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xin-37">[32]</a></sup> Earlier, the storm spurred fears of a possible repeat of either typhoon <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Rusa" title="Typhoon Rusa">Rusa</a> in 2002 or <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Maemi" title="Typhoon Maemi">Maemi</a> in 2003, both of which were devastating storms in South Korea.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[34]</a></sup> Officials in the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Far_East" title="Russian Far East">Russian Far East</a> issued a storm warning for <a href="/wiki/Vladivostok" title="Vladivostok">Vladivostok</a>, advising boats to remain at port.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[35]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Nabi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>While passing between Saipan and the volcanic island of <a href="/wiki/Anatahan" title="Anatahan">Anatahan</a>, Nabi brought tropical storm force winds to several islands in the Northern Mariana Islands. <a href="/wiki/Saipan_International_Airport" title="Saipan International Airport">Saipan International Airport</a> reported sustained winds of 95&#160;km/h (59&#160;mph), with gusts to 120&#160;km/h (75&#160;mph). Also on the island, Nabi produced 173&#160;mm (6.82&#160;in) of rainfall. The storm destroyed two houses and left 26&#160;others uninhabitable, while 77&#160;homes sustained minor damage, largely from flooding or roof damage. Nabi damaged 70\xe2\x80\x9380% of the crops on Saipan and also knocked down many trees, leaving behind 544&#160;tonnes (600&#160;tons) of debris. The entire island was left without power, some without water, after the storm. On Tinian to the south, Nabi damaged or destroyed nine homes, with heavy crop damage. On Rota, there was minor flooding and scattered power outages. Farther south, the outer reaches of the storm produced sustained winds of 69&#160;km/h (43&#160;mph) at <a href="/wiki/Apra_Harbor" title="Apra Harbor">Apra Harbor</a> on Guam, while gusts peaked at 101&#160;km/h (63&#160;mph) at <a href="/wiki/Mangilao,_Guam" class="mw-redirect" title="Mangilao, Guam">Mangilao</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ncdc_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc-22">[17]</a></sup> Gusts reached 72&#160;km/h (45&#160;mph) at the international airport on Guam, the highest during 2005. The storm dropped 115&#160;mm (4.53&#160;in) of rainfall in 24&#160;hours on the island.<sup id="cite_ref-soest_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-soest-41">[36]</a></sup> Flooding covered roads for several hours and entered classrooms at <a href="/wiki/Guam_Public_School_System#Middle_schools" class="mw-redirect" title="Guam Public School System">Untalan Middle School</a>, forcing hundreds of students to evacuate. Damage in the region was estimated US$2.5&#160;million.<sup id="cite_ref-ncdc_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc-22">[17]</a></sup> After Nabi exited the region, it produced high surf for several days on Guam and Saipan.<sup id="cite_ref-soest_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-soest-41">[36]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Later in its duration, Nabi brushed southeastern South Korea with rainbands.<sup id="cite_ref-gp_4-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-4">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ulsan" title="Ulsan">Ulsan</a> recorded a 24\xe2\x80\x91hour rainfall total of 319&#160;mm (12.6&#160;in),<sup id="cite_ref-gp_4-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-4">[2]</a></sup> while Pohang recorded a record 24\xe2\x80\x91hour total of 540.5&#160;mm (21.28&#160;in).<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[37]</a></sup> The highest total was 622.5&#160;mm (24.5&#160;in) of rainfall.<sup id="cite_ref-SKD_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SKD-43">[38]</a></sup> The periphery of the storm produced gusts of 121&#160;km/h (75&#160;mph) in the port city of <a href="/wiki/Busan" title="Busan">Busan</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-kh97_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kh97-38">[33]</a></sup> strong enough to damage eight billboards and knock trees over.<sup id="cite_ref-gp_4-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-4">[2]</a></sup> Heavy rains caused several traffic accidents and injuries in Busan,<sup id="cite_ref-kh97_38-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kh97-38">[33]</a></sup> while strong waves washed a cargo ship ashore in Pohang.<sup id="cite_ref-gp_4-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-4">[2]</a></sup> Throughout South Korea, the storm led to six fatalities and caused US$115.4&#160;million in damages.<sup id="cite_ref-SKD_43-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SKD-43">[38]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Kuril_Islands" title="Kuril Islands">Kuril Islands</a> of Russia, Nabi dropped about 75&#160;mm (3&#160;in) of rain, equivalent to the monthly average. Gusts reached 83&#160;km/h (51&#160;mph), weak enough not to cause major damage. During the storm\'s passage, high waves washed away unpaved roads in <a href="/wiki/Severo-Kurilsk" title="Severo-Kurilsk">Severo-Kurilsk</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[39]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Japan">Japan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="#cite_note-54">[49]</a></sup></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p>The outer rainbands of Nabi began affecting Okinawa on September&#160;3.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">[50]</a></sup> The storm\'s strongest winds ended up bypassing the island, and wind gusts peaked at 85&#160;km/h (53&#160;mph).<sup id="cite_ref-sas_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sas-28">[23]</a></sup> Two elderly women were injured from the wind gusts. There were minor power outages and some houses were damaged.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">[51]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Amami_Islands" title="Amami Islands">Amami Islands</a> between Okinawa and mainland Japan, Nabi produced gusts of 122&#160;km/h (76&#160;mph) in <a href="/wiki/Kikaijima" title="Kikaijima">Kikaijima</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-dt_57-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dt-57">[52]</a></sup> Waves of 9&#160;m (30&#160;ft) in height affected <a href="/wiki/Amami_%C5%8Cshima" title="Amami \xc5\x8cshima">Amami \xc5\x8cshima</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">[53]</a></sup></p>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Kusatsu_gyoko_typhoon_2005-1.jpg/220px-Kusatsu_gyoko_typhoon_2005-1.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Kusatsu_gyoko_typhoon_2005-1.jpg/330px-Kusatsu_gyoko_typhoon_2005-1.jpg 1.5x, port</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>While moving through western Japan, Nabi dropped heavy rainfall that totaled 1,322&#160;mm (52.0&#160;in) over a three-day period in Miyazaki Prefecture,<sup id="cite_ref-dt_57-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dt-57">[52]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">[54]</a></sup> the equivalent to nearly three times the average annual precipitation.<sup id="cite_ref-ap99_60-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ap99-60">[55]</a></sup> The same station in Miyazaki reported a 24\xe2\x80\x91hour rainfall total of 932&#160;mm (36.7&#160;in), as well as an hourly total of 66&#160;mm (2.6&#160;in).<sup id="cite_ref-dt_57-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dt-57">[52]</a></sup> Within the main islands of Japan, Nabi dropped 228.6&#160;mm (9&#160;in) of rainfall per hour in the capital Tokyo.<sup id="cite_ref-gp_4-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-4">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<p>During the storm\'s passage, there were 61&#160;daily rainfall records broken by Nabi\'s precipitation across Japan.<sup id="cite_ref-gp_4-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-4">[2]</a></sup> The rains from Nabi caused significant <a href="/wiki/Landslide" title="Landslide">slope failures</a> and large accumulations of driftwood. The amount of sediment displaced by the rains was estimated at 4,456&#160;m<sup>3</sup>/km<sup>2</sup>, over four times the yearly average. A total of 630&#160;m<sup>3</sup> (2,066&#160;ft<sup>3</sup>) of driftwood was recorded.<sup id="cite_ref-slopes_61-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-slopes-61">[56]</a></sup> However, the rainfall also helped to end water restrictions in <a href="/wiki/Kagawa_Prefecture" title="Kagawa Prefecture">Kagawa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tokushima" class="mw-redirect" title="Tokushima">Tokushima</a> prefectures.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">[57]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">[58]</a></sup> In addition to the heavy rainfall, Nabi produced gusty winds on the Japan mainland, peaking at 115&#160;km/h (72&#160;mph) in <a href="/wiki/Muroto,_K%C5%8Dchi" title="Muroto, K\xc5\x8dchi">Muroto</a>. A station on <a href="/wiki/Tobishima_(Yamagata)" title="Tobishima (Yamagata)">Tobishima</a> in the Sea of Japan recorded a gust of 119&#160;km/h (74&#160;mph).<sup id="cite_ref-dt_57-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dt-57">[52]</a></sup> The typhoon spawned a <a href="/wiki/Fujita_scale" title="Fujita scale">F1</a> tornado in Miyazaki, which damaged several buildings.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">[59]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Wajima,_Ishikawa" title="Wajima, Ishikawa">Wajima, Ishikawa</a>, Nabi produced a <a href="/wiki/Foehn_wind" title="Foehn wind">Foehn wind</a>, causing temperatures to rise quickly.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">[60]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Throughout Japan, Nabi caused damage in 31 of the 47&#160;<a href="/wiki/Prefectures_of_Japan" title="Prefectures of Japan">prefectures</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">[61]</a></sup> leaving over 270,000&#160;residences without power.<sup id="cite_ref-gp_4-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-4">[2]</a></sup> Torrential rains caused flooding and landslides throughout the country.<sup id="cite_ref-PDO1_67-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PDO1-67">[62]</a></sup> The storm destroyed 7,452&#160;houses and flooded 21,160&#160;others.<sup id="cite_ref-dt_57-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dt-57">[52]</a></sup> Several car assembly plants were damaged in southwestern Japan,<sup id="cite_ref-gp_4-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-4">[2]</a></sup> while others were closed due to power outages, such as Toyota, Mazda, and Mitsubishi.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">[63]</a></sup> In addition, the storm wrecked about 81&#160;ships along the coast.<sup id="cite_ref-dt_57-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dt-57">[52]</a></sup> On the island of Kyushu, damage in <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Cita_Prefecture" title="\xc5\x8cita Prefecture">\xc5\x8cita Prefecture</a> on Kyushu reached \xc2\xa511.7&#160;billion (US$106&#160;million), the fifth highest of any typhoon in the preceding 10&#160;years; about 20% of the total there was related to road damages.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">[64]</a></sup> In nearby <a href="/wiki/Saga_Prefecture" title="Saga Prefecture">Saga Prefecture</a>, crop damage totaled about \xc2\xa51.06&#160;billion (US$9.6&#160;million), mostly to rice but also to soybeans and various other vegetables.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">[65]</a></sup> Crop damage as a whole on Kyushu totaled \xc2\xa54.08&#160;billion (US$36.9&#160;million).<sup id="cite_ref-ap99_60-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ap99-60">[55]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In the capital city of Tokyo, heavy rainfall increased levels along several rivers, which inundated several houses.<sup id="cite_ref-Digital_Typhoon_71-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Digital_Typhoon-71">[66]</a></sup> Strong winds damaged \xc2\xa528.8&#160;million (US$259,000) in crop damage in <a href="/wiki/Gifu_Prefecture" title="Gifu Prefecture">Gifu Prefecture</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">[67]</a></sup> and \xc2\xa527.1&#160;million (US$244,000) in crop damage in <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">[68]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Yamaguchi_Prefecture" title="Yamaguchi Prefecture">Yamaguchi Prefecture</a> on western <a href="/wiki/Honshu" title="Honshu">Honshu</a>, Nabi damaged a portion of the historical <a href="/wiki/Kintai_Bridge" title="Kintai Bridge">Kintai Bridge</a>, originally built in 1674.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">[69]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Yamagata_Prefecture" title="Yamagata Prefecture">Yamagata Prefecture</a>, the winds damaged a window in a school, injuring several boys from the debris.<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">[70]</a></sup> One person was seriously injured in <a href="/wiki/Kitakata,_Fukushima" title="Kitakata, Fukushima">Kitakata, Fukushima</a> after strong winds blew a worker from scaffolding of a building under construction.<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">[71]</a></sup> Effects from Nabi spread as far north as Hokkaido, where heavy rainfall damaged roads and caused hundreds of schools to close.<sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">[72]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Ashoro,_Hokkaido" title="Ashoro, Hokkaido">Ashoro</a>, an overflown river flooded a hotel,<sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78">[73]</a></sup> and a minor power outage occurred in <a href="/wiki/Teshikaga,_Hokkaido" title="Teshikaga, Hokkaido">Teshikaga</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">[74]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Ahead of the storm, high waves and gusty winds led to one drowning when a woman was knocked off a ferry in <a href="/wiki/Takamatsu,_Kagawa" title="Takamatsu, Kagawa">Takamatsu, Kagawa</a>. A landslide in <a href="/wiki/Miyazaki,_Miyazaki" title="Miyazaki, Miyazaki">Miyazaki</a> destroyed five homes,<sup id="cite_ref-IOL1_80-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IOL1-80">[75]</a></sup> killing three people. A man who was listed as missing was found dead in a flooded rice field. In Tarumi, a landslide buried a home in mud, killing two people.<sup id="cite_ref-PDO1_67-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PDO1-67">[62]</a></sup> Nabi caused a portion of the <a href="/wiki/San%27y%C5%8D_Expressway" title="San\'y\xc5\x8d Expressway">San\'y\xc5\x8d Expressway</a> to collapse in Yamaguchi Prefecture, killing three people.<sup id="cite_ref-Digital_Typhoon_71-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Digital_Typhoon-71">[66]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Fukui_Prefecture" title="Fukui Prefecture">Fukui Prefecture</a>, the winds knocked an elderly man off a bicycle, killing him.<sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-81">[76]</a></sup> Overall, Nabi killed 29&#160;people in Japan and injured 179&#160;others, 45 of them severely. Damage was estimated at \xc2\xa594.9&#160;billion (US$854&#160;million).<sup id="cite_ref-dt_57-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dt-57">[52]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Nabi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>After the storm, members of <a href="/wiki/Marine_Corps_Air_Station_Iwakuni" title="Marine Corps Air Station Iwakuni">Marine Corps Air Station Iwakuni</a> provided $2,500 to the town of <a href="/wiki/Iwakuni,_Yamaguchi" class="mw-redirect" title="Iwakuni, Yamaguchi">Iwakuni</a> toward cleanup and disaster relief.<sup id="cite_ref-82" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-82">[77]</a></sup> Soldiers also helped nearby residents and farmers to complete the rice harvest, after floods from the typhoon damaged harvesting machines.<sup id="cite_ref-83" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-83">[78]</a></sup> The local government of Iwakunda distributed disinfectant chemicals to flooded houses.<sup id="cite_ref-84" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-84">[79]</a></sup> Closed markets and decreased supplies caused the price of beef to reach record levels in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-85" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-85">[80]</a></sup> Following the storm, the General Insurance Association of Japan reported that insurance claims from the typhoon totaled \xc2\xa558.8&#160;billion (US$53&#160;million), the tenth-highest for any natural disaster in the country. Miyazaki Prefecture reported the highest claims with \xc2\xa512.6&#160;billion (US$11.4&#160;million). The total was split between \xc2\xa549&#160;billion (US$44&#160;million) in housing claims and \xc2\xa57.9&#160;billion (US$71&#160;million) in car claims.<sup id="cite_ref-jiji_86-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jiji-86">[81]</a></sup> The Japanese government provided food, water, and rescue workers to the affected areas in the days after the storm, along with <a href="/wiki/Japan_Post" title="Japan Post">Japan Post</a>, the local post system; trucks were mobilized to affected towns, accompanied by a mobile bank and insurance agent.<sup id="cite_ref-87" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-87">[82]</a></sup></p>\n<p>On November&#160;8, nearly two months after the dissipation of Typhoon Nabi, <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President</a> <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">George W. Bush</a> declared a major disaster declaration for the Northern Mariana islands. The declaration allocated aid from the United States to help restore damaged buildings, pay for debris removal, and other emergency services. Federal funding was also made available on a cost-sharing basis for the islands to mitigate against future disasters.<sup id="cite_ref-88" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-88">[83]</a></sup> The government ultimately provided $1,046,074.03 to the commonwealth.<sup id="cite_ref-89" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-89">[84]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Due to the damage of the storm in Japan, the Typhoon Committee of the <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">World Meteorological Organization</a> agreed to <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Pacific_typhoon_names" title="List of retired Pacific typhoon names">retire</a> the name <i>Nabi</i>. The agency replaced it with the name <i>Doksuri</i>, effective January&#160;1, 2007,<sup id="cite_ref-90" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-90">[85]</a></sup> and was first used during the <a href="/wiki/2012_Pacific_typhoon_season" title="2012 Pacific typhoon season">2012 Pacific typhoon season</a>.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span
