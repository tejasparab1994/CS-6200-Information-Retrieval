href="/wiki/South_Pole" title="South Pole">South Pole</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>The <b>Southern Hemisphere</b> is the half <a href="/wiki/Sphere" title="Sphere">sphere</a> of <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a> which is south of the <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">equator</a>. It contains all or parts of five <a href="/wiki/Continent" title="Continent">continents</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia_(continent)" title="Australia (continent)">Australia</a>, about 90% of <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>, the southern third of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, and several southern islands off the continental <a href="/wiki/Mainland" title="Mainland">mainland</a> of <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>), four <a href="/wiki/Ocean" title="Ocean">oceans</a> (<a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">South Atlantic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Southern_Ocean" title="Southern Ocean">Southern</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">South Pacific</a>) and most of the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Islands" title="Pacific Islands">Pacific Islands</a> in <a href="/wiki/Oceania" title="Oceania">Oceania</a>. Its surface is 80.9% water, compared with 60.7% water in the case of the Northern Hemisphere, and it contains 32.7% of Earth\'s land.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Owing to the tilt of <a href="/wiki/Earth%27s_rotation" title="Earth\'s rotation">Earth\'s rotation</a> relative to the <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">Sun</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Ecliptic" title="Ecliptic">ecliptic plane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Summer" title="Summer">summer</a> is from December to March and <a href="/wiki/Winter" title="Winter">winter</a> is from June to September. September 22 or 23 is the vernal <a href="/wiki/Equinox" title="Equinox">equinox</a> and March 20 or 21 is the autumnal equinox. The <a href="/wiki/South_Pole" title="South Pole">South Pole</a> is in the middle of the southern hemispherical region.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc">\n<div class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Southern Hemisphere <a href="/wiki/Climate" title="Climate">climates</a> tend to be slightly milder than those at similar latitudes in the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Hemisphere" title="Northern Hemisphere">Northern Hemisphere</a>, except in the <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctic</a> which is colder than the <a href="/wiki/Arctic" title="Arctic">Arctic</a>. <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>In the Southern Hemisphere the sun passes from east to west through the north, although north of the <a href="/wiki/Tropic_of_Capricorn" title="Tropic of Capricorn">Tropic of Capricorn</a> the <a href="/wiki/Mean_solar_time" class="mw-redirect" title="Mean solar time">mean sun</a> can be directly overhead or due south at midday. The Sun rotating through the north causes an apparent right-left trajectory through the sky unlike the left-right motion of the Sun when seen from the Northern Hemisphere as it passes through the southern sky. Sun-cast shadows turn anticlockwise throughout the day and <a href="/wiki/Sundial" title="Sundial">sundials</a> have the hours increasing in the anticlockwise direction. During <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse" title="Solar eclipse">solar eclipses</a> viewed from a point to the south of the Tropic of Capricorn, the Moon moves from left to right on the disc of the Sun (see, for example, photos with timings of the <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_November_13,_2012" title="Solar eclipse of November 13, 2012">solar eclipse of November 13, 2012</a>), while viewed from a point to the north of the Tropic of Cancer (i.e.,&#160;in the Northern Hemisphere), the Moon moves from right to left during solar eclipses.</p>\n<p>Cyclones and tropical storms spin clockwise in the Southern Hemisphere (as opposed to <a href="/wiki/Anticlockwise" class="mw-redirect" title="Anticlockwise">anticlockwise</a> in the Northern Hemisphere) due to the <a href="/wiki/Coriolis_effect" class="mw-redirect" title="Coriolis effect">Coriolis effect</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The southern <a href="/wiki/Temperateness" class="mw-redirect" title="Temperateness">temperate zone</a>, a subsection of the Southern Hemisphere, is nearly all oceanic. This zone includes the southern tip of <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">Uruguay</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>; the southern half of <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a> and <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>; parts of <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, going south from <a href="/wiki/Adelaide" title="Adelaide">Adelaide</a>, and all of <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>.</p>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Sagittarius_(constellation)" title="Sagittarius (constellation)">Sagittarius</a> constellation that includes the <a href="/wiki/Milky_Way" title="Milky Way">galactic centre</a> is a southern constellation and this, combined with clearer skies, makes for excellent viewing of the night sky from the Southern Hemisphere with brighter and more numerous stars.</p>\n<p>Forests in the Southern Hemisphere have special features which set them apart from those in the Northern Hemisphere. Both <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a> and <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> share, for example, unique <a href="/wiki/Beech" title="Beech">beech</a> species or <i><a href="/wiki/Nothofagus" title="Nothofagus">Nothofagus</a></i>, and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> has members of the closely related genera <a href="/wiki/Lophozonia" title="Lophozonia">Lophozonia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fuscospora" title="Fuscospora">Fuscospora</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Eucalyptus" title="Eucalyptus">eucalyptus</a> is native to <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> but is now also planted in <a href="/wiki/Southern_Africa" title="Southern Africa">Southern Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin America</a> for pulp production and, increasingly, <a href="/wiki/Biofuel" title="Biofuel">biofuel</a> uses.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a 800,000,000 humans live in the Southern Hemisphere, representing only 10\xe2\x80\x9312% of the total global human population of 7.3 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a> is the most spoken language in the Southern Hemisphere.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="List_of_continents_and_countries">List of
