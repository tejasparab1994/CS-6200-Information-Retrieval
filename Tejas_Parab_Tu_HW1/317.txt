border-top:#aaa 1px solid;">Part of the <b><a href="/wiki/1780_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1780 Atlantic hurricane season">1780 Atlantic hurricane season</a></b></td>\n</tr>\n<tr style="display:none">\n<td colspan="2"></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p>The <b>Great Hurricane of 1780</b>, also known as <b>Hurac\xc3\xa1n San Calixto</b>, the <b>Great Hurricane of the Antilles</b>, and the <b>1780 Disaster</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-Mujica_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mujica-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pr_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pr-2">[2]</a></sup> is the deadliest <a href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_tropical_cyclone" class="mw-redirect" title="North Atlantic tropical cyclone">Atlantic hurricane</a> on record. Between 20,000 and 22,000 people died throughout the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a> when the storm passed through them from October 10\xe2\x80\x9316.<sup id="cite_ref-deadliest_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-deadliest-3">[3]</a></sup> Specifics on the hurricane\'s track and strength are unknown because the official <a href="/wiki/List_of_Atlantic_hurricane_seasons" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Atlantic hurricane seasons">Atlantic hurricane database</a> only goes back to 1851.<sup id="cite_ref-reanal_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reanal-4">[4]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The hurricane struck <a href="/wiki/Barbados" title="Barbados">Barbados</a> with winds possibly exceeding 320&#160;km/h (200&#160;mph)<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>, before moving past <a href="/wiki/Martinique" title="Martinique">Martinique</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saint_Lucia" title="Saint Lucia">Saint Lucia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sint_Eustatius" title="Sint Eustatius">Sint Eustatius</a>; thousands of deaths were reported on the islands. Coming in the midst of the <a href="/wiki/American_Revolution" title="American Revolution">American Revolution</a>, the storm caused heavy losses to British and French fleets <a href="/wiki/Naval_operations_in_the_American_Revolutionary_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Naval operations in the American Revolutionary War">contesting for control</a> of the area. The hurricane later passed near <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> and over the eastern portion of <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">Hispaniola</a>. There, it caused heavy damage near the coastlines. It ultimately turned to the northeast and was last observed on October 20 southeast of <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Canada" title="Atlantic Canada">Atlantic Canada</a>.</p>\n<p>The death toll from the Great Hurricane alone exceeds that of many entire decades of Atlantic hurricanes. Estimates are marginally higher than for <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Mitch" title="Hurricane Mitch">Hurricane Mitch</a>, the second-deadliest Atlantic storm, for which figures are likely more accurate. The hurricane was part of the disastrous <a href="/wiki/1780_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1780 Atlantic hurricane season">1780 Atlantic hurricane season</a>, with two other deadly storms occurring in October.<sup id="cite_ref-deadliest_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-deadliest-3">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<p></p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc">\n<div class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Great_hurricane_of_1780_Sint_Eustatius.jpg" class="image"><img alt="A beach with mainly palm trees and destroyed buildings" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Great_hurricane_of_1780_Sint_Eustatius.jpg/220px-Great_hurricane_of_1780_Sint_Eustatius.jpg" width="220" height="164" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Great_hurricane_of_1780_Sint_Eustatius.jpg/330px-Great_hurricane_of_1780_Sint_Eustatius.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Great_hurricane_of_1780_Sint_Eustatius.jpg/440px-Great_hurricane_of_1780_Sint_Eustatius.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2336" data-file-height="1742" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Great_hurricane_of_1780_Sint_Eustatius.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nWarehouses on the beach of St. Eustatius were damaged by the hurricane.</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>The exact origin of the hurricane is unknown since nobody recorded anything or anticipated anything, but it probably developed, as do many hurricanes, in the east Atlantic Ocean off the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde" title="Cape Verde">Cape Verde</a> Islands in early October. The system strengthened and expanded as it tracked slowly westward and first began affecting <a href="/wiki/Barbados" title="Barbados">Barbados</a> late on October 9. Late on October 10, the worst of the hurricane passed over the island. Early on October 11, the hurricane turned north-northwest about 90 kilometres (56&#160;mi) east of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Lucia" title="Saint Lucia">Saint Lucia</a>, and later that night it neared the island of <a href="/wiki/Martinique" title="Martinique">Martinique</a>. The cyclone gradually weakened as it passed to the southwest of <a href="/wiki/Dominica" title="Dominica">Dominica</a> early on October 12 and subsequently struck the island of <a href="/wiki/Guadeloupe" title="Guadeloupe">Guadeloupe</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pr_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pr-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<p>After hitting Guadeloupe, the hurricane turned west-northwest, passing about 145 kilometres (90&#160;mi) southwest of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Kitts" title="Saint Kitts">Saint Kitts</a>. The hurricane steadily neared <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> as it paralleled the southern coastline, and on October 14 made its closest point of approach, to the southwest portion of the island. It subsequently turned to the northwest, hitting the island of <a href="/wiki/Mona,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Mona, Puerto Rico">Mona</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Mona_Passage" title="Mona Passage">Mona Passage</a> before making landfall near the present-day <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a> province of <a href="/wiki/Saman%C3%A1_Province" title="Saman\xc3\xa1 Province">Saman\xc3\xa1</a>. Late on October 15, it reached the Atlantic Ocean and after passing about 260 kilometres (160&#160;mi) east of <a href="/wiki/Grand_Turk_Island" title="Grand Turk Island">Grand Turk Island</a>; it is estimated to have recurved to the northeast. The hurricane passed 240 kilometres (150&#160;mi) southeast of <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> on October 18,<sup id="cite_ref-pr_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pr-2">[2]</a></sup> and was last observed two days later about 475 kilometres (295&#160;mi) southeast of <a href="/wiki/Cape_Race" title="Cape Race">Cape Race, Newfoundland, Canada</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-revised_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-revised-6">[6]</a></sup></p>\n<p>On October 19, strong winds and high tides were reported in the British province of <a href="/wiki/East_Florida" title="East Florida">East Florida</a> (the northeastern portion of present-day <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>). One modern historian suggests the hurricane passed much closer to the province than previously thought. Another possibility considered was an extension to a hurricane in the western Caribbean Sea. Because of lack of data, the exact track of the Great Hurricane is unknown.<sup id="cite_ref-aoml_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aoml-7">[7]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Great_Hurricane_of_1780&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Great Hurricane first began affecting <a href="/wiki/Barbados" title="Barbados">Barbados</a> with rain late on October 9. Northwesterly winds increased through the day on October 10.</p>\n<blockquote class="templatequote">\n<p>"<i>... a dreadful hurricane which began to rage with great fury at noon and continue with great violence till four o\'clock the next morning, the 11th; At eight o\'clock at night <a href="/wiki/Saint_Thomas,_Barbados" title="Saint Thomas, Barbados">St. Thomas</a>\'s parsonage was demolished and the church where the Rector and his family saought shelter began to fall about two hours after, the Chancel fell while the family were in the church ... St. Thomas\'s Chapel, St. Michael\'s, St. George\'s, Christ Church\'s and St. Lucy\'s churches were totally destroyed, the other churches were severely</i> \xe2\x80\x98injured\xe2\x80\x99 <i>(except St. Peter\'s and St. Philip\'s). Because of the demolition of the parish church and chapel \xe2\x80\x98divine services\xe2\x80\x99 continued in the \xe2\x80\x98boiling house\xe2\x80\x99 at the \xe2\x80\x98Rock Hall\xe2\x80\x99 estate of Thomas Harper by Rev Wm Duke and curate Hugh Austin of St Thomas. Most other buildings and works were blown down and many lives were lost. The dead could not be brought to a church so were buried in gardens and private land.</i>\xe2\x80\x9d<sup id="cite_ref-bdosreg_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bdosreg-8">[8]</a></sup></p>\n</blockquote>\n<p>The ships in the bay broke their moorings by 4 pm, and the "full impact" arrived by 6 pm. The wind gradually backed to westerly through the night and peaked at midnight. Wind speed returned to normal by 8 am on October 11.<sup id="cite_ref-pr_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pr-2">[2]</a></sup> These winds suggest that the eye of the storm passed north of Barbados. The hurricane produced violent winds "so deafening that people could not hear their own voices." The hurricane stripped the bark off trees and left none standing on the island.<sup id="cite_ref-pr_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pr-2">[2]</a></sup> Even the strongest hurricanes do not strip the bark off trees, and Cuban meteorologist <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Mill%C3%A1s" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Carlos Mill\xc3\xa1s">Jos\xc3\xa9 Carlos Mill\xc3\xa1s</a> estimated that this phenomenon would require rain and winds over 200 miles per hour (320&#160;km/h).<sup id="cite_ref-nemo_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nemo-9">[9]</a></sup> The winds also destroyed every house on Barbados. The hurricane also destroyed all forts on the island.<sup id="cite_ref-pr_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pr-2">[2]</a></sup> According to British admiral <a href="/wiki/George_Brydges_Rodney,_1st_Baron_Rodney" title="George Brydges Rodney, 1st Baron Rodney">George Brydges Rodney, 1st Baron Rodney</a>, the winds carried their heavy cannons aloft 100 feet (30&#160;m). About 4,500&#160;people died on Barbados.<sup id="cite_ref-nemo_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nemo-9">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<table class="wikitable" align="right" style="margin:0 0 0.5em href="/wiki/St._Vincent_(island)" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Vincent (island)">Saint Vincent</a>, the hurricane destroyed 584 of the 600&#160;houses in <a href="/wiki/Kingstown" title="Kingstown">Kingstown</a>. At <a href="/wiki/Grenada" title="Grenada">Grenada</a>, 19 <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a> ships were wrecked. On <a href="/wiki/Saint_Lucia" title="Saint Lucia">Saint Lucia</a>, rough waves and a strong <a href="/wiki/Storm_tide" class="mw-redirect" title="Storm tide">storm tide</a> struck the fleet of British <a href="/wiki/George_Brydges_Rodney,_1st_Baron_Rodney" title="George Brydges Rodney, 1st Baron Rodney">Admiral Rodney</a> at Port <a href="/wiki/Castries" title="Castries">Castries</a>, with one ship destroying the city\'s hospital by being lifted on top of it. The hurricane destroyed all but two houses at Port Castries, and throughout the island about 6,000 perished.<sup id="cite_ref-pr_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pr-2">[2]</a></sup> Among the ships lost from Rodney\'s fleet were the <a href="/wiki/Frigates" class="mw-redirect" title="Frigates">frigates</a> <a href="/wiki/HMS_Phoenix_(1759)" title="HMS Phoenix (1759)">HMS <i>Phoenix</i></a> wrecked on the Cuban coast, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=HMS_Blanche_(1779)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="HMS Blanche (1779) (page does not exist)">HMS <i>Blanche</i></a> which disappeared without a trace, the <a href="/wiki/Sixth_rate" class="mw-redirect" title="Sixth rate">sixth rate</a> frigates <a href="/wiki/HMS_Andromeda_(1777)" title="HMS Andromeda (1777)">HMS <i>Andromeda</i></a> and <a href="/wiki/HMS_Laurel_(1779)" title="HMS Laurel (1779)">HMS <i>Laurel</i></a> which were wrecked on Martinique with heavy loss of life and five other smaller vessels either wrecked or foundered. By far the worst losses in the British fleet however were among the squadron of Rear-Admiral <a href="/wiki/Sir_Joshua_Rowley,_1st_Baronet" class="mw-redirect" title="Sir Joshua Rowley, 1st Baronet">Joshua Rowley</a>, which was caught at sea by the hurricane off the San Domingo coast. <a href="/wiki/Ships_of_the_line" class="mw-redirect" title="Ships of the line">Ships of the line</a> <a href="/wiki/HMS_Thunderer_(1760)" title="HMS Thunderer (1760)">HMS <i>Thunderer</i></a> which foundered with all hands, and <a href="/wiki/HMS_Stirling_Castle_(1775)" title="HMS Stirling Castle (1775)">HMS <i>Stirling Castle</i></a> which was smashed on the coast with fewer than 50 survivors, were lost and the remaining six ships were all severely damaged.<sup id="cite_ref-WLC58_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WLC58-10">[10]</a></sup></p>\n<p>A fleet of 40 French ships involved in the <a href="/wiki/France_in_the_American_Revolutionary_War" title="France in the American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a> capsized as a result of the hurricane off <a href="/wiki/Martinique" title="Martinique">Martinique</a>; about 4,000 soldiers drowned. Among the French losses was the frigate <a href="/wiki/French_frigate_Junon_(1778)" title="French frigate Junon (1778)"><i>Junon</i></a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WLC114_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WLC114-11">[11]</a></sup> The hurricane produced a 25-foot (7.6&#160;m) storm surge on Martinique, destroying all houses in <a href="/wiki/Saint-Pierre,_Martinique" title="Saint-Pierre, Martinique">Saint-Pierre</a>; 9,000 died on the island. High winds, rains, and storm surge caused severe damage at <a href="/wiki/Roseau" title="Roseau">Roseau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dominica" title="Dominica">Dominica</a>. A storm surge also struck the south coast of Guadeloupe and caused considerable damage. Strong winds affected <a href="/wiki/Antigua" title="Antigua">Antigua</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saint_Kitts" title="Saint Kitts">Saint Kitts</a>, and in the latter island, many ships were washed ashore. A Dutch sea-officer whose ship was blown from <a href="/wiki/St._Eustatius" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Eustatius">St. Eustatius</a> to Martinique by the hurricane reports on the damage in Saint-Pierre (Martinique), St. Vincent and St. Lucia. On his return to St. Eustatius he reports that here "too some houses are destroyed" and the sea water ruined the merchandise that was left on the beach. However, he says that the situation was not as bad as on the French and English islands and does not speak of any dramatic death toll.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Heavy damage was reported in southern <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, primarily in <a href="/wiki/Cabo_Rojo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Cabo Rojo, Puerto Rico">Cabo Rojo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lajas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Lajas, Puerto Rico">Lajas</a>. Severe damage also occurred in the eastern region of Santo Domingo. The hurricane later grounded 50 ships near <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a>. Throughout its path, the hurricane killed over 20,000&#160;people, possibly as high as 24,000, making it the deadliest hurricane in Atlantic hurricane history.<sup id="cite_ref-pr_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pr-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-deadliest_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-deadliest-3">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline"
