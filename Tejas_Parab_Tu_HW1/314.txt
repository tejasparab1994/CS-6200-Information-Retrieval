Pacific typhoon season</a></b></td>\n</tr>\n<tr style="display:none">\n<td colspan="2"></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Typhoon Nina</b>, known in the Philippines as <b>Typhoon Bebeng</b>, was the fourth-deadliest <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> on record. Approximately 229,000&#160;people died after the <a href="/wiki/Banqiao_Dam" title="Banqiao Dam">Banqiao Dam</a> (in <a href="/wiki/Zhumadian" title="Zhumadian">Zhumadian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Henan" title="Henan">Henan</a> Province, China) collapsed and devastated areas downstream. The collapse of the dam due to heavy floods also caused a string of smaller dams to collapse, adding more damage by the typhoon.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc">\n<div id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Nina_(1975)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nina_1975_track.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/70/Nina_1975_track.png/220px-Nina_1975_track.png" width="220" height="136" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/70/Nina_1975_track.png/330px-Nina_1975_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/70/Nina_1975_track.png/440px-Nina_1975_track.png 2x" data-file-width="2000" data-file-height="1236" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nina_1975_track.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nMap plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>A well defined trough line extending southeastward into the Philippine Sea spawned a disturbance on July 29. After initial status as a disturbance, Tropical Depression 04W moved southwestward for 36&#160;hours as the structure of the system began to organize. On July 31, the depression slowed down and began to <a href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapid deepening">rapidly intensify</a> to a tropical storm and was named "Nina" then it began to turn to the northwest. A subtropical ridge prevented Nina from turning further north and it began to track west-northwest just before reaching typhoon intensity.</p>\n<p>Nina underwent explosive development on the late hours of August 1. Aircraft reconnaissance reported a 65&#160;hPa drop of pressure on the same day as well as August 2 with wind speeds increasing from a mere 65&#160;kn (75&#160;mph, 120&#160;km/h) to 130&#160;kt (150&#160;mph, 240&#160;km/h) during that period and it attained its peak intensity of 135&#160;kn (155&#160;mph, 250&#160;km/h) later that day. The typhoon began to weaken as it approached <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>, making landfall near the coastal city of <a href="/wiki/Hualien_City" title="Hualien City">Hualien</a> as a Category&#160;3 storm with 100&#160;kn (115&#160;mph, 185&#160;km/h) winds.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The storm began to weaken as it battered across the island\'s central mountain range, sparing the most populated areas from the eyewall. It entered the <a href="/wiki/Formosa_Straits" class="mw-redirect" title="Formosa Straits">Formosa Straits</a> as a weak typhoon and the storm came ashore near <a href="/wiki/Jinjiang,_Fujian" title="Jinjiang, Fujian">Jinjiang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fujian" title="Fujian">Fujian</a>, <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" class="mw-redirect" title="People\'s Republic of China">China</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> After moving toward the northwest and crossing <a href="/wiki/Jiangxi" title="Jiangxi">Jiangxi</a>, it turned north on the night of August 5 near <a href="/wiki/Changde" title="Changde">Changde</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hunan" title="Hunan">Hunan</a>. A day later, the storm moved over <a href="/wiki/Xinyang" title="Xinyang">Xinyang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Henan" title="Henan">Henan</a>, and later was blocked by a cold front near <a href="/wiki/Zhumadian" title="Zhumadian">Zhumadian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Henan" title="Henan">Henan</a> for three days.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The stationary thunderstorm system brought heavy rainfall, causing the infamous collapse of the <a href="/wiki/Banqiao_Dam" title="Banqiao Dam">Banqiao Dam</a>. The storm moved southwest on August 8, and dissipated soon afterwards.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Nina_(1975)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Taiwan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Upon making landfall in Taiwan, the storm brought winds of 185&#160;km/h (115&#160;mph) to places near the storm\'s eye.<sup id="cite_ref-ATCR_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ATCR-5">[5]</a></sup> Wind gusts were also measured up to 222&#160;km/h (138&#160;mph).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Widespread heavy rainfall, peaking around 700&#160;mm (28&#160;in),<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> from the storm triggered deadly flooding and landslides which killed 29 people and injured 168 others. Reports from the island indicate that 3,000 homes were damaged or destroyed by the typhoon.<sup id="cite_ref-ATCR_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ATCR-5">[5]</a></sup> In the city of <a href="/wiki/Hualien_City" title="Hualien City">Hualien</a> alone, four people were killed, 561 homes were destroyed and 1,831 more were damaged.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Across the island, domestic flights, trains, and bus services were all suspended due to the storm; however, <a href="/wiki/Taipei_Songshan_Airport" title="Taipei Songshan Airport">Taipei Songshan Airport</a> remained open for international flights.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mainland_China">Mainland China</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Nina_(1975)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Mainland China">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Banqiao_Dam#1975_flood" title="Banqiao Dam">Banqiao Dam \xc2\xa7&#160;1975 flood</a></div>\n<p>Due to the interaction with the mountains of Taiwan, Nina weakened to a tropical storm before making landfall in mainland China. The storm crossed the coastline with winds of 110&#160;km/h (70&#160;mph); however, little damage resulted near where the system struck land.<sup id="cite_ref-ATCR_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ATCR-5">[5]</a></sup> Further inland, the remnants of the storm produced widespread torrential rainfall, with more than 400&#160;mm (16&#160;in) falling across an area of 19,410&#160;km<sup>2</sup> (7,500&#160;mi<sup>2</sup>). The heaviest rainfall was recorded along the <a href="/wiki/Banqiao_Dam" title="Banqiao Dam">Banqiao Dam</a> where 1,631&#160;mm (64.2&#160;in) of rain fell, 830&#160;mm (33&#160;in) of which fell in a six-hour span.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> These rains led to the collapse of the <a href="/wiki/Banqiao_Dam" title="Banqiao Dam">Banqiao Dam</a>, which received 1-in-2000-year flood conditions. In all, 62 dams failed during the disaster, causing large temporary <a href="/wiki/Lake" title="Lake">lakes</a> and $1.2 billion (1975&#160;USD) in damage.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Nina_(1975)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul>\n<li><a href="/wiki/List_of_tropical_cyclones" title="List of tropical cyclones">List of tropical cyclones</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Banqiao_Dam" title="Banqiao Dam">Banqiao Dam</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Cyclone_Nargis" title="Cyclone Nargis">Cyclone Nargis</a></li>\n</ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Nina_(1975)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap mw-references-columns">\n<ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20100202110321/http://www.usno.navy.mil/NOOC/nmfc-ph/RSS/jtwc/atcr/1975atcr/pdf/1975_complete.pdf">"Archived copy"</a> <span style="font-size:85%;">(PDF)</span>.
