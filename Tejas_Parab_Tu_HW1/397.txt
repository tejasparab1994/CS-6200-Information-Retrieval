Elizabeth Mastrantonio">Mary Elizabeth Mastrantonio</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-reelviews_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reelviews-2">[2]</a></sup> It was released on June 30, 2000, by <a class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Perfect_Storm_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In October 1991, the commercial fishing boat <i><a href="/wiki/Andrea_Gail" title="Andrea Gail">Andrea Gail</a></i> returns to port in <a href="/wiki/Gloucester,_Massachusetts" title="Gloucester, Massachusetts">Gloucester, Massachusetts</a>, with a poor catch. Desperate for money, Captain Billy Tyne (<a href="/wiki/George_Clooney" title="George Clooney">Clooney</a>) convinces the <i>Andrea Gail</i> crew to join him for one more late season fishing expedition. The crew heads out past their usual fishing grounds in the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Banks" class="mw-redirect" title="Grand Banks">Grand Banks</a>, leaving a developing <a href="/wiki/Thunderstorm" title="Thunderstorm">thunderstorm</a> behind them. Initially unsuccessful, they head to the <a href="/wiki/Flemish_Cap" title="Flemish Cap">Flemish Cap</a>, where their luck greatly improves. At the height of their fishing the ice machine breaks; the only way to sell their catch before it spoils is to hurry back to shore. After debating whether to sail through the building storm or to wait it out, the crew decides to risk the storm. However, between the <i>Andrea Gail</i> and Gloucester is a confluence of two powerful weather fronts and a <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Grace" title="Hurricane Grace">hurricane</a>, which the <i>Andrea Gail</i> crew underestimates.</p>\n<p>After repeated warnings from other ships, <i>Andrea Gail</i> loses her antenna, forcing Captain <a href="/wiki/Linda_Greenlaw" title="Linda Greenlaw">Linda Greenlaw</a> (<a href="/wiki/Mary_Elizabeth_Mastrantonio" title="Mary Elizabeth Mastrantonio">Mastrantonio</a>) of sister ship <i>Hannah Boden</i> to call in a <a href="/wiki/Mayday_(distress_signal)" class="mw-redirect" title="Mayday (distress signal)">Mayday</a>. A <a href="/wiki/New_York_Air_National_Guard" title="New York Air National Guard">New York Air National Guard</a> <a href="/wiki/HH-60_Pave_Hawk" class="mw-redirect" title="HH-60 Pave Hawk">HH-60 Pave Hawk</a> rescue helicopter responds, but after failing to perform a midair refueling with an <a href="/wiki/HC-130_Hercules" class="mw-redirect" title="HC-130 Hercules">HC-130 Hercules</a>, the helicopter crew ditch their aircraft. All but one of the <a href="/wiki/Air_National_Guard" title="Air National Guard">Air National Guard</a> crew members are rescued by a <a href="/wiki/USCG_Medium_Endurance_Cutter#Reliance_class_cutters" class="mw-redirect" title="USCG Medium Endurance Cutter">Coast Guard vessel</a>, the <a href="/wiki/USCGC_Tamaroa_(WMEC-166)" title="USCGC Tamaroa (WMEC-166)">USCGC <i>Tamaroa</i></a>.</p>\n<p>After <i>Andrea Gail</i> endures various problems, the crew struggles to sail through pounding waves and shrieking winds, while friends and family worry and wait for a ship that never comes home. The vessel encounters an enormous <a href="/wiki/Rogue_wave" title="Rogue wave">rogue wave</a>. They attempt to drive the boat over the wave but it crests before it can get to the top and is overturned; Billy elects to <a href="/wiki/The_captain_goes_down_with_the_ship" title="The captain goes down with the ship">go down with his ship</a>, the rest of the crew are trapped inside and only one manages to get out. He surfaces and watches as the <i>Andrea Gail</i> rights itself before sinking stern-first into the Atlantic. The rapidly rising swell carries the man away.</p>\n<p>There are no survivors; Linda reads the eulogy at the memorial, followed by her remembering Billy\'s voice soliloquising about what it means to be a swordboat captain.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cast">Cast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Perfect_Storm_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul>\n<li><a href="/wiki/George_Clooney" title="George Clooney">George Clooney</a> as Frank William "Billy" Tyne, Jr., captain of the <i><a href="/wiki/Andrea_Gail" title="Andrea Gail">Andrea Gail</a></i>, a swordfishing boat. Billy is a divorced father of two daughters, who is determined to undertake one last fishing trip before the end of the season to make up for a recent string of poor catches.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Mark_Wahlberg" title="Mark Wahlberg">Mark Wahlberg</a> as Robert "Bobby" Shatford, the least experienced of the crew of the <i>Andrea Gail</i>. Bobby is the son of Ethel Shatford, the owner of the Crow\'s Nest, and boyfriend to Chris Cotter. He enjoys commercial fishing, but his deepening relationship with Chris (coupled with her reluctance to let him sail again) creates conflict within himself and between the couple. Yet, he is compelled by the potential to earn more money at sea than he could make with a job on shore to sign on for one last trip.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Mary_Elizabeth_Mastrantonio" title="Mary Elizabeth Mastrantonio">Mary Elizabeth Mastrantonio</a> as <a href="/wiki/Linda_Greenlaw" title="Linda Greenlaw">Linda Greenlaw</a>, the captain of the <i>Hannah Boden</i>. Linda and Billy both captain ships for the same owner and maintain a friendly rivalry. She is concerned about Billy and his crew\'s going out in what she considers dangerous weather. Linda is the last to speak to the <i>Andrea Gail</i>.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Diane_Lane" title="Diane Lane">Diane Lane</a> as Christina "Chris" Cotter, girlfriend of Bobby Shatford. She does not want Bobby to go on the trip because of a bad feeling she has about it. She spends her time during the last fishing trip decorating an apartment she has rented as a surprise for Bobby to symbolize her commitment to him.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/John_C._Reilly" title="John C. Reilly">John C. Reilly</a> as Dale "Murph" Murphy, crewmember on the <i>Andrea Gail</i>. Murph is a veteran fisherman who is divorced with a son with whom he\'s very involved. Murph has a rocky relationship with crewmember David "Sully" Sullivan that is eventually resolved during the trip.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/William_Fichtner" title="William Fichtner">William Fichtner</a> as David "Sully" Sullivan, crewmember on the <i>Andrea Gail</i>. He signed on for the trip at the last minute when another fisherman suddenly backed out. Although Brown seems to harbor a deep-seated recognition of Tyne\'s skills at catching fish, he nevertheless pressures Tyne over the latter\'s recent inability to bring in larger hauls, resulting in an uneasy relationship between the two.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Bob_Gunton" title="Bob Gunton">Bob Gunton</a> as Alexander McAnally III, owner of the <i>Mistral</i>, a yacht caught in the storm.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Karen_Allen" title="Karen Allen">Karen Allen</a> as Melissa Brown, crewmember on the <i>Mistral</i>.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Cherry_Jones" title="Cherry Jones">Cherry Jones</a> as Edie Bailey, crewmember on the <i>Mistral</i>.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Allen_Payne" title="Allen Payne">Allen Payne</a> as Alfred Pierre, one of the crew of the <i>Andrea Gail</i>.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/John_Hawkes_(actor)" title="John Hawkes (actor)">John Hawkes</a> as Michael "Bugsy" Moran, a member of the <i>Andrea Gail</i> crew. National Guard</a> rescue helicopter.</li>\n</ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_accuracy">Historical accuracy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Perfect_Storm_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Historical accuracy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Andrea_Gail">The Andrea Gail</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Perfect_Storm_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The Andrea Gail">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Grace_(1991).JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/Hurricane_Grace_%281991%29.JPG/220px-Hurricane_Grace_%281991%29.JPG" width="220" height="242" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/ff/Hurricane_Grace_%281991%29.JPG 1.5x" data-file-width="266" data-file-height="292" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Grace_(1991).JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nHurricane Grace on October 28, 1991, when the Andrea Gail went missing.</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>A ship similar to the <i>Andrea Gail</i>, the <i>Lady Grace</i>, was used during the filming of the movie.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Most of the names used were not changed for the fictional film, but in response two <a href="/wiki/The_Perfect_Storm_(film)#Lawsuits" title="The Perfect Storm (film)">lawsuits</a> were later filed by certain families of the crew members. The film only claims to be "based on a true story", and differs in many ways from the <a href="/wiki/The_Perfect_Storm_(book)" title="The Perfect Storm (book)">book</a> starting with the fictionalization of the material into a "story". The events shown in the film after the Andrea Gail\'s last radio contact are pure speculation, as the boat and the bodies of the crew were never found.</p>\n<p>Contrary to the movie\'s storyline, Captain Linda Greenlaw says she did not place a distress call on behalf of the <i>Andrea Gail</i>. "Without a distress call (directly) from the imperiled vessel, the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Coast_Guard" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Coast Guard">Coast Guard</a> will not initiate a search until the vessel is five days overdue in port," Greenlaw said. The 1993 U.S. Coast Guard\'s investigative report said that the <i>Andrea Gail</i> was experiencing 30-foot waves and winds from anywhere from 50 to 80 knots <small>(58 - 92 MPH)</small> around the time of the last communication. The conditions, though threatening, were probably not unfamiliar to Tyne, who had been a successful fisherman for about a decade on other vessels, taking trips to the Grand Banks and fishing off Florida, the Carolinas, and elsewhere.<sup id="cite_ref-GAIL_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GAIL-5">[5]</a></sup> In addition, <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Grace_(1991)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hurricane Grace (1991)">Hurricane Grace (1991)</a> was described by a weather forecaster as a <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">category 5</a> storm in the movie, though it never achieved those wind speeds. In the movie, Tyne and his crew agreed to head into the dangerous storm in order to save their fish from spoiling. Greenlaw acknowledged that Tyne did mention having ice problems, but that was not unusual. "My one gripe about [the] movie was how Warner Brothers depicted Billy Tyne and his crew as making a very conscious decision to steam into a storm that they knew was dangerous," said Greenlaw. "That is not what happened. The <i>Andrea Gail</i> was three days into their steam home when the storm hit. Whatever happened to the <i>Andrea Gail</i> happened very quickly."<sup id="cite_ref-GAIL_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GAIL-5">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<p>An Air National Guard helicopter was dispatched from <a href="/wiki/Francis_S._Gabreski_Air_National_Guard_Base" title="Francis S. Gabreski Air National Guard Base">Francis S. Gabreski Air National Guard Base</a> on <a href="/wiki/Long_Island,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Long Island, New York">Long Island, New York</a>, but not in response to the Andrea Gail or Satori (<i>Mistral</i> in the movie). The helicopter departed mid-storm on a mission to help save a lone Japanese fisherman from a sinking sailboat 250 miles off the New Jersey coast. Unsuccessful and running low on fuel, the Air National Guard HH-60G helicopter was compelled to attempt a mid-flight refueling maneuver. The zero-visibility conditions thwarted their efforts, however, and lacking enough fuel to make the flight back to the Long Island base, the crew were forced to ditch the helicopter. After a heroic search operation by the <i>Tamaroa</i>, four of the HH-60\'s crew were picked up; one was never seen again. The Japanese yachtsman was later rescued by a Romanian cargo ship.<sup id="cite_ref-GAIL_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GAIL-5">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<p>When asked about the portrayal of "Sully" in the movie, Cathy Sullivan Mustone, an older sister of David "Sully" Sullivan, said she was disappointed. "They made my brother\'s character out to be a hothead," she said. "I guess every movie needs a villain, but my brother was a funny guy with a loud laugh and a big smile. He had a lot of guts and he loved fishing." In fact, David\'s bravery and quick thinking made headlines on a different boat\xe2\x80\x94the <i>Harmony</i>. One night during a winter fishing trip, the <i>Harmony</i> began taking on water while tied to another boat. The crew of the <i>Harmony</i> yelled for help, hoping to wake the nearby crew. No one woke, so David dove into the icy water, pulling himself on the ropes that tied the boats together. As a result of his bravery, the <i>Harmony\'s</i> crew was saved. Mustone said, "At least in the movie, they did represent my brother\'s bravery in a water rescue scene. He was a good man. And I just know he is at peace in heaven, safe with our Dad."<sup id="cite_ref-GAIL_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GAIL-5">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Satori">The Satori</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Perfect_Storm_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The Satori">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The crew members of <i>Satori</i> (renamed <i>Mistral</i> in the movie) were not rescued by an <a href="/wiki/Air_National_Guard" title="Air National Guard">Air National Guard</a> helicopter, but rather a <a href="/wiki/U.S._Coast_Guard" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Coast Guard">U.S. Coast Guard</a> helicopter. The helicopter was changed in the film after the Air National Guard had issues consulting with the movie producers. According to the owner\'s son, the <i>Satori</i> never made a 360\xc2\xb0 roll, although it had two knockdowns, during which it lay on its side for about 30 seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-Satori_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Satori-6">[6]</a></sup> In response to requests by the crew, Captain Ray Leonard permitted the two crewmembers to make a position report over radio, during which they made an unauthorized Mayday call. One of those crewmembers reported that she was so convinced that she was going to die that she wrote her name down and put it into a plastic bag so that her body could be identified when it was finally found.</p>\n<p>There is controversy over whether the Captain was drunk, as charged by the women in the book, with Leonard objecting to this characterization. Out of compassion for the expected loss of his boat, the Coast Guard did not test his blood alcohol levels at the time. The Coast Guard declared the voyage manifestly unsafe and ordered everyone off-board, including the unwilling skipper.<sup id="cite_ref-Satori_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Satori-6">[6]</a></sup> The Coast Guard first tried to take them on board via an inflatable boat, but after it was damaged when trying to approach the <i>Satori</i> they sent a helicopter, which is a much riskier approach, as a rescue swimmer must jump into dangerous seas. The Coast Guard helicopter did not try to lower rescue gear onto the yacht (as shown in the movie, where it gets entangled with the mast), but rather asked the crew of the <i>Satori</i> to jump overboard to meet a rescue swimmer in the water. Leonard eventually complied with the request.</p>\n<p>After the storm, Leonard searched for the "Satori", hoping to find her still afloat, but in spite of his attempts she was found a few days later washed ashore on a Maryland beach, a bag of personal belongings still on deck. Leonard paid for a 60-foot fishing vessel to drag her off the beach, helped by a channel dug by Park Rangers who had been guarding the boat. He continued to sail the boat until 2000, and she remained in use with a new owner as of May 2000<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=The_Perfect_Storm_(film)&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>.<sup id="cite_ref-Satori_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Satori-6">[6]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Perfect_Storm_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><i>The Perfect Storm</i> received mixed reviews from critics, with a 47% approval rating on critic site <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> with a consensus of, "While the special effects are well done and quite impressive, this film suffers a lack of any actual drama or characterization. The end result is a film that offers nifty eye-candy and nothing else."<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>\n<p><i>The Perfect Storm</i> was a huge box office success. On its opening weekend, the film debuted with $42 million ahead of Sony\'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Patriot_(2000_film)" title="The Patriot (2000 film)">The Patriot</a></i> and eventually brought in over $182.6 million in the United States, and $146.1 million around the world to a total of $328.7 million worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The film was nominated for two <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Academy Award">Academy Awards</a>, Best Visual Effects (<a href="/wiki/Walt_Conti" title="Walt Conti">Walt Conti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stefen_Fangmeier" title="Stefen Fangmeier">Stefen Fangmeier</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Frazier" title="John Frazier">John Frazier</a> and <a href="/wiki/Habib_Zargarpour" title="Habib Zargarpour">Habib Zargarpour</a>) and Best Sound (<a href="/wiki/John_T._Reitz" title="John T. Reitz">John T. Reitz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gregg_Rudloff" title="Gregg Rudloff">Gregg Rudloff</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_E._Campbell_(sound_engineer)" title="David E. Campbell (sound engineer)">David E. Campbell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Keith_A._Wester" title="Keith A. Wester">Keith A. Wester</a>), but lost both to <i><a href="/wiki/Gladiator_(2000_film)" title="Gladiator (2000 film)">Gladiator</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Oscars2001_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Oscars2001-9">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lawsuits">Lawsuits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Perfect_Storm_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Lawsuits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>After the film was released, the families of two crew members sued the film makers for the fictionalization of events which happened prior to the loss of the <i>Andrea Gail</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In 2005, the <a href="/wiki/Florida_Supreme_Court" class="mw-redirect" title="Florida Supreme Court">Florida Supreme Court</a> ruled against the family of Captain Tyne by a 6\xe2\x80\x932 vote. Some unnamed families also sued the producers in <a href="/wiki/United_States_District_Court_for_the_Middle_District_of_Florida" title="United States District Court for the Middle District of Florida">federal district court</a>, claiming that their names were used without their permission, and that facts were changed.<sup id="cite_ref-ccase_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ccase-11">[11]</a></sup> The district court, which is also located in Florida, dismissed the case, as in their opinion the defendants\' <a href="/wiki/First_Amendment" class="mw-redirect" title="First Amendment">First Amendment</a> right to <a href="/wiki/Freedom_of_speech" title="Freedom of speech">freedom of speech</a> barred the suit. The plaintiffs appealed to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Court_of_Appeals_for_the_Eleventh_Circuit" title="United States Court of Appeals for the Eleventh Circuit">U.S. Court of Appeals for the 11th Circuit</a>, which could not decide how to interpret the Florida law at issue and certified the question to the Florida Supreme Court. In the end, the Florida Supreme Court upheld the district court\'s interpretation of Florida law, and remanded the case to the 11th Circuit, which affirmed the prior decision to dismiss the case.<sup id="cite_ref-ccase_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ccase-11">[11]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
