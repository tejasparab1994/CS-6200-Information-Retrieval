href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/\xcb\x88/: primary stress follows">\xcb\x88</span><span title="\'n\' in \'nigh\'">n</span><span title="/o\xca\x8a/: \'o\' in \'code\'">o\xca\x8a</span><span title="/./: syllable break">.</span><span title="/\xc9\x99/: \'a\' in \'about\'">\xc9\x99</span></span>/</a></span></span>, like "<a href="/wiki/Noah_(name)" title="Noah (name)">Noah</a>") is an American scientific agency within the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Commerce" title="United States Department of Commerce">United States Department of Commerce</a> that focuses on the conditions of the <a href="/wiki/Oceans" class="mw-redirect" title="Oceans">oceans</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Earth%27s_atmosphere" class="mw-redirect" title="Earth\'s atmosphere">atmosphere</a>. NOAA warns of dangerous <a href="/wiki/Weather" title="Weather">weather</a>, charts seas, guides the use and protection of ocean and coastal resources and conducts research to provide understanding and improve stewardship of the <a href="/wiki/Environment_(biophysical)" title="Environment (biophysical)">environment</a>. In addition to its over 11,000 civilian employees,<sup id="cite_ref-BestPlacesToWork.org_Agency_Report_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BestPlacesToWork.org_Agency_Report-3">[3]</a></sup> NOAA research and operations are supported by 321 <a href="/wiki/Uniformed_services_of_the_United_States" title="Uniformed services of the United States">uniformed service</a> members who make up the <a href="/wiki/NOAA_Commissioned_Officer_Corps" title="NOAA Commissioned Officer Corps">NOAA Commissioned Officer Corps</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> NOAA traces its history back to the convergence of multiple agencies: The United States Coastal and Geodetic Survey (founded in 1807), the Weather Bureau (1870) and the Bureau of Commercial Fisheries (1871). NOAA was officially formed in 1970.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The acting <a href="/wiki/Under_Secretary_of_Commerce_for_Oceans_and_Atmosphere" title="Under Secretary of Commerce for Oceans and Atmosphere">Under Secretary of Commerce for Oceans and Atmosphere</a> at the Department of Commerce and the agency\'s interim administrator has been Supplier of Environmental Information Products</i>. NOAA supplies to its customers and partners information pertaining to the state of the oceans and the atmosphere. This is clear through the production of weather warnings and forecasts via the <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">National Weather Service</a>, but NOAA\'s information products extend to climate, ecosystems and commerce as well.</li>\n<li><i>A Provider of Environmental Stewardship Services</i>. NOAA is a steward of U.S. coastal and marine environments. In coordination with federal, state, local, tribal and international authorities, NOAA manages the use of these environments, regulating fisheries and marine sanctuaries as well as protecting threatened and endangered marine species.</li>\n<li><i>A Leader in Applied Scientific Research</i>. NOAA is intended to be a source of accurate and objective scientific information in the four particular areas of national and global importance identified above: ecosystems, climate, weather and water, and commerce and transportation.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>\n</ul>\n<p>The five "fundamental activities" are:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>Monitoring and observing Earth systems with instruments and data collection networks.</li>\n<li>Understanding and describing Earth systems through research and analysis of that data.</li>\n<li>Assessing and predicting the changes of these systems over time.</li>\n<li>Engaging, advising, and informing the public and partner organizations with important information.</li>\n<li>Managing resources for the The NMFS operates six fisheries science centers throughout the United States, which are the sites of research and management on marine resources. The NMFS also operates the <a href="/wiki/National_Oceanic_and_Atmospheric_Administration_Fisheries_Office_for_Law_Enforcement" title="National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration Fisheries Office for Law Enforcement">National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration Fisheries Office of Law Enforcement</a> in <a href="/wiki/Silver_Spring,_Maryland" title="Silver Spring, Maryland">Silver Spring, Maryland</a>, which is the primary site of marine resource law enforcement.</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Office_of_Oceanic_and_Atmospheric_Research">Office of Oceanic and Atmospheric Research</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Oceanic_and_Atmospheric_Administration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Office of Oceanic and Atmospheric Research">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Office_of_Oceanic_and_Atmospheric_Research" title="Office of Oceanic and Atmospheric Research">Office of Oceanic and Atmospheric Research</a></div>\n<p>NOAA\'s research, conducted through the Office of Oceanic and Atmospheric Research (OAR), is the driving force behind NOAA environmental products and services that protect life and property and promote economic growth. Research, conducted in OAR laboratories and by extramural programs, focuses on enhancing our understanding of environmental phenomena such as tornadoes, hurricanes, climate variability, solar flares, changes in the ozone, <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_dispersion_modeling" title="Atmospheric dispersion modeling">air pollution transport and dispersion</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> El Ni\xc3\xb1o/La Ni\xc3\xb1a events, fisheries productivity, ocean currents, deep sea thermal vents, and coastal ecosystem health. NOAA research also develops innovative technologies and observing systems.</p>\n<p>The NOAA Research network consists of seven internal research laboratories, extramural research at 30 <a href="/wiki/Sea_Grant_Colleges" class="mw-redirect" title="Sea Grant Colleges">Sea Grant</a> university and research programs, six undersea research centers, a research grants program through the Climate Program Office, and 13 cooperative institutes with academia. Through NOAA and its academic partners, thousands of scientists, engineers, technicians, and graduate students participate in furthering our knowledge of natural phenomena that affect the lives of us all.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Air_Resources_Laboratory" title="Air Resources Laboratory">Air Resources Laboratory</a> (ARL) is one of the laboratories in the Office of Oceanic and Atmospheric Research. It studies processes and develops models relating to climate and air quality, including the transport, <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_dispersion_modeling" title="Atmospheric dispersion modeling">dispersion</a>, transformation and removal of <a href="/wiki/Pollutants" class="mw-redirect" title="Pollutants">pollutants</a> from the ambient atmosphere. The emphasis of the ARL\'s work is on data interpretation, technology development and transfer. The specific goal of ARL research is to improve and eventually to institutionalize prediction of trends, dispersion of <a href="/wiki/Air_pollution_dispersion_terminology" class="mw-redirect" title="Air pollution dispersion terminology">air pollutant plumes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Air_quality" class="mw-redirect" title="Air quality">air quality</a>, atmospheric deposition, and related variables.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Oceanographic_and_Meteorological_Laboratory" title="Atlantic Oceanographic and Meteorological Laboratory">Atlantic Oceanographic and Meteorological Laboratory</a> (AOML), is part of NOAA\'s Office of Oceanic and Atmospheric Research, located in <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>. AOML\'s research spans hurricanes, coastal ecosystems, oceans and human health, climate studies, global carbon systems, and ocean observations. AOML\'s organizational structure consists of an Office of the Director and three scientific research divisions (Physical Oceanography, Ocean Chemistry and Ecosystems, and Hurricane Research). The Office of the Director oversees the Laboratory\'s scientific programs, as well as its financial, administrative, computer, outreach/education, and facility management services. Research programs are augmented by the Cooperative Institute for Marine and Atmospheric Studies (CIMAS), a joint enterprise with the University of Miami\'s Rosenstiel School of Marine and Atmospheric Science. CIMAS enables AOML and university scientists to collaborate on research areas of mutual interest and facilitates the participation of students and visiting scientists. AOML is a member of a unique community of marine research and educational institutions located on Virginia Key in Miami, Florida.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>\n<p>In 1977 the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Marine_Environmental_Laboratory" title="Pacific Marine Environmental Laboratory">Pacific Marine Environmental Laboratory</a> (PMEL) deployed the first successful moored equatorial current meter \xe2\x80\x93 the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Atmosphere_Ocean_project" title="Tropical Atmosphere Ocean project">Tropical Atmosphere Ocean</a>, TAO, array. In 1984 the <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Ocean-Global_Atmosphere_program" class="mw-redirect" title="Tropical Ocean-Global Atmosphere program">Tropical Ocean-Global Atmosphere program</a> (TOGA) program began.</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Office_of_Marine_and_Aviation_Operations_and_the_Commissioned_Officer_Corp">Office of Marine and Aviation Operations and the Commissioned Officer Corp</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Oceanic_and_Atmospheric_Administration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Office of Marine and Aviation Operations and the Commissioned Officer Corp">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The Office of Marine and Aviation Operations is responsible for the fleet of NOAA ships, aircraft, and diving operations. It has the largest research fleet of the Federal government. Its personnel is made up of civilians and the <a href="/wiki/NOAA_Commissioned_Officer_Corps" title="NOAA Commissioned Officer Corps">NOAA Commissioned Officer Corps</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> It is headed by the <a href="/wiki/Rear_admiral_(United_States)" title="Rear admiral (United States)">Rear Admiral</a>, who also commands the Corps.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Geodetic_Survey">National Geodetic Survey</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Oceanic_and_Atmospheric_Administration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: National Geodetic Survey">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/National_Geodetic_Survey" class="mw-redirect" title="National Geodetic Survey">National Geodetic Survey</a> (NGS) is the primary surveying organization in the United States.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Integrated_Drought_Information_System">National Integrated Drought Information System</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Oceanic_and_Atmospheric_Administration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: National Integrated Drought Information System">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/National_Integrated_Drought_Information_System" title="National Integrated Drought Information System">National Integrated Drought Information System</a> is a program within NOAA with an interagency mandate to coordinate and integrate drought research, building upon existing federal, tribal, state, and local partnerships in support of creating a national drought early warning information system.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Office_of_Program_Planning_and_Integration">Office of Program Planning and Integration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Oceanic_and_Atmospheric_Administration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Office of Program Planning and Integration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The Office of Program Planning and Integration (PPI) was established in June 2002 as the focus for a new corporate management culture at NOAA. PPI was created to address the needs to foster strategic management among NOAA Line and Staff Offices, Goal Teams, Programs, and Councils, support planning activities through greater opportunities for active participation of employees, stakeholders, and partners, build decision support systems based on the goals and outcomes set in NOAA\'s strategic plan, and guide managers and employees on program and performance management, the <a href="/wiki/National_Environmental_Policy_Act" title="National Environmental Policy Act">National Environmental Policy Act</a>, and socioeconomic analysis.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Intergovernmental_Panel_on_Climate_Change">Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change</span><span predecessor organizations, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_and_Geodetic_Survey" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Coast and Geodetic Survey">United States Coast and Geodetic Survey</a>. The Coast and Geodetic Survey\'s flag, authorized in 1899 and in use until 1970, was blue, with a white circle centered in it and a red triangle centered within the circle. It symbolized the use of <a href="/wiki/Triangulation" title="Triangulation">triangulation</a> in <a href="/wiki/Surveying" title="Surveying">surveying</a>, and was flown by ships of the Survey.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>\n<p>When NOAA was established in 1970 and the Coast and Geodetic Survey\'s assets became a part of NOAA, NOAA based its own flag on that of the Coast and Geodetic Survey. The NOAA flag is in essence the Coast and Geodetic Survey flag, with the NOAA logo\xe2\x80\x94a circle divided by the silhouette of a seabird into an upper dark blue and a lower light blue section, but with the "NOAA" legend omitted\xe2\x80\x94centered within the red triangle. NOAA ships in <a href="/wiki/Ship_commissioning" title="Ship commissioning">commission</a> display the NOAA flag; those with only one <a href="/wiki/Mast_(sailing)" title="Mast (sailing)">mast</a> fly it immediately beneath the ship\'s <a href="/wiki/Commissioning_pennant" class="mw-redirect" title="Commissioning pennant">commissioning pennant</a> or the personal flag of a civilian official or <a href="/wiki/Flag_officer" title="Flag officer">flag officer</a> if one is aboard the ship, while multimasted vessels fly it at the masthead of the forwardmost mast.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> NOAA ships fly the same <a href="/wiki/Ensign" title="Ensign">ensign</a> as <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States
