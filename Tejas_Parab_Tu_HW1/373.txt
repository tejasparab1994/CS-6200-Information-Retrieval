class="mw-body-content">\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<div id="contentSub"></div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">\n\t\t\t\t\tJump to:\t\t\t\t\t<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, \t\t\t\t\t<a href="#p-search">search</a>\n\t\t\t\t</div>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>Azores High</b> (<a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>: <span lang="pt" xml:lang="pt"><i>Anticiclone dos A\xc3\xa7ores</i></span>) also known as <b>North Atlantic (Subtropical) High/Anticyclone</b> or the <b>Bermuda-Azores High</b>, is a large <a href="/wiki/Subtropics" title="Subtropics">subtropical</a> semi-permanent centre of <a href="/wiki/High-pressure_area" title="High-pressure area">high atmospheric pressure</a> typically found south of the <a href="/wiki/Azores" title="Azores">Azores</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>, at the <a href="/wiki/Horse_latitudes" title="Horse latitudes">Horse latitudes</a>. It forms one pole of the <a href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_oscillation" title="North Atlantic oscillation">North Atlantic oscillation</a>, the other being the <a href="/wiki/Icelandic_Low" title="Icelandic Low">Icelandic Low</a>. The system influences the <a href="/wiki/Weather" title="Weather">weather</a> and <a href="/wiki/Climate" title="Climate">climatic</a> patterns of vast areas of <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a> and southern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, and to a lesser extent, eastern <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>. The aridity of the <a href="/wiki/Sahara_Desert" class="mw-redirect" title="Sahara Desert">Sahara Desert</a> and the summer drought of the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Basin" title="Mediterranean Basin">Mediterranean Basin</a> is due to the large-scale <a href="/wiki/Subsidence_(atmosphere)" title="Subsidence (atmosphere)">subsidence</a> and sinking motion of air in the system. In its summer position (the Bermuda High), the high is centered near Bermuda, and creates a southwest flow of warm tropical air toward the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="East Coast of the United States">East Coast of the United States</a>. In summer, the Azores-Bermuda High is strongest. The <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" title="Atmospheric pressure">central pressure</a> hovers around 1024 <a href="/wiki/Mbar" class="mw-redirect" title="Mbar">mbar</a> (<a href="/wiki/HPa" class="mw-redirect" title="HPa">hPa</a>).</p>\n<div class="center">\n<div class="thumb tnone">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:702px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Tropical_waves.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Tropical_waves.jpg/700px-Tropical_waves.jpg" width="700" height="278" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Tropical_waves.jpg/1050px-Tropical_waves.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Tropical_waves.jpg/1400px-Tropical_waves.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2374" data-file-height="944" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Tropical_waves.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nTropical wave formation on the Atlantic Ocean.</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>This high-pressure <a href="/wiki/Block_(meteorology)" title="Block (meteorology)">block</a> exhibits <a href="/wiki/Anticyclone" title="Anticyclone">anticyclonic</a> nature, circulating the air <a href="/wiki/Clockwise" title="Clockwise">clockwise</a>. Due to this direction of movement, <a href="/wiki/Tropical_waves" class="mw-redirect" title="Tropical waves">African eastern waves</a> are impelled along the southern periphery of the Azores High away from coastal <a href="/wiki/West_Africa" title="West Africa">West Africa</a> towards the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a>, or the <a href="/wiki/Bahamas" class="mw-redirect" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a>, favouring <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclogenesis" title="Tropical cyclogenesis">tropical cyclogenesis</a>, especially during the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Atlantic_hurricane_seasons" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Atlantic hurricane seasons">hurricane season</a>.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Variations">Variations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Azores_High&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Variations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_Oscillation" class="mw-redirect" title="North Atlantic Oscillation">North Atlantic Oscillation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tropical_upper_tropospheric_trough" title="Tropical upper tropospheric trough">Tropical upper tropospheric trough</a></div>\n<p>Research into <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">global warming</a> suggests that it may be intensifying the Bermuda High in some years, independently of oscillations such as <a href="/wiki/ENSO" class="mw-redirect" title="ENSO">ENSO</a>, leading to more precipitation extremes across the <a href="/wiki/Southeastern_United_States" title="Southeastern United States">Southeastern United States</a>. Latitudinal displacement of the ridge is also occurring, and <a href="/wiki/Computer_model" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer model">computer models</a> depict more westward expansion of the anticyclone in the future.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> However, during the <a href="/wiki/Global_storm_activity_of_early_2010" class="mw-redirect" title="Global storm activity of early 2010">winter of 2009\xe2\x80\x932010</a>, the Azores High was smaller, displaced to the northeast and weaker than usual, allowing <a href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperature" title="Sea surface temperature">sea surface temperatures</a> in the Central Atlantic to increase quickly.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
