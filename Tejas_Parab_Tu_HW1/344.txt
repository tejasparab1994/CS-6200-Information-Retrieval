colspan="2"></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Typhoon Paka</b>, known in the Philippines as <b>Typhoon Rubing</b>, was the last <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> of the 1997 <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a> <a href="/wiki/1997_Pacific_hurricane_season" title="1997 Pacific hurricane season">hurricane</a> and <a href="/wiki/1997_Pacific_typhoon_season" title="1997 Pacific typhoon season">typhoon</a> season, and was among the strongest Pacific typhoons in the month of December.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Paka, which is the <a href="/wiki/Hawaiian_language" title="Hawaiian language">Hawaiian</a> name for Pat,<sup id="cite_ref-cphc_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cphc-2">[2]</a></sup> developed on November 28 from a <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a> well to the southwest of <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a>. The storm tracked generally westward for much of its duration, and on December 7 it crossed into the western Pacific Ocean. Much of its track was characterized by fluctuations in intensity, and on December 10 the cyclone attained typhoon status as it crossed the <a href="/wiki/Marshall_Islands" title="Marshall Islands">Marshall Islands</a>. On December 16, Paka struck <a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">Guam</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rota_(island)" title="Rota (island)">Rota</a> with winds of 230&#160;km/h (145&#160;mph), and it strengthened further to reach peak winds on December 18 over open waters. Subsequently, it underwent a steady weakening trend, and on December 23 Paka dissipated.</p>\n<p>Typhoon Paka first impacted the Marshall Islands, where it dropped heavy rainfall and left <span style="display:none" class="sortkey">7007800000000000000\xe2\x99\xa0</span><a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>80&#160;million in damages. Later, it passed just north of Guam, where strong winds destroyed about 1,500&#160;buildings and damaged 10,000&#160;more; 5,000&#160;people were left homeless, and the island experienced a complete power outage following the typhoon. Damage on the island totaled <span style="display:none" class="sortkey">7008500000000000000\xe2\x99\xa0</span><a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>500&#160;million, which warranted the <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Pacific_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Pacific hurricane names">retirement of its name</a>. Paka also caused minor damage in the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Mariana_Islands" title="Northern Mariana Islands">Northern Mariana Islands</a>, and overall the typhoon caused no reported fatalities.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Meteorological_history"><span id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Paka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Paka_1997_track.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Paka_1997_track.png/220px-Paka_1997_track.png" width="220" height="136" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Paka_1997_track.png/330px-Paka_1997_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Paka_1997_track.png/440px-Paka_1997_track.png 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="633" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Paka_1997_track.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nMap plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>As the weather synoptics of the northern <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a> transitioned into a late-fall to early winter-type pattern, <a href="/wiki/Convection" title="Convection">convection</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Monsoon_trough" title="Monsoon trough">monsoon trough</a> extended to the east of the <a href="/wiki/International_Date_Line" title="International Date Line">International Date Line</a>. During late November, a westerly disturbance developed into twin <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">troughs</a> on opposite sides of the <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">equator</a>; the one in the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Hemisphere" title="Southern Hemisphere">Southern Hemisphere</a> ultimately developed into <a href="/wiki/1997%E2%80%9398_South_Pacific_cyclone_season#Tropical_Cyclone_Pam" title="1997\xe2\x80\x9398 South Pacific cyclone season">Tropical Cyclone Pam</a>, while the one in the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Hemisphere" title="Northern Hemisphere">Northern Hemisphere</a> formed into an area of convection about 2000&#160;km (1240&#160;mi) southwest of <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a>. The disturbance gradually organized as it drifted north-northeastward, and on November 28 it developed into Tropical Depression Five-C about 465&#160;km (290&#160;mi) west-northwest of <a href="/wiki/Palmyra_Atoll" title="Palmyra Atoll">Palmyra Atoll</a>. Operationally, the <a href="/wiki/Central_Pacific_Hurricane_Center" title="Central Pacific Hurricane Center">Central Pacific Hurricane Center</a> (CPHC) did not begin issuing warnings on the system until December 2.<sup id="cite_ref-cphc_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cphc-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-summary_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-summary-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pakatcr_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pakatcr-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-epachurdat_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-epachurdat-5">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The tropical depression continued drifting north-northeastward, and failed to strengthen significantly. It turned to the west on December 1, due to the presence of a strong <a href="/wiki/Anticyclone" title="Anticyclone">anticyclone</a> to its north with a <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">ridge</a> extending westward past the <a href="/wiki/International_Date_Line" title="International Date Line">International Date Line</a>. On December 2, based on satellite estimates, the CPHC upgraded the depression to Tropical Storm Paka while located about 1000&#160;km (625&#160;mi) south-southeast of <a href="/wiki/Johnston_Atoll" title="Johnston Atoll">Johnston Atoll</a>. Due to the presence of high <a href="/wiki/Cloud" title="Cloud">clouds</a> across the area, forecasters had difficulty at times in locating the low-level circulation. After becoming a tropical storm, Paka remained nearly stationary for about two days before resuming a slow motion to the west-southwest. It steadily intensified due to warm water temperatures, and on December 3 the storm attained winds of 105&#160;km/h (65&#160;mph). The next day, however, it encountered dry air and began weakening; by December 6, the winds had decreased to minimal tropical storm status for about 12&#160;hours. Subsequently, Paka began to re-intensify, and on December 7 the storm crossed the <a href="/wiki/International_Date_Line" title="International Date Line">International Date Line</a> into the western North Pacific Ocean with winds of 80&#160;km/h (50&#160;mph).<sup id="cite_ref-cphc_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cphc-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-epachurdat_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-epachurdat-5">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Upon entering the western North Pacific Ocean, tropical cyclone warning duties transferred from the CPHC to the <a href="/wiki/Japan_Meteorological_Agency" title="Japan Meteorological Agency">Japan Meteorological Agency</a> (JMA), and the JMA first assessed Paka as a 65&#160;km (40&#160;mph) storm.<sup id="cite_ref-jma_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jma-6">[6]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Joint_Typhoon_Warning_Center" title="Joint Typhoon Warning Center">Joint Typhoon Warning Center</a> (JTWC) unofficially assumed warning duties for interests in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Defense" title="United States Department of Defense">United States Department of Defense</a>. Paka continued to intensify after crossing the date line, and from late on December 7 through early the following day it remained a strong tropical storm. However, upper-level <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a> increased, and it again weakened. At 1200&#160;UTC on December 9, the JTWC assessed Paka as an 85&#160;km/h (50&#160;mph) tropical storm and forecast it to continue weakening. By December 10, the shear had begun to decrease as the storm moved through the <a href="/wiki/Marshall_Islands" title="Marshall Islands">Marshall Islands</a>, and that night the JTWC upgraded Paka to typhoon status.<sup id="cite_ref-pakatcr_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pakatcr-4">[4]</a></sup> Paka officially attained typhoon status when JMA classified it with winds of 120&#160;km/h (75&#160;mph) at 0000&#160;UTC on December 11.<sup id="cite_ref-jma_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jma-6">[6]</a></sup></p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Paka_15_dec_1997_0429Z.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Paka_15_dec_1997_0429Z.jpg/250px-Paka_15_dec_1997_0429Z.jpg" width="250" height="250" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Paka_15_dec_1997_0429Z.jpg/375px-Paka_15_dec_1997_0429Z.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Paka_15_dec_1997_0429Z.jpg/500px-Paka_15_dec_1997_0429Z.jpg 2x" data-file-width="580" data-file-height="580" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Paka_15_dec_1997_0429Z.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nSuper Typhoon Paka on December 15</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>After attaining typhoon status, Paka strengthened fairly quickly, and by December 12 it reached sustained winds of 150&#160;km/h (90&#160;mph) for a ten-minute (10 min) duration,<sup id="cite_ref-jma_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jma-6">[6]</a></sup> or 215&#160;km/h (135&#160;mph) over a one-minute (1 min) duration. Subsequently, it briefly weakened as its forward motion increased. However, Paka again re-intensified, and at 1200&#160;UTC on December 14 it attained the unofficial ranking of super typhoon status while over the open Pacific Ocean.<sup id="cite_ref-pakatcr_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pakatcr-4">[4]</a></sup> At the same time, the JMA classified it with 10 min sustained winds of 175&#160;km/h (110&#160;mph).<sup id="cite_ref-jma_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jma-6">[6]</a></sup> After reaching its initial peak intensity, Typhoon Paka began to weaken as it approached the southern <a href="/wiki/Mariana_Islands" title="Mariana Islands">Mariana Islands</a>; the <a href="/wiki/NEXRAD" title="NEXRAD">NEXRAD</a> Doppler <a href="/wiki/Weather_radar" title="Weather radar">weather radar</a> from <a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">Guam</a> revealed the presence of a primary <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eyewall</a> of 74&#160;km (46&#160;mi) in diameter, with a fragmented inner wall cloud of 19&#160;km (11&#160;mi) in diameter.<sup id="cite_ref-pakatcr_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pakatcr-4">[4]</a></sup> Additionally, satellite imagery indicated an <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eyewall mesovortex</a> within the eye of the typhoon.<sup id="cite_ref-gp1297_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp1297-7">[7]</a></sup> It slowed and began to re-intensify as it continued westward, and at 0530&#160;UTC on December 16 the northern portion of the outer eyewall of Paka passed over the island of <a href="/wiki/Rota_(island)" title="Rota (island)">Rota</a>; 20&#160;minutes later, the southern portion of the inner wall cloud moved across northern <a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">Guam</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pakatcr_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pakatcr-4">[4]</a></sup> As it tracked through the Rota Channel, the center of Paka passed about 8&#160;km (5&#160;mi) north of the northern tip of Guam, its closest approach to the island.<sup id="cite_ref-ncdc_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc-8">[8]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Typhoon Paka continued to steadily intensify after passing the Marianas Islands, and late on December 17 it reached its peak intensity of 185&#160;km/h (115&#160;mph <small>10 min sustained</small>) while located 440&#160;km (275&#160;mi) west-northwest of Guam.<sup id="cite_ref-jma_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jma-6">[6]</a></sup> Early on December 18, the JTWC assessed it as attaining peak winds of 295&#160;km/h (185&#160;mph <small>1 min winds</small>).<sup id="cite_ref-pakatcr_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pakatcr-4">[4]</a></sup> On December 19, it entered the area of responsibility of the <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Atmospheric,_Geophysical_and_Astronomical_Services_Administration" class="mw-redirect" title="Philippine Atmospheric, Geophysical and Astronomical Services Administration">Philippine Atmospheric, Geophysical and Astronomical Services Administration</a>, or PAGASA, and was named Rubing.<sup id="cite_ref-wmo905_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wmo905-9">[9]</a></sup> Shortly thereafter, Paka moved through an area of progressively increasing <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a>, which resulted in a steady weakening trend.<sup id="cite_ref-gp1297_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp1297-7">[7]</a></sup> By December 21, winds decreased to tropical storm status. The next day, it degenerated into a tropical depression before it dissipated on December 23.<sup id="cite_ref-jma_6-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jma-6">[6]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations_and_impact">Preparations and impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Paka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations and impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marshall_Islands">Marshall Islands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Paka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Marshall Islands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Prior to the typhoon passing through the <a href="/wiki/Marshall_Islands" title="Marshall Islands">Marshall Islands</a>, several hundred residents on the island of <a href="/wiki/Ebeye" class="mw-redirect" title="Ebeye">Ebeye</a> fled to safer structures. The threat of Paka prevented <a href="/wiki/Continental_Micronesia" title="Continental Micronesia">Continental Micronesia</a> from flying in or out of the area.<sup id="cite_ref-afp1212_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-afp1212-10">[10]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Paka entered the Marshall Islands between <a href="/wiki/Mili_Atoll" title="Mili Atoll">Mili</a> and <a href="/wiki/Majuro" title="Majuro">Majuro</a> as a tropical storm on December 10, and after strengthening into a typhoon, it left the archipelago on December 14.<sup id="cite_ref-ncdc_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc-8">[8]</a></sup> The cyclone affected several islands in the nation, and the <a href="/wiki/Majuro" title="Majuro">Majuro</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kwajalein" class="mw-redirect" title="Kwajalein">Kwajalein</a> atolls reported wind gusts in excess of 75&#160;km/h (45&#160;mph).<sup id="cite_ref-pakatcr_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pakatcr-4">[4]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/Jaluit_Atoll" title="Jaluit Atoll">Jaluit Atoll</a>, the typhoon dropped 217&#160;mm (8.54&#160;in) of precipitation in six hours, with a total of about 300&#160;mm (11.8&#160;in) recorded in 30&#160;hours.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Strong waves inundated low-lying islands, which flooded crops with salt water.<sup id="cite_ref-afp1212_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-afp1212-10">[10]</a></sup> The combination of the wind and flooding caused severe damage to banana, papaya, and lime trees across the territory.<sup id="cite_ref-ncdc_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc-8">[8]</a></sup> Typhoon Paka damaged 70% of the houses on <a href="/wiki/Ailinglaplap_Atoll" title="Ailinglaplap Atoll">Ailinglaplap Atoll</a>, and most of the coconut trees on the atoll were left toppled or damaged. Strong winds left large portions of <a href="/wiki/Ebeye" class="mw-redirect" title="Ebeye">Ebeye</a> island without electricity or telephone.<sup id="cite_ref-afp1212_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-afp1212-10">[10]</a></sup> The typhoon caused no reported deaths or injuries in the region, and damage was estimated at <span style="display:none" class="sortkey">7007800000000000000\xe2\x99\xa0</span><a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>80&#160;million.<sup id="cite_ref-ncdc_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc-8">[8]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Guam">Guam</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Paka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Guam">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Typhoon_Paka_-_Guam_Damage.gif" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Typhoon_Paka_-_Guam_Damage.gif/250px-Typhoon_Paka_-_Guam_Damage.gif" width="250" height="170" class="thumbimage" //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/19/Typhoon_Paka_-_Guam_Damage.gif 2x" data-file-width="419" data-file-height="285" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Typhoon_Paka_-_Guam_Damage.gif" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nDamage on Guam</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">Guam</a> <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">National Weather Service</a> issued a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">typhoon watch</a> on December 14, which was upgraded to a typhoon warning the next day. The <a href="/wiki/Antonio_B._Won_Pat_International_Airport" title="Antonio B. Won Pat International Airport">Antonio B. Won Pat International Airport</a> was closed during the passage of Paka, with only emergency flights permitted.<sup id="cite_ref-ncdc_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc-8">[8]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Passing a short distance north of the island, Typhoon Paka produced strong winds across northern <a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">Guam</a>, though reliable wind reports are incomplete due to the long duration and intensity of the winds. The highest reading believed to be reliable was at <a href="/wiki/Apra_Harbor" title="Apra Harbor">Apra Harbor</a>. There, a station recorded a wind gust of 277&#160;km/h (171&#160;mph) before the sensor failed as winds shifted to the southwest; since the winds from the southwest were stronger and of greater duration, officials believe gusts there reached 297&#160;km/h (184&#160;mph).<sup id="cite_ref-pakatcr_4-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pakatcr-4">[4]</a></sup> Additionally, <a href="/wiki/Andersen_Air_Force_Base" title="Andersen Air Force Base">Andersen Air Force Base</a> recorded a peak wind gust of 381&#160;km/h (236&#160;mph),<sup id="cite_ref-pakwinds_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pakwinds-12">[12]</a></sup> which at the time was considered the highest wind speed on record, surpassing the 1934 world record of 372&#160;km/h (231&#160;mph) on <a href="/wiki/Mount_Washington_(New_Hampshire)" title="Mount Washington (New Hampshire)">Mount Washington</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_Hampshire" title="New Hampshire">New Hampshire</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-afp1218_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-afp1218-13">[13]</a></sup> However, a subsequent wind survey of the area discarded the reading at the base, as it was considered unreliable.<sup id="cite_ref-pakwinds_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pakwinds-12">[12]</a></sup> As <a href="/wiki/Microbarograph" class="mw-redirect" title="Microbarograph">microbarographs</a> are less exposed than wind sensors, pressure readings on the island are considered accurate; the lowest reading on the island was 948&#160;hPa (27.99&#160;inHg) at Andersen Air Force Base.<sup id="cite_ref-pakatcr_4-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pakatcr-4">[4]</a></sup> In two days, the typhoon dropped about 533&#160;mm (21&#160;in) of precipitation on the northern portion of the island, or about 89% of the monthly rainfall total.<sup id="cite_ref-enso_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-enso-14">[14]</a></sup> Waves along northern Guam reached about 11&#160;m (35&#160;ft) in height.<sup id="cite_ref-eqe_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eqe-15">[15]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The strong winds from Paka left around 1,500&#160;buildings destroyed on the island,<sup id="cite_ref-eqe_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eqe-15">[15]</a></sup> of which 1,160 were single-family homes.<sup id="cite_ref-ncdc_8-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc-8">[8]</a></sup> A further 10,000&#160;buildings sustained damage to some degree,<sup id="cite_ref-eqe_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eqe-15">[15]</a></sup> with 60% of the homes on the island reporting major damage.<sup id="cite_ref-ncdc_8-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc-8">[8]</a></sup> In all, about 5,000&#160;people were left homeless due to the typhoon. Additionally, an estimated 30\xe2\x80\x9340% of the public buildings received major damage.<sup id="cite_ref-ncdc_8-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc-8">[8]</a></sup> Buildings on the island made of reinforced concrete fared well, as opposed to light metal-frame structures, which more often than not were destroyed. Large tourist hotels near <a href="/wiki/Hag%C3%A5t%C3%B1a,_Guam" class="mw-redirect" title="Hag\xc3\xa5t\xc3\xb1a, Guam">Hag\xc3\xa5t\xc3\xb1a</a>, on which Guam is dependent, received generally minor damage, such as broken windows and damaged power generators.<sup id="cite_ref-eqe_15-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eqe-15">[15]</a></sup></p>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:FEMA_-_1089_-_Photograph_by_David_Fowler_taken_on_12-17-1997_in_Guam.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/FEMA_-_1089_-_Photograph_by_David_Fowler_taken_on_12-17-1997_in_Guam.jpg/220px-FEMA_-_1089_-_Photograph_by_David_Fowler_taken_on_12-17-1997_in_Guam.jpg" width="220" height="126" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/0b/FEMA_-_1089_-_Photograph_by_David_Fowler_taken_on_12-17-1997_in_Guam.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="300" data-file-height="172" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:FEMA_-_1089_-_Photograph_by_David_Fowler_taken_on_12-17-1997_in_Guam.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nA damaged business at <a href="/wiki/Aga%C3%B1a" class="mw-redirect" title="Aga\xc3\xb1a">Aga\xc3\xb1a</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>A complete island-wide power outage followed the typhoon; damage to the main electrical transmission and distribution system was estimated at <span style="display:none" class="sortkey">7007160000000000000\xe2\x99\xa0</span>USD16&#160;million. Following the passage of the typhoon, 25% of the homes on Guam were left without water.<sup id="cite_ref-ncdc_8-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc-8">[8]</a></sup> Telephone service remained working after the storm, due to most lines being underground. Strong waves washed away a few coastal roads in the northern portion of the island, leaving them temporarily closed. The waves surpassed the seawall at <a href="/wiki/Apra_Harbor" title="Apra Harbor">Apra Harbor</a>, damaging the road and infrastructure of the seaport; many boats were washed ashore after breaking from their moorings. Strong winds damaged a radar system and lights along the runway of the <a href="/wiki/Antonio_B._Won_Pat_International_Airport" title="Antonio B. Won Pat International Airport">Antonio B. Won Pat International Airport</a>, though most airport facilities received light damage. <a href="/wiki/Andersen_Air_Force_Base" title="Andersen Air Force Base">Andersen Air Force Base</a> also sustained heavy damage, with hundreds of downed trees and many facilities left damaged.<sup id="cite_ref-eqe_15-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eqe-15">[15]</a></sup> Across Guam, damage was estimated at <span style="display:none" class="sortkey">7008500000000000000\xe2\x99\xa0</span>USD500&#160;million.<sup id="cite_ref-ncdc_8-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc-8">[8]</a></sup> About 100&#160;people were injured,<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-16">[16]</a></sup> but the typhoon caused no deaths on the island.<sup id="cite_ref-ncdc_8-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc-8">[8]</a></sup></p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a data-file-width="659" data-file-height="522" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Paka_NEXRAD.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\n<a href="/wiki/NEXRAD" title="NEXRAD">NEXRAD</a> image of Typhoon Paka from Guam</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Northern_Mariana_Islands">Northern Mariana Islands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Paka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Northern Mariana Islands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>A <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">typhoon watch</a> was issued for <a href="/wiki/Rota_(island)" title="Rota (island)">Rota</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tinian" title="Tinian">Tinian</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Saipan" title="Saipan">Saipan</a> on December 14, which was upgraded to a typhoon warning the next day.<sup id="cite_ref-ncdc_8-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc-8">[8]</a></sup> Because Paka was intensifying while passing to the south of <a href="/wiki/Rota_(island)" title="Rota (island)">Rota</a>, the first wind, or northeast through east winds, was less severe than the second wind from the southeast.<sup id="cite_ref-pakatcr_4-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pakatcr-4">[4]</a></sup> Sustained winds on the island reached 145&#160;km/h (90&#160;mph), with gusts reaching 185&#160;km/h (115&#160;mph).<sup id="cite_ref-ncdc_8-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc-8">[8]</a></sup> Many trees in the mountainous portion of the island were left defoliated, which limited nesting and foraging sites for the endangered <a href="/wiki/Bridled_white-eye" title="Bridled white-eye">bridled white-eye</a> bird.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> While passing to the south of the island, Paka dropped 250\xe2\x80\x93300&#160;mm (10\xe2\x80\x9312&#160;in) of rain.<sup id="cite_ref-enso_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-enso-14">[14]</a></sup> Damage on the island totaled $4.4&#160;million (1997&#160;USD, $6.4&#160;million 2015&#160;USD).<sup id="cite_ref-ncdc_8-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc-8">[8]</a></sup> The typhoon also produced above-normal precipitation on <a href="/wiki/Saipan" title="Saipan">Saipan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-enso_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-enso-14">[14]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Paka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Following the passage of the typhoon in the <a href="/wiki/Ailinglaplap_Atoll" title="Ailinglaplap Atoll">Ailinglaplap Atoll</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Marshall_Islands" title="Marshall Islands">Marshall Islands</a>, residents experienced severe food shortages due to damaged crops and little rainfall. Experts estimated the entire redevelopment of its fauna would require more than a decade. As a result of the crop shortage, large-scale evacuations of the islands\' residents were considered. Officials in the nation requested assistance from the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Emergency_Management_Agency" title="Federal Emergency Management Agency">Federal Emergency Management Agency</a> of the United States,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> and on March 20, 1998, the area was declared a disaster area; the declaration allowed for the usage of emergency funds.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>\n<p>On December 17, 1997, <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Bill_Clinton" title="Bill Clinton">Bill Clinton</a> declared <a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">Guam</a> a federal disaster area, making it eligible for federal assistance.<sup id="cite_ref-1217fem_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1217fem-20">[20]</a></sup> One week later, a disaster declaration was ordered for the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Mariana_Islands" title="Northern Mariana Islands">Northern Mariana Islands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1224fem_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1224fem-21">[21]</a></sup> Ultimately, <a href="/wiki/Federal_Emergency_Management_Agency" title="Federal Emergency Management Agency">FEMA</a> received 14,770&#160;Individual Assistance Applications from residents on Guam. In turn, FEMA provided the residents with over $27&#160;million in assistance (1997&#160;USD, $39&#160;million 2015&#160;USD).<sup id="cite_ref-fempongsona_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fempongsona-22">[22]</a></sup> The entire island of Guam was left without power after Paka. Water and sewage systems on Guam were directly affected minimally by the typhoon. With the usage of power generators, most areas of the island had <a href="/wiki/Water_pumping" title="Water pumping">water pumping</a> capabilities within a few days after the typhoon. The <a href="/wiki/Antonio_B._Won_Pat_International_Airport" title="Antonio B. Won Pat International Airport">Antonio B. Won Pat International Airport</a> was partially reopened to daytime flights a day after the typhoon, and by a week after the passage of Paka the airport was fully re-opened.<sup id="cite_ref-eqe_15-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eqe-15">[15]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement">Retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Paka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Retirement">edit</a><span
