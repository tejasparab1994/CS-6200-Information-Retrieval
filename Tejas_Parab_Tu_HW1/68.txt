Snow grains are characterized as very small (&lt;1&#160;mm), white, opaque grains of ice that are fairly flat or elongated. Unlike <a href="/wiki/Snow_pellets" class="mw-redirect" title="Snow pellets">snow pellets</a>, snow grains do not bounce or break up on impact. Usually, very small amounts fall, mostly from <a href="/wiki/Stratus_cloud" title="Stratus cloud">stratus clouds</a> or <a href="/wiki/Fog" title="Fog">fog</a>, and never fall in the form of a shower.</p>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/METAR" title="METAR">METAR</a> code for snow grains is <b>SG</b>.</p>\n<table class="vertical-navbox nowraplinks hlist"
