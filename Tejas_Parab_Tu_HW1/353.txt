href="/wiki/South_Pacific_tropical_cyclone" title="South Pacific tropical cyclone">South Pacific tropical cyclone seasons</a><br />\n<a href="/wiki/1991%E2%80%9392_South_Pacific_cyclone_season" title="1991\xe2\x80\x9392 South Pacific cyclone season">1991\xe2\x80\x9392</a>, <a href="/wiki/1992%E2%80%9393_South_Pacific_cyclone_season" title="1992\xe2\x80\x9393 South Pacific cyclone season">1992\xe2\x80\x9393</a>, <b>1993\xe2\x80\x9394</b>, <a href="/wiki/1994%E2%80%9395_South_Pacific_cyclone_season" title="1994\xe2\x80\x9395 South Pacific cyclone season">1994\xe2\x80\x9395</a>, Pacific cyclone season</b> was a near average tropical cyclone season with five <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclones" class="mw-redirect" title="Tropical cyclones">tropical cyclones</a> occurring within the South Pacific to the east of <a href="/wiki/160th_meridian_east" title="160th meridian east">160\xc2\xb0E</a>. The season officially ran from November 1, 1993, to April 30, 1994, with the first disturbance of the season forming on December 26 and the last disturbance dissipating on April 25.</p>\n<p>During the season, tropical cyclones were officially monitored by the <a href="/wiki/Regional_Specialized_Meteorological_Center" title="Regional Specialized Meteorological Center">Regional Specialized Meteorological Center</a> (RSMC) in <a href="/wiki/Fiji_Meteorological_Service" title="Fiji Meteorological Service">Nadi, Fiji</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Regional_Specialized_Meteorological_Center#Tropical_cyclone_warning_centers" title="Regional Specialized Meteorological Center">Tropical Cyclone Warning Centers</a> (TCWC) in <a href="/wiki/Bureau_of_Meteorology" title="Bureau of Meteorology">Brisbane, Australia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Meteorological_Service_of_New_Zealand_Limited" class="mw-redirect" title="Meteorological Service of New Zealand Limited">Wellington, New Zealand</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-99_Plan_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-99_Plan-1">[1]</a></sup> Throughout the season the United States Navy also monitored the basin and issued unofficial warnings, through its <a href="/wiki/Joint_Typhoon_Warning_Center" title="Joint Typhoon Warning Center">Joint Typhoon Warning Center</a> (JTWC) and Naval Western and Oceanography Center (NWOC).<sup id="cite_ref-JTWCATCR_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JTWCATCR-2">[2]</a></sup> Tropical cyclones that were located between 160\xc2\xb0E and 120\xc2\xb0W as well as the Equator and 25\xc2\xb0S were monitored by RSMC Nadi while any that were located to the south of 25\xc2\xb0S between 160\xc2\xb0E and 120\xc2\xb0W were monitored by TCWC Wellington.<sup id="cite_ref-99_Plan_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-99_Plan-1">[1]</a></sup> During the season the JTWC issued warnings on any tropical cyclone that was located between 160\xc2\xb0E and 180\xc2\xb0 while the NPMOC issued warnings for tropical cyclones forming between the 180\xc2\xb0 and the American coast. RSMC Nadi and TCWC Wellington both used the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Tropical_Cyclone_Intensity_Scale" class="mw-redirect" title="Australian Tropical Cyclone Intensity Scale">Australian Tropical Cyclone Intensity Scale</a>, and measured windspeeds over a 10-minute period during the season, while the JTWC and the NPMOC measured sustained windspeeds over a 1&#160;-minute period.<sup id="cite_ref-99_Plan_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-99_Plan-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-JTWCATCR_2-1" class="reference"><a id="cite_ref-Hanstrum_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hanstrum-5">[5]</a></sup> Later that day, the JTWC classified the depression as Tropical Cyclone 05P, before TCWC Nadi reported that Over the next few days the cyclone continued to move to the south-east towards the South Pacific basin, before as it peaked in intensity on January 16 as a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_scales#Australia_and_Fiji" title="Tropical cyclone scales">category 5 severe tropical cyclone</a>, Rewa turned and started to move towards the south-west. Over the next few days, the cyclone gradually weakened while it moved towards the south-west and a predicted landfall near Mackay in Queensland. However, during January 18, Rewa interacted with an upper level trough and as a result turned and started to move towards the south-east along the Queensland coast.
