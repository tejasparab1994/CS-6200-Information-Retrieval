action</h1>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<div id="contentSub"></div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">\n\t\t\t\t\tJump to:\t\t\t\t\t<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, \t\t\t\t\t<a href="#p-search">search</a>\n\t\t\t\t</div>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Centers of action</b> are extensive and almost stationary <a href="/wiki/Low-pressure_area" title="Low-pressure area">low-pressure areas</a> or <a href="/wiki/Anticyclone" title="Anticyclone">anticyclones</a> which control the movement of atmospheric disturbances over a large area.<sup id="cite_ref-OMM_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OMM-1">[1]</a></sup> This does not mean that the position of the center is constant over a specific area but that the monthly atmospheric pressure corresponds to a high or a low pressure.<sup id="cite_ref-OMM2_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OMM2-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-OMM3_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OMM3-3">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The French meteorologist <a href="/wiki/L%C3%A9on_Teisserenc_de_Bort" title="L\xc3\xa9on Teisserenc de Bort">L\xc3\xa9on Teisserenc de Bort</a> was the first in 1881 to apply this term to maxima and minima of pressure on daily charts. The main centers of action in the Northern Hemisphere are the <a href="/wiki/Icelandic_Low" title="Icelandic Low">Icelandic Low</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Aleutian_Low" title="Aleutian Low">Aleutian Low</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Azores_High" title="Azores High">Azores/Bermuda High</a>, the <a href="/wiki/North_Pacific_High" title="North Pacific High">Pacific High</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Siberian_High" title="Siberian High">Siberian High</a> (in winter), and the Asiatic Low (in summer).<sup id="cite_ref-AMS_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AMS-4">[4]</a></sup> Sir <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Walker" title="Gilbert Walker">Gilbert Walker</a> used the same term to relate meteorological elements in a region to weather in the following season in other regions for the <a href="/wiki/El_Ni%C3%B1o%E2%80%93Southern_Oscillation#Southern_Oscillation" title="El Ni\xc3\xb1o\xe2\x80\x93Southern Oscillation">Southern Oscillation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-AMS_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AMS-4">[4]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Causes">Causes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Centers_of_action&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Causes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:HadleyCross-sec.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/HadleyCross-sec.jpg/220px-HadleyCross-sec.jpg" width="220" height="126" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/HadleyCross-sec.jpg/330px-HadleyCross-sec.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/38/HadleyCross-sec.jpg 2x" data-file-width="396" data-file-height="226" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:HadleyCross-sec.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nHadley cells transporting heat and humidity from the Tropics to Mid-latitudes.</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>In the region called the <a href="/wiki/Horse_latitudes" title="Horse latitudes">Horse latitudes</a>, between 30 and 35 degrees of latitude North or South, there is a series of semi-permanent anticyclones on the downward side of the <a href="/wiki/Hadley_cell" title="Hadley cell">Hadley cell</a> of the general <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_circulation" title="Atmospheric circulation">atmospheric circulation</a>. The thermal contrasts over oceanic waters leads to the formation of theses centers of action as a direct convective circulation is established between the equator and higher latitudes in a very weak <a href="/wiki/Coriolis_force" title="Coriolis force">Coriolis force</a> zone.<sup id="cite_ref-Leduc_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Leduc-5">[5]</a></sup> The Azores/Bermuda High is found in this zone.</p>\n<p>Similarly, low pressure areas are formed in the oceanic circulation near the Polar regions were sea water is much warmer than the land. Such centers are the <a href="/wiki/Icelandic_Low" title="Icelandic Low">Icelandic Low</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Aleutian_Low" title="Aleutian Low">Aleutian Low</a> and numerous lows near the coast of <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctica</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-MF_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MF-6">[6]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Finally, in both hemispheres, there are centers of action inland in large continental zones where cold or warm air can be trapped by surrounding mountainous zones or high contrast or temperature by surrounding seas. This is the case for <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a> where the temperature is very cold in winter forming the <a href="/wiki/Siberian_High" title="Siberian High">Siberian High</a>, or very hot in summer to give a <a href="/wiki/Thermal_low" title="Thermal low">thermal low</a> in summer. Antarctica is so cold in all seasons that it has a permanent anticyclone at its center.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climatology">Climatology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Centers_of_action&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Climatology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mslp-jja-djf.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Mslp-jja-djf.png/220px-Mslp-jja-djf.png" width="220" height="227" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Mslp-jja-djf.png/330px-Mslp-jja-djf.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Mslp-jja-djf.png/440px-Mslp-jja-djf.png 2x" data-file-width="652" data-file-height="674" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mslp-jja-djf.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nDistribution of the centers of action (June\xe2\x80\x93July\xe2\x80\x93August at top and December\xe2\x80\x93January\xe2\x80\x93February at bottom)</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>The intensity of these systems vary during the year depending on the temperature of water and air. The action centers falter when the temperature contrasts decrease and conversely, they reinforce when these increases. Their position also varies, always following the maximum contrast. For example, the Azores anticyclone moves between the <a href="/wiki/Azores" title="Azores">Azores</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a>, according to the position of the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Stream" title="Gulf Stream">Gulf Stream</a> and the difference in warming between the Poles and the equator.<sup id="cite_ref-MF_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MF-6">[6]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The distribution of the action centers in the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Hemisphere" title="Southern Hemisphere">Southern Hemisphere</a> changes far less than in the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Hemisphere" title="Northern Hemisphere">Northern Hemisphere</a> during the year because the landmass in that hemisphere is relatively small. Apart from a thermal anticyclone over Australia during the southern winter, the centers of action are fairly stable over the oceans. The southern parts of the Atlantic, Indian, and Pacific oceans have three anticyclones over the <i>Horse latitudes</i>. Further South, a fairly continuous series of low pressure areas cover the ocean near Antarctica during the whole year and does the Antarctica High over the continent.<sup id="cite_ref-MF_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MF-6">[6]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Centers_of_action&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap">\n<ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-OMM-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-OMM_1-0">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">World Meteorological Organization</a>. <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.eumetcal.org/euromet/glossary/centreof.htm">"Center of action"</a>.
