Near the strong wake low, strong winds and a temperature spike were recorded</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>A <b>wake low</b>, or <b>wake depression</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Mesoscale_meteorology" title="Mesoscale meteorology">mesoscale</a> <a href="/wiki/Low-pressure_area" title="Low-pressure area">low-pressure area</a> which trails the mesoscale high following a <a href="/wiki/Squall_line" title="Squall line">squall line</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Due to the subsiding warm air associated with the systems formation, clearing skies are associated with the wake low. Once difficult to detect in <a href="/wiki/Surface_weather_observation" title="Surface weather observation">surface weather observations</a> due to their broad spacing, the formation of mesoscale <a href="/wiki/Weather_station" title="Weather station">weather station</a> networks, or <a href="/wiki/Mesonet" title="Mesonet">mesonets</a>, has increased their detection.<sup id="cite_ref-Conder_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Conder-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Severe_weather" title="Severe weather">Severe weather</a>, in the form of high <a href="/wiki/Wind" title="Wind">winds</a>, can be generated by the wake low when the pressure difference between the mesohigh preceding it and the wake low is intense enough.<sup id="cite_ref-Gaffin_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gaffin-3">[3]</a></sup> When the squall line is in the process of decay, <a href="/wiki/Heat_burst" title="Heat burst">heat bursts</a> can be generated near the wake low. Once new <a href="/wiki/Thunderstorm" title="Thunderstorm">thunderstorm</a> activity along the squall line concludes, the wake low associated with it weakens in <a href="/wiki/Tandem" title="Tandem">tandem</a>.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Formation"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Formation</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Detection"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Detection</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#Association_with_heat_bursts"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">Association with heat bursts</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#See_also"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">See also</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n<p></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation">Formation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wake_low&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Formation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Wake lows form due to <a href="/wiki/Lapse_rate" title="Lapse rate">adiabatic</a> warming in the wake of mature squall lines at the back edge of their rain shields, where <a href="/wiki/Evaporation" title="Evaporation">evaporative</a> cooling is unable to offset warming due to atmospheric <a href="/wiki/Subsidence" title="Subsidence">subsidence</a>, or downward motion. They can be caused by <a href="/wiki/Gravity_wave" title="Gravity wave">gravity waves</a> which duct through boundary layers which are deep and cold to the north of a <a href="/wiki/Weather_front" title="Weather front">weather front</a>. As with mesoscale <a href="/wiki/High-pressure_area" title="High-pressure area">high-pressure areas</a> behind a squall line, when new <a href="/wiki/Thunderstorm" title="Thunderstorm">thunderstorm</a> development stops along the squall line, the wake low will weaken as well. Clearing conditions will accompany the wake low, due to the descending warm air mass associated with the feature.<sup id="cite_ref-Barry_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Barry-4">[4]</a></sup> Within the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, these systems have been observed to form in the <a href="/wiki/Mississippi_river" class="mw-redirect" title="Mississippi river">Mississippi river</a> valley,<sup id="cite_ref-Gaffin_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gaffin-3">[3]</a></sup> Southeast,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Great_Plains" title="Great Plains">Great Plains</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Santos_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Santos-6">[6]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Detection">Detection</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wake_low&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Detection">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Tahomawakelow.gif" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Tahomawakelow.gif/250px-Tahomawakelow.gif" width="250" height="201" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Tahomawakelow.gif/375px-Tahomawakelow.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Tahomawakelow.gif/500px-Tahomawakelow.gif 2x" data-file-width="566" data-file-height="454" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Tahomawakelow.gif" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nThis radar image shows the location of a wake low wind event near <a href="/wiki/Tahoka,_Texas" title="Tahoka, Texas">Tahoka, Texas</a>. Note the minimum in the radar returns in that area.</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Both direct and indirect ways have been found to indicate the presence of a wake depression. The most direct is through the use of surface observations. Prior to the development of mesoscale weather station networks, or mesonets, it was difficult to locate wake lows. With the advent of mesoscale networks, wake lows have become easier to detect. On <a href="/wiki/Weather_satellite" title="Weather satellite">weather satellite</a> and <a href="/wiki/Weather_radar" title="Weather radar">weather radar</a> imagery, wake lows and heat bursts normally occur on the rear side of a precipitation area. However, within larger precipitation areas, they can be located within the area showing a minimum of returns.<sup id="cite_ref-Conder_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Conder-2">[2]</a></sup> When using velocity data from a <a href="/wiki/WSR-88D" class="mw-redirect" title="WSR-88D">WSR-88D</a> doppler radar, high winds on the back edge of reflectivity data can give away the location of a wake low.<sup id="cite_ref-Santos_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Santos-6">[6]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Association_with_heat_bursts">Association with heat bursts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wake_low&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Association with heat bursts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Heat_burst" title="Heat burst">Heat burst</a></div>\n<p>Heat bursts are rare atmospheric phenomenon characterized by gusty winds and a rapid increase in <a href="/wiki/Temperature" title="Temperature">temperature</a> and decrease in <a href="/wiki/Dew_point" title="Dew point">dew point</a> (moisture). Heat bursts typically occur during night-time and are associated with decaying <a href="/wiki/Thunderstorms" class="mw-redirect" title="Thunderstorms">thunderstorms</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In association with wake lows, heat bursts are caused when rain evaporates (<a href="/wiki/Virga" title="Virga">virga</a>) into a parcel of cold dry air high in the atmosphere making the air denser than its surroundings.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The parcel descends rapidly, warming due to compression, overshoots its <a href="/wiki/Equilibrium_level" title="Equilibrium level">equilibrium level</a> and reaches the surface, similar to a <a href="/wiki/Downburst" title="Downburst">downburst</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Recorded temperatures during heat bursts have reached well above 90&#160;<a href="/wiki/Fahrenheit" title="Fahrenheit">\xc2\xb0F</a> (32&#160;<a href="/wiki/Celsius" title="Celsius">\xc2\xb0C</a>), sometimes rising by 20&#160;\xc2\xb0F (11&#160;\xc2\xb0C) or more within only a few minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> More extreme events have also been documented, where temperatures have been reported to exceed 130&#160;\xc2\xb0F (54&#160;\xc2\xb0C), although such extreme events have never been officially verified.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Heat bursts are also characterised by extremely dry air and are sometimes associated with very strong, even damaging, <a href="/wiki/Wind" title="Wind">winds</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Conder_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Conder-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
