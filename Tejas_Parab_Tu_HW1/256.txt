map with the tropical zone highlighted in red, which is bordered by the middle latitudes both to the north and south.</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>The <b>middle latitudes</b> (<b>mid-latitudes</b>, sometimes <b>midlatitudes</b>) are between 23\xc2\xb026\'22" North and 66\xc2\xb033\'39" North, and between 23\xc2\xb026\'22" South and 66\xc2\xb033\'39" South <a href="/wiki/Latitude" title="Latitude">latitude</a>, or, the Earth\'s <a href="/wiki/Temperate_zone" class="mw-redirect" title="Temperate zone">temperate zones</a> between the <a href="/wiki/Tropics" title="Tropics">tropics</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Arctic" title="Arctic">Arctic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Antarctic" title="Antarctic">Antarctic</a> <a href="/wiki/Polar_region" class="mw-redirect" title="Polar region">polar regions</a>. <a href="/wiki/Weather_front" title="Weather front">Weather fronts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical cyclones</a> are usually found in this area, as well as occasional <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclones" class="mw-redirect" title="Tropical cyclones">tropical cyclones</a> which have traveled from their areas of formation closer to the equator.</p>\n<p>The prevailing winds in the middle latitudes are often very strong. These parts of the world also see a wide variety of fast-changing weather as cold <a href="/wiki/Air_mass" title="Air mass">air masses</a> from the poles and warm air masses from the tropics push up and down over them, sometimes alternating within hours of each other, especially in the <a href="/wiki/Roaring_Forties" title="Roaring Forties">roaring forties</a> (between 40 and 50 degrees latitude in both hemispheres).</p>\n<p>There are four types of epic mid-latitude climates: <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_climate" title="Mediterranean climate">mediterranean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Humid_subtropical" class="mw-redirect" title="Humid subtropical">humid subtropical</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marine_west_coast" class="mw-redirect" title="Marine west coast">marine west coast</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Humid_continental" class="mw-redirect" title="Humid continental">humid continental</a>.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span
