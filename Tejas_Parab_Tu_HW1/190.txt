It is the principal agency responsible for <a href="/wiki/Meteorological" class="mw-redirect" title="Meteorological">meteorological</a> observations, <a href="/wiki/Weather_forecasting" title="Weather forecasting">weather forecasting</a> and <a href="/wiki/Seismology" title="Seismology">seismology</a>. IMD is headquartered in Pune and operates hundreds of observation stations across <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> and <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctica</a>.Regional office are at <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">Mumbai</a>,<a href="/wiki/Kolkata" title="Kolkata">Kolkata</a>,<a href="/wiki/Nagpur" title="Nagpur">Nagpur</a> and <a href="/wiki/Delhi" title="Delhi">Delhi</a></p>\n<p>IMD is also one of the six <a href="/wiki/Regional_Specialised_Meteorological_Centre" class="mw-redirect" title="Regional Specialised Meteorological Centre">Regional Specialised Meteorological Centres</a> of the <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">World Meteorological Organization</a>. It has the responsibility for forecasting, naming and distribution of warnings for <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclones</a> in the Northern Indian Ocean region, including the <a href="/wiki/Malacca_Straits" class="mw-redirect" title="Malacca Straits">Malacca Straits</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal" title="Bay of Bengal">Bay of Bengal</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Sea" title="Arabian Sea">Arabian Sea</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Persian_Gulf" title="Persian Gulf">Persian Gulf</a>.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#History"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">History</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Organisation"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Organisation</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#Tasks"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">Tasks</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#New_initiatives"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">New initiatives</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n<p></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=India_Meteorological_Department&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In 1686, <a href="/wiki/Edmond_Halley" title="Edmond Halley">Edmond Halley</a> published his treatise on the Indian summer <a href="/wiki/Monsoon" title="Monsoon">monsoon</a>, which he attributed to a seasonal reversal of winds due to the differential heating of the Asian land mass and the Indian Ocean. The first meteorological observatories were established in <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> by the <a href="/wiki/British_East_India_Company" class="mw-redirect" title="British East India Company">British East India Company</a>. These included the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Calcutta_Observatory&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Calcutta Observatory (page does not exist)">Calcutta Observatory</a> in 1785, the <a href="/wiki/Madras_Observatory" title="Madras Observatory">Madras Observatory</a> in 1796 and the <a href="/wiki/Colaba_Observatory" title="Colaba Observatory">Colaba Observatory</a> in 1826. Several other observatories were established in India during the first half of the 19th century by various provincial governments.</p>\n<p><a href="/wiki/The_Asiatic_Society" title="The Asiatic Society">The Asiatic Society</a>, founded in <a href="/wiki/Calcutta" class="mw-redirect" title="Calcutta">Calcutta</a> in 1784 and in <a href="/wiki/Bombay" class="mw-redirect" title="Bombay">Bombay</a> in 1804, promoted the study of meteorology in India.<a href="/wiki/Henry_Piddington" title="Henry Piddington">Henry Piddington</a> published almost 40 papers dealing with <a href="/wiki/Tropical_storms" class="mw-redirect" title="Tropical storms">tropical storms</a> from Calcutta between 1835 and 1855 in <i>The Journal of the Asiatic Society</i>. He also coined the term <i><a href="/wiki/Cyclone" title="Cyclone">cyclone</a></i>, meaning the coil of a snake. In 1842, he published his landmark thesis, <i>Laws of the Storms</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-imd-mm_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-imd-mm-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<p>After a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> hit <a href="/wiki/Calcutta" class="mw-redirect" title="Calcutta">Calcutta</a> in 1864, and the subsequent <a href="/wiki/Famines" class="mw-redirect" title="Famines">famines</a> in 1866 and 1871 due to the failure of the monsoons, it was decided to organise the collection and analysis of meteorological observations under one roof. As a result, the <b>India Meteorology Department</b> was established in 1875. <a href="/wiki/Henry_Francis_Blanford" title="Henry Francis Blanford">Henry Francis Blanford</a> was appointed the first Meteorological Reporter of the IMD. In May 1889, <a href="/wiki/John_Eliot_(meteorologist)" title="John Eliot (meteorologist)">Sir John Eliot</a> was appointed the first <i>Director General of Observatories</i> in the erstwhile capital, Calcutta. The IMD headquarters were later shifted to <a href="/wiki/Shimla" title="Shimla">Shimla</a> in 1905, then to <a href="/wiki/Pune" title="Pune">Pune</a> in 1928 and finally to New Delhi in 1944.<sup id="cite_ref-imd-establishment_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-imd-establishment-3">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<p>after <a href="/wiki/Independence_of_India" class="mw-redirect" title="Independence of India">independence</a> on 27 April 1949.<sup id="cite_ref-wmo-members_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wmo-members-4">[4]</a></sup> The agency has gained in prominence due to the significance of the monsoon rains on <a href="/wiki/Indian_agriculture" class="mw-redirect" title="Indian agriculture">Indian agriculture</a>. It plays a vital role in preparing the annual monsoon forecast, as well as in tracking the progress of the monsoon across India every season.<sup id="cite_ref-bs-imd_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bs-imd-5">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organisation">Organisation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=India_Meteorological_Department&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Organisation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>IMD is headed by the <i>Director General of Meteorology</i>. Dr. K. J. Ramesh is Director General of Meteorology since August 1 2016. <sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>IMD has 6 <i>Regional Meteorological Centre</i>s, each under a Deputy Director General. These are located in <a href="/wiki/Regional_Meteorological_Centre,_Chennai" title="Regional Meteorological Centre, Chennai">Chennai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guwahati" title="Guwahati">Guwahati</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kolkata" title="Kolkata">Kolkata</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">Mumbai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nagpur" title="Nagpur">Nagpur</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Delhi" title="New Delhi">New Delhi</a>. There are also <i>Meteorological Centres</i> in every state capital. Other IMD units such as Forecasting Offices, Agrometeorological Advisory Service Centers, Flood Meteorological Offices, Area Cyclone Warning Centers and Cyclone Warning Centers are usually co-located with various observatories or meteorological center.<sup id="cite_ref-imd-org_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-imd-org-8">[8]</a></sup></p>\n<p>IMD operates a network of hundreds of surface and glacial observatories, Upper Air (high altitude) stations, <a href="/wiki/Ozone_layer" title="Ozone layer">ozone</a> and radiation observatories and <a href="/wiki/Meteorological_radar" class="mw-redirect" title="Meteorological radar">meteorological radar</a> stations. Additional data is received from India\'s constellation of satellites, such as <a href="/wiki/Kalpana-1" title="Kalpana-1">Kalpana-1</a>, <a href="/wiki/Megha-Tropiques" title="Megha-Tropiques">Megha-Tropiques</a> and instruments on board the <a href="/wiki/IRS_series" class="mw-redirect" title="IRS series">IRS series</a> and the <a href="/wiki/INSAT" class="mw-redirect" title="INSAT">INSAT</a> series of satellites.<sup id="cite_ref-imd-satellites_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-imd-satellites-9">[9]</a></sup> Data and observations are also reported into the IMD network from meteorological instruments on board Indian merchant marine and <a href="/wiki/Indian_Navy" title="Indian Navy">Indian Navy</a> ships. IMD was the first organisation in India to deploy a message switching computer for supporting its global data exchange.</p>\n<p>IMD collaborates with other agencies such as the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Institute_of_Tropical_Meteorology" title="Indian Institute of Tropical Meteorology">Indian Institute of Tropical Meteorology</a>, <a href="/wiki/National_Centre_for_Medium_Range_Weather_Forecasting" title="National Centre for Medium Range Weather Forecasting">National Centre for Medium Range Weather Forecasting</a> and the <a href="/wiki/National_Institute_of_Ocean_Technology" title="National Institute of Ocean Technology">National Institute of Ocean Technology</a>.</p>\n<p>IMD also operates seismic monitoring centres at key locations for earthquake monitoring and measurements.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tasks">Tasks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=India_Meteorological_Department&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Tasks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>IMD undertakes observations, communications, forecasting and weather services. In collaboration with the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Space_Research_Organisation" title="Indian Space Research Organisation">Indian Space Research Organisation</a>, the IMD also uses the <a href="/wiki/IRS_series" class="mw-redirect" title="IRS series">IRS series</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Indian_National_Satellite_System" title="Indian National Satellite System">Indian National Satellite System</a> (INSAT) for weather monitoring of the Indian subcontinent. IMD was the first weather bureau of a developing country to develop and maintain its own satellite system.</p>\n<p>IMD is one of the 6 worldwide <a href="/wiki/Regional_Specialised_Meteorological_Centre" class="mw-redirect" title="Regional Specialised Meteorological Centre">Regional Specialised Meteorological Centres</a> of the Tropical Cyclone Programme of the World Weather Watch of the <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">World Meteorological Organization</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wmo-rsmc_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wmo-rsmc-10">[10]</a></sup> It is regional nodal agency for forecasting, naming and disseminating warnings about <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> in the Indian Ocean north of the <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">Equator</a>.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="New_initiatives">New initiatives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=India_Meteorological_Department&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: New initiatives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The IMD launched System of Aerosol Monitoring and Research (SAMAR) in January 2016 to study the concentration of <a href="/wiki/Black_carbon" title="Black carbon">Black carbon</a>, radiative properties of <a href="/wiki/Aerosol" title="Aerosol">aerosols</a>, environmental visibility and their climatological impacts. It would contain a network of 16 <a href="/wiki/Aethalometer" title="Aethalometer">aethalometers</a>, 12 sky radiometers and 12 <a href="/wiki/Nephelometer" title="Nephelometer">nephelometers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=India_Meteorological_Department&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<table role="presentation" class="mbox-small plainlinks sistersitebox" style="background-color:#f9f9f9;border:1px solid #aaa;color:#000">\n<tr>\n<td class="mbox-image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/4a/Commons-logo.svg/30px-Commons-logo.svg.png" width="30" height="40" class="noviewer" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/4a/Commons-logo.svg/45px-Commons-logo.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/4a/Commons-logo.svg/59px-Commons-logo.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="1376" /></td>\n<td class="mbox-text plainlist">Wikimedia Commons has media related to <i><b><a href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:India_Meteorological_Department" class="extiw" title="commons:Category:India Meteorological Department">India Meteorological Department</a></b></i>.</td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em;
