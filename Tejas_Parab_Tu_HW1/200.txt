href="#p-search">search</a>\n\t\t\t\t</div>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>A <b>Regional Specialized Meteorological id="Environmental_emergency_response_programme">Environmental emergency response programme</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Regional_Specialized_Meteorological_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Environmental emergency response programme">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>As a result of the poor communications between countries following the <a href="/wiki/Chernobyl_disaster" title="Chernobyl disaster">Chernobyl disaster</a> in the Spring of 1986, the World Meteorological Organization <small>(WMO)</small> was requested by the <a href="/wiki/International_Atomic_Energy_Agency" title="International Atomic Energy Agency">International Atomic Energy Agency</a> <small>(IAEA)</small> and other international organizations to arrange for early warning messages about nuclear accidents to be transmitted over the <a href="/wiki/Global_Telecommunications_System" title="Global Telecommunications System">Global Telecommunications System</a> (GTS).<sup id="cite_ref-NOAA_ARL_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NOAA_ARL-1">[1]</a></sup> In addition some WMO member countries that lacked extensive forecasting capability requested that specialized pollutant transport and dispersion forecasts be provided during these emergencies. As a result, during 1989 Meteo-France <small>(MF)</small>, <a href="/wiki/Environment_Canada" class="mw-redirect" title="Environment Canada">Environment Canada</a> <small>(EC)</small> and the United Kingdom\'s <a href="/wiki/Met_Office" title="Met Office">Met Office</a> <small>(UKMO)</small> were all set up as RSMCs under interim arrangements between the WMO and the IAEA.<sup id="cite_ref-NOAA_ARL_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NOAA_ARL-1">[1]</a></sup> Under these arrangements Meteo-France provided global coverage with the UKMO as the backup center until each WMO region had at least two RSMCs for transport model products.<sup id="cite_ref-NOAA_ARL_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NOAA_ARL-1">[1]</a></sup> The need for the rationalization of transport and dispersion forecasts became even more apparent during the oil fire emergency after the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_War" title="Gulf War">Gulf War</a>, when several organizations provided personnel on the ground with predictions of the smoke plume behaviour which were often misleading as there was no existing and well-recognized system to sort out the predictions from less experienced sources.<sup id="cite_ref-NOAA_ARL_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NOAA_ARL-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>After it had successfully demonstrated its RSMC capabilities to the WMO\'s Commission for Basic Systems (CBS) during November 1992, the United States <a href="/wiki/National_Oceanic_and_Atmospheric_Administration" title="National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration">National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration</a> was made the fourth RSMC effective on July 1, 1993.<sup id="cite_ref-NOAA_ARL_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NOAA_ARL-1">[1]</a></sup> This resulted in the WMO regions RA and RA IV having two RSMCs each which indicated the need to revise the interim arrangements.<sup id="cite_ref-NOAA_ARL_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NOAA_ARL-1">[1]</a></sup> The new arrangements came into force in August 1994, with EC and NOAA responsible for the Americas, while MF and the UKMO covered the remaining parts of the World.<sup id="cite_ref-NOAA_ARL_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NOAA_ARL-1">[1]</a></sup> The Australian Bureau of Meteorology was subsequently made an RSMC on July 1, 1995, while the Japan Meteorological Agency was made one in July 1997.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<p>There are eight meteorological centres for distribution of transport, deposition, and dispersion modeling, in the event of an environmental catastrophe that crosses international borders:<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>\n<ul>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Exeter,_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Exeter, England">Exeter, England</a> RSMC&#160;\xe2\x80\x94 For Europe and Africa</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Toulouse,_France" class="mw-redirect" title="Toulouse, France">Toulouse, France</a> RSMC&#160;\xe2\x80\x94 For Europe and Africa</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Montr%C3%A9al,_Canada" class="mw-redirect" title="Montr\xc3\xa9al, Canada">Montr\xc3\xa9al, Canada</a> RSMC&#160;\xe2\x80\x94 For the Americas, with backup responsibility for the Southwest Pacific</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> RSMC&#160;\xe2\x80\x94 For the Americas, with backup responsibility for the Southwest Pacific</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Beijing,_China" class="mw-redirect" title="Beijing, China">Beijing, China</a> RSMC&#160;\xe2\x80\x94 For Asia</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/New_Delhi,_India" class="mw-redirect" title="New Delhi, India">New Delhi, India</a> RSMC&#160;- For Asia</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Obninsk" title="Obninsk">Obninsk</a>, Russia RSMC&#160;\xe2\x80\x94 For Asia</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Tokyo,_Japan" class="mw-redirect" title="Tokyo, Japan">Tokyo, Japan</a> RSMC&#160;\xe2\x80\x94 For Asia</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Nairobi,_Kenya" class="mw-redirect" title="Nairobi, Kenya">Nairobi, Kenya</a> RSMC&#160;\xe2\x80\x94 For East Africa</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Pretoria,_South_Africa" class="mw-redirect" title="Pretoria, South Africa">Pretoria, South Africa</a> RSMC&#160;\xe2\x80\x94 For Southern Africa</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Darwin,_Australia" class="mw-redirect" title="Darwin, Australia">Darwin, Australia</a> RSMC&#160;\xe2\x80\x94 For the Southwest Pacific<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>\n</ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tropical_cyclones">Tropical cyclones</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Regional_Specialized_Meteorological_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Tropical cyclones">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>A Tropical Cyclone Regional Specialized Meteorological Centre is responsible for detecting tropical cyclones in its designated area of responsibility, and for providing basic information about the <a href="/wiki/Low-pressure_area" title="Low-pressure area">systems</a> present and their forecast position, movement and intensity.<sup id="cite_ref-WMO_FFM_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WMO_FFM-6">[6]</a></sup> There are six such meteorological centres in addition to six regional Tropical Cyclone Warning Centres (TCWCs) that all provide public tropical cyclone advisory messages and assist other National Meteorological and Hydrological Services in preparing alerts and warnings for their respective countries.<sup id="cite_ref-WMO_FFM_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WMO_FFM-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In addition, all of the centres are responsible for naming tropical cyclones when they develop into or become equivalent to tropical storms in their area of responsibility, with the exceptions of RSMC La Reunion and TCWC Wellington.</p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:402px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Tropical_Cyclone_Centers_and_Regions.png" class="image"><img alt="" regions</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li>The United States <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> (NHC/RSMC Miami) is responsible for the tracking of tropical cyclones within the Atlantic Ocean and Eastern Pacific basins.</li>\n<li>The United States <a href="/wiki/Central_Pacific_Hurricane_Center" title="Central Pacific Hurricane Center">Central Pacific Hurricane Center</a> (CPHC/RSMC Honolulu) provides satellite fixes for the Western Pacific and Southern Pacific basins and tropical cyclone warnings for the Central Pacific basin and in the Eastern Pacific basin if the NHC is too busy with the Atlantic basin or is incapacitated in any way.</li>\n<li>The <a href="/wiki/Japan_Meteorological_Agency" title="Japan Meteorological Agency">Japan Meteorological Agency</a> (JMA/RSMC Tokyo) is responsible for issuing advisories within the Western Pacific basin.</li>\n<li>The <a href="/wiki/India_Meteorological_Department" title="India Meteorological Department">India Meteorological Department</a> (IMD/RSMC New Delhi) is responsible for tracking tropical cyclones within the North Indian Ocean.</li>\n<li>M\xc3\xa9t\xc3\xa9o-France La Reunion (MFR/RSMC La Reunion) is responsible for the issuing advisories and tracking of tropical cyclones, however, the naming of systems is deferred to the <a href="/wiki/Mauritius" title="Mauritius">Mauritius</a> and <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a> weather services.</li>\n<li>Within the Australian region five tropical cyclone warning centres are responsible for the naming and tracking of tropical cyclones. Three of these centres are run by and located within the Australian <a href="/wiki/Bureau_of_Meteorology" title="Bureau of Meteorology">Bureau of Meteorology</a> regional offices in Perth, Darwin and Brisbane and use one national list of names. The other two tropical cyclone warning centres in the Australian region are located in <a href="/wiki/Port_Moresby" title="Port Moresby">Port Moresby</a>, <a href="/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea" title="Papua New Guinea">Papua New Guinea</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jakarta" title="Jakarta">Jakarta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>.</li>\n<li>Within the Southern Pacific the <a href="/wiki/Fiji_Meteorological_Service" title="Fiji Meteorological Service">Fiji Meteorological Service</a> (FMS, RSMC Nadi) is responsible for the naming of tropical cyclones for the whole basin, however, the <a href="/wiki/Meteorological_Service_of_New_Zealand" class="mw-redirect" title="Meteorological Service of New Zealand">Meteorological Service of New Zealand</a> (MetService, TCWC Wellington) issues forecasts for the area below 25\xc2\xb0S.</li>\n</ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
