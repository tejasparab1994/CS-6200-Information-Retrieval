It is characterized by dry<sup id="cite_ref-dove_press_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dove_press-1">[1]</a></sup> and dusty northeasterly <a href="/wiki/Trade_wind" class="mw-redirect" title="Trade wind">trade wind</a>, of the same name, which blows from the <a href="/wiki/Sahara_Desert" class="mw-redirect" title="Sahara Desert">Sahara Desert</a> over West Africa into the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Guinea" title="Gulf of Guinea">Gulf of Guinea</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BHarmattan_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BHarmattan-2">[2]</a></sup> The name comes from or is related to <a href="/wiki/Ga_language" title="Ga language">Ga</a> <i>haramata</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It is cold<sup id="cite_ref-dove_press_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dove_press-1">[1]</a></sup> in most places, but can be hot<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> in some places, depending on circumstances.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The Harmattan blows during the dry season, which occurs during the lowest-sun months, when the <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_ridge" title="Subtropical ridge">subtropical ridge</a> of high pressure stays over the central Sahara Desert and when the low-pressure <a href="/wiki/Intertropical_Convergence_Zone" title="Intertropical Convergence Zone">Intertropical Convergence Zone</a> (ITCZ) stays over the Gulf of Guinea. On its passage over the Sahara, it picks up fine dust and sand particles (between 0.5 and 10 microns).</p>\n<p></p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Effects"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Effects</span></a>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-2 tocsection-2"><a href="#Harmattan_haze"><span class="tocnumber">1.1</span> <span class="toctext">Harmattan haze</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-2 tocsection-3"><a href="#Health"><span class="tocnumber">1.2</span> <span class="toctext">Health</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#See_also"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">See also</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-6"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n<p></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Effects">Effects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harmattan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Effects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Harmattan season differs from <a href="/wiki/Winter" title="Winter">winter</a>, because it is characterized by cold, dry, dust-laden wind, and also wide \xef\xac\x82uctuations in the <a href="/wiki/Room_temperature#Ambient_versus_room_temperature" title="Room temperature">ambient temperatures</a> of the day and night. Temperatures can easily be as low as 9&#160;\xc2\xb0C (48&#160;\xc2\xb0F) all day, but sometimes in the afternoon the temperature can also soar to as high as 30&#160;\xc2\xb0C (86&#160;\xc2\xb0F), while the <a href="/wiki/Relative_humidity" title="Relative humidity">relative humidity</a> drops under 10%.</p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ho,_Ghana.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Ho%2C_Ghana.JPG/220px-Ho%2C_Ghana.JPG" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Ho%2C_Ghana.JPG/330px-Ho%2C_Ghana.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Ho%2C_Ghana.JPG/440px-Ho%2C_Ghana.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ho,_Ghana.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nHarmattan haze over <a href="/wiki/Ho,_Ghana" title="Ho, Ghana">Ho, Ghana</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>The air is particularly dry and desiccating when the Harmattan blows over the region.<sup id="cite_ref-dove_press_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dove_press-1">[1]</a></sup> The Harmattan brings desert-like weather conditions: it lowers the humidity, dissipates cloud cover, prevents rainfall formation and sometimes creates big clouds of dust which can result in <a href="/wiki/Dust_storm" title="Dust storm">dust storms</a> or <a href="/wiki/Sandstorms" class="mw-redirect" title="Sandstorms">sandstorms</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The wind can increase fire risk<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and cause severe crop damage.<sup id="cite_ref-Saharan_wind_stirs_cocoa_market_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Saharan_wind_stirs_cocoa_market-7">[7]</a></sup> The interaction of the Harmattan with <a href="/wiki/Monsoon" title="Monsoon">monsoon</a> winds can cause <a href="/wiki/Tornado" title="Tornado">tornadoes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BHarmattan_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BHarmattan-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Harmattan_haze">Harmattan haze</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harmattan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Harmattan haze">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In some countries in West Africa, the heavy amount of dust in the air can severely limit visibility and block the sun for several days,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> comparable to a heavy <a href="/wiki/Fog" title="Fog">fog</a>. This effect is known as the Harmattan haze. It costs airlines millions of dollars in cancelled and diverted flights each year.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Re_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Re-10">[10]</a></sup> When the haze is weak, the skies are clear.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The dry air can break the trunks of trees growing in the region.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Health">Health</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harmattan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Health">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Humidity drops to as low as 15%, which can result in spontaneous nosebleeds for some people.<sup id="cite_ref-Saharan_wind_stirs_cocoa_market_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Saharan_wind_stirs_cocoa_market-7">[7]</a></sup> Other health effects on humans may include conditions of the skin, eyes, and respiratory system, including aggravation of <a href="/wiki/Asthma" title="Asthma">asthma</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harmattan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Khamsin" title="Khamsin">Khamsin</a></li>\n</ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harmattan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em;
