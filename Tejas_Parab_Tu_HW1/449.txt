class="mw-body-content">\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<div id="contentSub"></div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">\n\t\t\t\t\tJump to:\t\t\t\t\t<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, \t\t\t\t\t<a href="#p-search">search</a>\n\t\t\t\t</div>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>University Corporation for Atmospheric Research</b> (<b>UCAR</b>) is a nonprofit consortium of more than 100 colleges and universities providing research and training in the <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_science" class="mw-redirect" title="Atmospheric science">atmospheric</a> and related sciences.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> UCAR manages the <a href="/wiki/National_Center_for_Atmospheric_Research" title="National Center for Atmospheric Research">National Center for Atmospheric Research</a> (NCAR) and provides additional services to strengthen and support research and education through its community programs. Its headquarters, in <a href="/wiki/Boulder,_Colorado" title="Boulder, Colorado">Boulder</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colorado" title="Colorado">Colorado</a>, include NCAR\'s <a href="/wiki/Mesa_Laboratory" title="Mesa Laboratory">Mesa Laboratory</a>, designed by <a href="/wiki/I.M._Pei" class="mw-redirect" title="I.M. Pei">I.M. Pei</a>.</p>\n<p>UCAR was established in 1959 by faculty from 14 leading universities to support and nourish the atmospheric sciences. They were motivated by a newly recognized need for pooled observational and computational facilities and a strong research staff, which together would allow the academic community to carry out complex, long-term scientific programs beyond the reach of individual universities.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<p>This group\xe2\x80\x99s first major action, in partnership with the <a href="/wiki/National_Science_Foundation" title="National Science Foundation">National Science Foundation</a>, was to establish NCAR. Since then, UCAR has managed NCAR on behalf of NSF to address pressing scientific and societal needs involving the atmosphere and its interactions with the oceans, land, and Sun\xe2\x80\x94what is now called Earth system science.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Community_programs"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Community programs</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Governance_and_funding"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Governance and funding</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#UCAR_Presidents"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">UCAR Presidents</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n<p></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Community_programs">Community programs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_Corporation_for_Atmospheric_Research&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Community programs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>UCAR\xe2\x80\x99s role in supporting and complementing the work of academia has grown to include new research, service, and education efforts, including</p>\n<ul>\n<li><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.comet.ucar.edu/">COMET Program</a> - offers professional training on site and online in <a href="/wiki/Meteorology" title="Meteorology">meteorology</a>, weather hazards, hydrology, oceanography, emergency management, and environmental science</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Constellation_Observing_System_for_Meteorology,_Ionosphere,_and_Climate" title="Constellation Observing System for Meteorology, Ionosphere, and Climate">COSMIC Program</a> <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.cosmic.ucar.edu/">[1]</a> - supports satellite and ground-based observations and research using GPS technology for meteorology, climate, and ionospheric studies</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/GLOBE_Program" title="GLOBE Program">GLOBE Program</a> - provides hands-on, school-based learning and collaboration with a global network of students, scientists, and educators</li>\n<li><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.joss.ucar.edu/">Joint Office for Science Support</a> - supports community research, conference, and field projects</li>\n<li><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://nsdl.org/about/contact/">National Science Digital Library Resource Center</a> and <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://dlsciences.org/">Digital Learning Sciences</a> - supports and sustains community digital education projects</li>\n<li><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.unidata.ucar.edu/">Unidata Program</a> - provides streaming observational data to university classrooms and researchers around the world</li>\n<li><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.vsp.ucar.edu/">Visiting Scientist Programs</a> - places postdoctoral fellows and visiting scientists at partner institutions</li>\n</ul>\n<p>The organization hosts numerous workshops, community meetings, and opportunities for collaboration throughout the year.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance_and_funding">Governance and funding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_Corporation_for_Atmospheric_Research&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Governance and funding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>As of 2016, UCAR comprises 109 <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.ucar.edu/governance/members">member colleges and universities</a> and 52 <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.ucar.edu/governance/iap">international affiliates</a>. The organization has a total of 1,390 staff, including 877 at NCAR and 282 engaged in community programs. Total expenditures for fiscal year 2015 (October 2014\xe2\x80\x93September 2015) were approximately $204.2 million.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<p>UCAR and NCAR are headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Boulder,_Colorado" title="Boulder, Colorado">Boulder, Colorado</a>, spread across four campuses. Ancillary activities take place in Cheyenne, Wyoming, Washington, D.C., and in about 15 other states.</p>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Walter_Orr_Roberts" title="Walter Orr Roberts">Walter Orr Roberts</a> was UCAR\xe2\x80\x99s first president. Antonio J. Busalacchi, Jr. has been UCAR president since August 2016.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="UCAR_Presidents">UCAR Presidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_Corporation_for_Atmospheric_Research&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: UCAR Presidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>A list of all UCAR presidents is given below.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>\n<table class="wikitable">\n<tr>\n<th>UCAR President</th>\n<th>Dates in office</th>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Walter_Orr_Roberts" title="Walter Orr Roberts">Walter Orr Roberts</a></td>\n<td>1960 - 1974</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Francis_Patton_Bretherton" class="mw-redirect" title="Francis Patton Bretherton">Francis P.
