in the annual cycle of <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> and <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_cyclone" title="Subtropical cyclone">subtropical cyclone</a> formation. It will officially begin on November 1, 2017, and will end officially on April 30, 2018, with the exception for <a href="/wiki/Mauritius" title="Mauritius">Mauritius</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Seychelles" title="Seychelles">Seychelles</a>, for which it will end on May 15, 2018. These dates conventionally delimit the period of each year when most tropical and subtropical cyclones form in the basin, which is west of <a href="/wiki/Longitude" title="Longitude">90\xc2\xb0E</a> and south of the <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">Equator</a>. Tropical and subtropical cyclones in this basin are monitored by the <a href="/wiki/Regional_Specialised_Meteorological_Centre" class="mw-redirect" title="Regional Specialised Meteorological Centre">Regional Specialised Meteorological Centre</a> in <a href="/wiki/R%C3%A9union" title="R\xc3\xa9union">R\xc3\xa9union</a>.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc">\n<div class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_systems">Other systems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2017%E2%80%9318_South-West_Indian_Ocean_cyclone_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Other systems">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>A low-pressure system, classified as a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_scales#Australia_and_Fiji" title="Tropical cyclone scales">tropical low</a> by the Australian <a href="/wiki/Bureau_of_Meteorology" title="Bureau of Meteorology">Bureau of Meteorology</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-:1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-1">[1]</a></sup> moved west-southwest across the <a href="/wiki/90th_meridian_east" title="90th meridian east">90th meridian east</a> into the Southwest Indian Ocean basin on August 8.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> At 10:00 UTC the following day, M\xc3\xa9t\xc3\xa9o-France R\xc3\xa9union upgraded the low-pressure system to a zone of disturbed weather, with sustained winds of up to 55 km/h (35 mph) being detected on the southern side of the circulation by <a href="/wiki/Scatterometer" title="Scatterometer">scatterometer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup> At this time, the disturbance was located approximately 2,375 km (1,475 mi) west of <a href="/wiki/Jakarta" title="Jakarta">Jakarta, Indonesia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup> M\xc3\xa9t\xc3\xa9o-France noted that the revival of <a href="/wiki/Convection" title="Convection">convective</a> activity in the basin was likely due to the interaction between an eastwards-moving <a href="/wiki/Kelvin_wave" title="Kelvin wave">Kelvin wave</a> and a westwards-moving <a href="/wiki/Rossby_wave" title="Rossby wave">Rossby wave</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<div style="clear:both;"></div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Storm_names">Storm names</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2017%E2%80%9318_South-West_Indian_Ocean_cyclone_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Storm names">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Within the South-West Indian Ocean, tropical depressions and subtropical depressions that are judged to have 10-minute sustained wind speeds of 65&#160;km/h (40&#160;mph) by the <a href="/wiki/Regional_Specialized_Meteorological_Center" title="Regional Specialized Meteorological Center">Regional Specialized Meteorological Center</a> on <a href="/wiki/R%C3%A9union" title="R\xc3\xa9union">La R\xc3\xa9union Island</a>, France (RSMC La R\xc3\xa9union) are usually assigned a name. However, it is the Sub-Regional Tropical Cyclone Advisory Centers in Mauritius and Madagascar who name the systems. The Sub-Regional Tropical Cyclone Advisory Center in Mauritius names a storm should it intensify into a moderate tropical storm between <a href="/wiki/55th_meridian_east" title="55th meridian east">55\xc2\xb0E</a> and <a href="/wiki/90th_meridian_east" title="90th meridian east">90\xc2\xb0E</a>. If instead a cyclone intensifies into a moderate tropical storm between <a href="/wiki/30th_meridian_east" title="30th meridian east">30\xc2\xb0E</a> and <a href="/wiki/55th_meridian_east" title="55th meridian east">55\xc2\xb0E</a> then the Sub-Regional Tropical Cyclone Advisory Center in <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a> assigns the appropriate name to the storm. Beginning from the 2016\xe2\x80\x9317 season, name lists within the South-West Indian Ocean will be rotated on a triennial basis. Storm names are only used once, so any storm name used this year will be removed from rotation and replaced with a new name for the 2020\xe2\x80\x9321 season. The unused names are expected to be reused in the list for the 2020\xe2\x80\x9321 season.<sup id="cite_ref-TCOP-SWIO_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TCOP-SWIO-4">[4]</a></sup></p>\n<table
