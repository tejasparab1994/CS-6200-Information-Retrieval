class="mw-parser-output"><table class="infobox" style="width:22em">\n<caption>Canadian Hurricane Centre</caption>\n<tr>\n<th colspan="2" style="text-align:center;background-color: #efefef">Agency overview</th>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<th scope="row">Formed</th>\n<td>1987</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<th scope="row">Jurisdiction</th>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a></td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<th href="http://www.ec.gc.ca/ouragans-hurricanes/Default.asp?lang=En&amp;n=DA74FE64-1">http://www.ec.gc.ca/ouragans-hurricanes/Default.asp?lang=En&amp;n=DA74FE64-1</a></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p>The <b>Canadian Hurricane Centre</b> (<b>CHC</b>) is a division of the <a href="/wiki/Meteorological_Service_of_Canada" title="Meteorological Service of Canada">Meteorological Service of Canada</a>, an agency of <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Environment_Canada" class="mw-redirect" title="Environment Canada">Department of the Environment</a>, that advises <a href="/wiki/Canadians" title="Canadians">Canadians</a> on the threat of <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclones</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Hurricane" class="mw-redirect" title="Hurricane">hurricanes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tropical_storm" class="mw-redirect" title="Tropical storm">tropical storms</a>. Founded in 1987, CHC provides guidance to MSC\'s weather centres in eastern and <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Canada" title="Atlantic Canada">Atlantic Canada</a>, and is based in <a href="/wiki/Dartmouth,_Nova_Scotia" title="Dartmouth, Nova Scotia">Dartmouth, Nova Scotia</a>. CHC frequently consults with its <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> counterpart, the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> in Miami, to coordinate the tracks and positions of storms that <a href="/wiki/List_of_Canada_hurricanes" title="List of Canada hurricanes">pose a threat to Canada</a>.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#History"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">History</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Duties"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Duties</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#Frequency"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">Frequency</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#References"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Canadian_Hurricane_Centre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Gloria" title="Hurricane Gloria">Hurricane Gloria</a> and its less-than-expected damage prompted <a href="/wiki/Environment_Canada" class="mw-redirect" title="Environment Canada">Environment Canada</a> to create the CHC.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Its first <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_warning" class="mw-redirect" title="Hurricane warning">hurricane warnings</a> were issued in <a href="/wiki/2008_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2008 Atlantic hurricane season">2008</a> for <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Kyle_(2008)" title="Hurricane Kyle (2008)">Hurricane Kyle</a>, which struck near <a href="/wiki/Yarmouth,_Nova_Scotia" title="Yarmouth, Nova Scotia">Yarmouth, Nova Scotia</a> on September 28, 2008, and quickly became <a href="/wiki/Extratropical" class="mw-redirect" title="Extratropical">extratropical</a> while maintaining much of its strength into New Brunswick and <a href="/wiki/Labrador" title="Labrador">Labrador</a>.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Duties">Duties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Canadian_Hurricane_Centre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Duties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The organization gathers information on tropical and post-tropical cyclones (systems in the process of becoming <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclones" class="mw-redirect" title="Extratropical cyclones">extratropical cyclones</a>), predicts their evolution and assesses their potential impact on Canada. Their area of responsibility is bound by the Canada-United States border and extends into waters offshore Canada to 200 nautical miles (370&#160;km). Like other hurricane centers, the Canadian Hurricane Centre makes presentations about tropical cyclones to schools, businesses, the media, and other governmental agencies. They also coordinate with the public concerning addition queries about hurricanes in Canada.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Frequency">Frequency</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Canadian_Hurricane_Centre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Frequency">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>While <a href="/wiki/Hurricane" class="mw-redirect" title="Hurricane">hurricanes</a> are relatively uncommon in Canada, they do approach or strike from time to time. Recent occurrences include:</p>\n<ul>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Bob" title="Hurricane Bob">Hurricane Bob</a>, which struck <a href="/wiki/Campobello_Island,_New_Brunswick" class="mw-redirect" title="Campobello Island, New Brunswick">Campobello Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick" title="New Brunswick">New Brunswick</a>, in 1991.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Luis" title="Hurricane Luis">Hurricane Luis</a> which struck <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Newfoundland and Labrador">Newfoundland</a> in September 1995.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hortense" title="Hurricane Hortense">Hurricane Hortense</a> which struck <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a> in September 1996.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Michael_(2000)" title="Hurricane Michael (2000)">Hurricane Michael</a>, which struck Newfoundland in October 2000.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Juan" title="Hurricane Juan">Hurricane Juan</a>, which struck Nova Scotia in September 2003.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Igor" title="Hurricane Igor">Hurricane Igor</a>, which struck Newfoundland in September 2010.</li>\n</ul>\n<p>Also of note due to its rarity and the damage caused is <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hazel" title="Hurricane Hazel">Hurricane Hazel</a> which hit <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a> in 1954, which remained a strong storm even after crossing over the <a href="/wiki/U.S._East_Coast" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. East Coast">U.S. East Coast</a> states.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
