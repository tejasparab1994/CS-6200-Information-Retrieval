season</a></b></td>\n</tr>\n<tr style="display:none">\n<td colspan="2"></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Hurricane Iris</b> was the ninth <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">named storm</a> and fifth hurricane of the <a href="/wiki/1995_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1995 Atlantic hurricane season">1995 Atlantic hurricane season</a>. Iris was the first of three tropical cyclones to affect the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a> in a three-week period, preceding the more destructive hurricanes <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Luis" title="Hurricane Luis">Luis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Marilyn" title="Hurricane Marilyn">Marilyn</a>. It developed from a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> to the east of the Lesser Antilles on August&#160;22 and attained hurricane status within 30&#160;hours. The hurricane weakened to a tropical storm before crossing the islands of the eastern Caribbean from August&#160;26 through August&#160;28. During that time, Iris became one of four active tropical storms in the Atlantic basin. Earlier it had interacted with <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Humberto_(1995)" title="Hurricane Humberto (1995)">Hurricane Humberto</a>, and beginning on August&#160;30, Iris interacted with Tropical Storm Karen. Iris re-intensified into a hurricane and attained peak <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">sustained winds</a> of 110&#160;mph (175&#160;km/h) while moving slowly across the central Atlantic. The hurricane accelerated to the north and absorbed a dissipating Tropical Depression Karen on September&#160;3. Iris weakened to a tropical storm and became <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical</a> on September&#160;4, though its remnants reattained hurricane-force winds before affecting western Europe on September&#160;7.</p>\n<p>As a tropical storm, Iris produced heavy rainfall across much of the <a href="/wiki/Leeward_Islands" title="Leeward Islands">Leeward Islands</a>. In the southern Lesser Antilles, high waves caused coastal flooding in <a href="/wiki/Trinidad" title="Trinidad">Trinidad</a>, while in <a href="/wiki/Martinique" title="Martinique">Martinique</a> further north, significant amounts of precipitation led to flooding and landslides. The threat from the hurricane halted airplane evacuations on <a href="/wiki/Montserrat" title="Montserrat">Montserrat</a>, which was being threatened by the eruption of the <a href="/wiki/Soufri%C3%A8re_Hills" title="Soufri\xc3\xa8re Hills">Soufri\xc3\xa8re Hills</a> volcano. There were five deaths in association with Iris\xe2\x80\x94four in Martinique, and one in <a href="/wiki/Guadeloupe" title="Guadeloupe">Guadeloupe</a>.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc">\n<div history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Iris_1995_track.png" //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Iris_1995_track.png/440px-Iris_1995_track.png 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="713" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Iris_1995_track.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nMap plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>A <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> exited western <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> on August&#160;16, with a <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_circulation" title="Atmospheric circulation">circulation</a> emerging just south of <a href="/wiki/Dakar" title="Dakar">Dakar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">Senegal</a>. It was the first of four consecutive waves that would later develop into tropical cyclones. As the system moved westward, thunderstorms diminished on August&#160;18, before they gradually redeveloped. At around 1200&#160;<a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a>, the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> (NHC) classified the system as Tropical Depression Ten about 690&#160;mi (1,110&#160;km) east of the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Around that time, the depression had a well-organized area of convection and evident <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_circulation" title="Atmospheric circulation">circulation</a>, as confirmed by a nearby ship.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Within six hours of developing, the depression had intensified into a tropical storm.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Initially, <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_forecast_model" title="Tropical cyclone forecast model">tropical cyclone forecast models</a> had difficulty predicting the future of the storm, due to uncertain <a href="/wiki/Fujiwhara_effect" title="Fujiwhara effect">interaction</a> between it and <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Humberto_(1995)" title="Hurricane Humberto (1995)">Tropical Storm Humberto</a> to its northeast.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In real-time, the NHC upgraded the depression to Tropical Storm Iris at 1500&#160;UTC on August&#160;23,<sup id="cite_ref-disc5_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-disc5-4">[4]</a></sup> or about 21&#160;hours later than assessed in post-analysis. The intensity was based on <a href="/wiki/Dvorak_technique" title="Dvorak technique">satellite intensity estimates</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> At that time, the storm had a ragged <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)#Structure" title="Eye (cyclone)">central dense overcast</a>\xe2\x80\x94a uniformly circular area of thunderstorms\xe2\x80\x94as well as <a href="/wiki/Rainband#Tropical_cyclones" title="Rainband">rainbands</a> to the north and south.<sup id="cite_ref-disc5_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-disc5-4">[4]</a></sup> A <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_hunters" title="Hurricane hunters">hurricane hunters</a> flight late on August&#160;23 indicated that Iris was significantly stronger, reporting 10\xe2\x80\x93second sustained winds of 106&#160;mph (170&#160;km/h) at flight-level.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Based on the reading, it is estimated Iris attained hurricane status around 1800&#160;UTC that day, or about three hours after it was <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">named</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-disc5_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-disc5-4">[4]</a></sup></p>\n<p>After Iris strengthened into a hurricane, it turned to the west-southwest due to interaction with Hurricane Humberto. An upper-level low north of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> increased <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a> over the hurricane, which dislocated the center from the deep convection. As a result, Iris weakened to tropical storm status on August&#160;24 after being a hurricane for about 24&#160;hours.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> As the storm approached the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a>, the thunderstorms decreased markedly and the cloud pattern became disorganized.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> As late as August&#160;25, there was uncertainty whether Iris would continue toward the islands or turn to the north.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> A new circulation became the dominant center as the storm continued westward, and Iris brushed <a href="/wiki/Saint_Lucia" title="Saint Lucia">Saint Lucia</a> early on August&#160;26. An approaching <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a> turned the storm to the northwest, bringing it near most of the Lesser Antilles. Early on August&#160;27, Iris weakened to an intensity of 40&#160;mph (64&#160;km/h), although it immediately began restrengthening. Later that day, the center made <a href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> on <a href="/wiki/Montserrat" title="Montserrat">Montserrat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anguilla" title="Anguilla">Anguilla</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Barbuda" title="Barbuda">Barbuda</a> with winds of over 60&#160;mph (95&#160;km/h).<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> By that time, an eye began reforming,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and the structure became better organized as wind shear decreased.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a that would ultimately become Karen and <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Luis" title="Hurricane Luis">Luis</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>On August&#160;28, Iris moved away from the Lesser Antilles, and at 1800&#160;UTC re-attained hurricane status as it began a steady motion to the north-northwest.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> By that time, there was uncertainty in its future track due to the possible interactions among Iris, Humberto to the northeast, Tropical Storm Karen to the southeast, and the remnants of <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Jerry_(1995)" title="Tropical Storm Jerry (1995)">Tropical Storm Jerry</a> to the west.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In addition, after <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Luis" title="Hurricane Luis">Tropical Storm Luis</a> formed on August&#160;27, there were four active tropical cyclones in the Atlantic.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> A few days later, there were three simultaneous hurricanes, which is a rare event, when Luis attained hurricane status.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On August&#160;30, Iris turned to the northeast while it began interacting directly with Tropical Storm Karen. Over the next few days, the smaller tropical storm moved around the larger circulation of Iris, potentially causing the hurricane to move erratically.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Iris\' intensity did not change significantly during that time, and it maintained strong <a href="/wiki/Outflow_(meteorology)" title="Outflow (meteorology)">outflow</a> but a weak eyewall. An eastward-moving trough bypassed the storm to the north, causing the motion to become nearly stationary.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> A building <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">ridge</a> to the northeast caused Iris to turn to a northwest drift on September&#160;1. By that time, the eye had become distinct and well-organized,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and at 0600&#160;UTC that day, Iris attained peak winds of 110&#160;mph (175&#160;km/h) to the southeast of Bermuda.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>After reaching peak intensity, the hurricane began weakening due to increasing shear and cooler waters.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_1-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> An approaching trough turned Iris to the north,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> and early on September&#160;3 the hurricane passed about 350&#160;mi (560&#160;km) east of Bermuda.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> That day, Iris absorbed the dissipating Tropical Depression Karen.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_1-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> It accelerated to the northeast as it lost tropical characteristics, and after weakening to a tropical storm, Iris transitioned into an <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical cyclone</a> on September&#160;4 to the southeast of <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_(island)" title="Newfoundland (island)">Newfoundland</a>. The remnants turned to the east,<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_1-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> moving in tandem with an extratropical storm to its north.<sup id="cite_ref-tj_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tj-16">[16]</a></sup> On September&#160;5, the <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" title="Atmospheric pressure">barometric pressure</a> dropped by more than 24 millibars (0.71&#160;inHg), which qualified as being classified as a meteorological <a href="/wiki/Bomb_(meteorology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Bomb (meteorology)">bomb</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tj_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tj-16">[16]</a></sup> The next day, the winds strengthened to hurricane-status,<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_1-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> and the storm maintained a track to the east due to a ridge weakening to the north. The pressure reached a minimum of 957&#160;mb (28.3&#160;inHg) early on September&#160;7, lower than while Iris was tropical.<sup id="cite_ref-tj_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tj-16">[16]</a></sup> That day, the storm weakened as it entered the <a href="/wiki/English_Channel" title="English Channel">English Channel</a>, and the extratropical remnants continued across western Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_1-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations_and_impact">Preparations and impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Iris_(1995)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations and impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Radar_image_of_Hurricane_Iris_1995.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/Radar_image_of_Hurricane_Iris_1995.jpg/220px-Radar_image_of_Hurricane_Iris_1995.jpg" width="220" height="273" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/Radar_image_of_Hurricane_Iris_1995.jpg/330px-Radar_image_of_Hurricane_Iris_1995.jpg 1.5x, class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Radar_image_of_Hurricane_Iris_1995.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\n<a href="/wiki/Weather_radar" title="Weather radar">Weather radar</a> image of Hurricane Iris on August&#160;29</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Before Iris moved through the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">tropical storm watches</a>, and later warnings, were issued from <a href="/wiki/Barbados" title="Barbados">Barbados</a> through the <a href="/wiki/British_Virgin_Islands" title="British Virgin Islands">British Virgin Islands</a>. The storm produced tropical storm force winds across the eastern Caribbean, although the primary meteorological event occurred from heavy rainfall.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_1-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Flooding prompted evacuations in communities in Saint Lucia, Dominica, and Saint Vincent and the Grenadines.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Iris was the first of three storms in a three-week period to affect the region, preceding the more destructive hurricanes <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Luis" title="Hurricane Luis">Luis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Marilyn" title="Hurricane Marilyn">Marilyn</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In western <a href="/wiki/Trinidad" title="Trinidad">Trinidad</a>, a feeder produced winds of 37&#160;mph (60&#160;km/h) along the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Paria" title="Gulf of Paria">Gulf of Paria</a>. The winds increased waves that caused coastal flooding and some damage to boats.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/Martinique" title="Martinique">Martinique</a>, Iris produced gusts of 56&#160;mph (90&#160;km/h), with torrential rainfall occurring on the island. A station in <a href="/wiki/Les_Trois-%C3%8Elets" title="Les Trois-\xc3\x8elets">Les Trois-\xc3\x8elets</a> recorded 1.89&#160;in (48&#160;mm) in a 30&#160;minute period, and the highest total on the island was 17.72&#160;in (450&#160;mm) at <a href="/wiki/Ducos" title="Ducos">Ducos</a>. The rains caused mudslides that killed four people,<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_1-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> including two after a house was swept off a cliff in <a href="/wiki/Le_Vauclin" title="Le Vauclin">Le Vauclin</a>. Flooding was reported across coastal areas of Martinique, and heavy damage was reported in the southern city of <a href="/wiki/Le_Vauclin" title="Le Vauclin">Le Vauclin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> To the north of Martinique, winds reached 43&#160;mph (69&#160;km/h) on Dominica.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_1-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> While the storm passed <a href="/wiki/Guadeloupe" title="Guadeloupe">Guadeloupe</a>, it produced sustained winds of 45&#160;mph (72&#160;km/h), with gusts to 65&#160;mph (105&#160;km/h) on <a href="/wiki/La_D%C3%A9sirade" title="La D\xc3\xa9sirade">La D\xc3\xa9sirade</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> There was one death on the island after a person drowned in a storm-flooded river.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> To the northwest, the hurricane moved over <a href="/wiki/Montserrat" title="Montserrat">Montserrat</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_1-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> causing additional problems on the island just weeks after the <a href="/wiki/Soufri%C3%A8re_Hills" title="Soufri\xc3\xa8re Hills">Soufri\xc3\xa8re Hills</a> volcano began erupting.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Officials on the island closed the <a href="/wiki/W._H._Bramble_Airport" title="W. H. Bramble Airport">primary airport</a> due to the storm, which prevented residents from evacuating from the island from the volcano threat.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Further north, Iris dropped 6&#160;in (150&#160;mm) of rain on <a href="/wiki/Antigua" title="Antigua">Antigua</a>, which destroyed banana trees and caused flooding in low-lying areas.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_1-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Due to uncertainties in Iris\' track, the government of <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> issued a tropical storm watch on September&#160;1. This was downgraded on September&#160;3 after the hurricane bypassed the island.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_1-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Later, after Iris became extratropical, the storm produced winds of 45&#160;mph (72&#160;km/h) at <a href="/wiki/La_Rochelle" title="La Rochelle">La Rochelle</a> on the Atlantic coast of <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tj_16-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tj-16">[16]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
