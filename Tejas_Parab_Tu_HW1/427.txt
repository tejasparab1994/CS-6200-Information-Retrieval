message</a>)</i></small></div>\n</td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Hurricane engineering</b> is a specialist sub-discipline of <a href="/wiki/Civil_engineering" title="Civil engineering">civil engineering</a> that encompasses planning, analysis, design, response, and recovery of civil engineering systems and infrastructure for <a href="/wiki/Hurricane" class="mw-redirect" title="Hurricane">hurricane</a> hazards. Hurricane engineering is a relatively new and emerging discipline within the field of civil engineering. It is an integration of many recognized branches of engineering, such as <a href="/wiki/Structural_engineering" title="Structural engineering">structural engineering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wind_engineering" title="Wind engineering">wind engineering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coastal_engineering" title="Coastal engineering">coastal engineering</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Forensic_engineering" title="Forensic engineering">forensic engineering</a>, with other recognized sciences and planning functions such as, <a href="/wiki/Climatology" title="Climatology">climatology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oceanography" title="Oceanography">oceanography</a>, <a href="/wiki/Architecture" title="Architecture">architecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Emergency_management" title="Emergency management">emergency management</a> and preparedness, hazard mitigation, and hazard vulnerability analysis. Hurricane engineering aims to minimize risks to human safety, the natural and built environment, and business processes.</p>\n<p>As a result of the tremendous threats to life safety and economic disruptions caused by the <a href="/wiki/2004_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2004 Atlantic hurricane season">2004</a> and <a href="/wiki/2005_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2005 Atlantic hurricane season">2005</a> hurricane seasons, governmental organizations, such as the United States <a href="/wiki/National_Science_Foundation" title="National Science Foundation">National Science Foundation</a>, have recognized the need to better understand hurricane threats and further establish this discipline. In September 2006, the <a href="/wiki/National_Science_Board" title="National Science Board">National Science Board</a> released recommendations to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">United States Congress</a> calling for major new investments in hurricane science and engineering.</p>\n<p>Accredited university engineering programs, such as the <a href="/wiki/Louisiana_State_University" title="Louisiana State University">Louisiana State University</a> civil engineering department and <a href="/wiki/University_of_Notre_Dame" title="University of Notre Dame">University of Notre Dame</a> Department of Civil Engineering and Geological Sciences, are establishing programs to better understand these catastrophic storms and their interaction with the environment. The LSU Hurricane Center has begun to offer hurricane engineering courses with the focus of educating students on the unique threats caused by hurricanes.</p>\n<p>In the Northwest Pacific, where the term for strong tropical cyclones is <b>Typhoon,</b> instead of Hurricane, the concept of Typhoon Engineering, which is very similar to Hurricane Engineering, is being proposed.</p>\n<p>The past two decades have witnessed exponential growth in damage due to hurricanes and the situation continues to deteriorate. The most vulnerable areas, coastal counties along the Gulf and Atlantic seaboards, are experiencing greater population growth and development than anyplace else in the country. If the trend of rapidly increasing losses caused by hurricanes is to be reversed, a whole new philosophy of understanding, planning, and preparedness is required. The Hurricane Engineering curriculum is the result of a multidisciplinary project aimed at giving engineering students a comprehensive understanding of the hazards associated with hurricanes:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>Extreme winds</li>\n<li>Windborne debris</li>\n<li>Storm surges</li>\n<li>River flooding</li>\n<li>Rain-induced landslides</li>\n</ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul>\n<li><cite id="CITEREFLevitan2005" class="citation">Levitan, M.
