class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:276px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Iniki.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Hurricane_Iniki.jpg/274px-Hurricane_Iniki.jpg" width="274" height="206" class="thumbimage" class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Iniki.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nOn September 11, 1992, <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Iniki" title="Hurricane Iniki">Hurricane Iniki</a> caused more than US$3&#160;billion of damage in Hawai\'i.</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>The <b>Central Pacific Hurricane Center</b> (<b>CPHC</b>) of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">National Weather Service</a> is the official body responsible for tracking and issuing <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> warnings, watches, advisories, discussions, and statements for the Central Pacific region: from the equator northward, 140\xc2\xb0W\xe2\x80\x93180\xc2\xb0W, most significantly for Hawai\xe2\x80\x98i. It is the <a href="/wiki/Regional_Specialized_Meteorological_Center" title="Regional Specialized Meteorological Center">Regional Specialized Meteorological Center</a> (RSMC) for tropical cyclones in this region, and in this capacity is known as <b>RSMC Honolulu</b>.</p>\n<p>Based in <a href="/wiki/Honolulu,_Hawaii" class="mw-redirect" title="Honolulu, Hawaii">Honolulu, Hawai\xe2\x80\x98i</a>, the CPHC is co-located with the National Weather Service\'s Honolulu forecast office on the campus of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Hawaii_at_Manoa" title="University of Hawaii at Manoa">University of Hawai\xe2\x80\x98i at M\xc4\x81noa</a>. The Honolulu forecast office activates the CPHC when tropical cyclones form in, or move into, the Central Pacific region. The CPHC replaced the previous forecaster, the Joint Hurricane Warning Center, starting in the <a href="/wiki/1970_Pacific_hurricane_season" title="1970 Pacific hurricane season">1970 season</a>.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Area_of_responsibility"><span href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n<p></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Area_of_responsibility">Area of responsibility</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Central_Pacific_Hurricane_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Area of responsibility">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:447px;"><a href="/wiki/File:7_zones_dels_ciclons_tropicals.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/7_zones_dels_ciclons_tropicals.jpg/445px-7_zones_dels_ciclons_tropicals.jpg" width="445" height="185" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/7_zones_dels_ciclons_tropicals.jpg/668px-7_zones_dels_ciclons_tropicals.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/7_zones_dels_ciclons_tropicals.jpg/890px-7_zones_dels_ciclons_tropicals.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1780" data-file-height="738" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:7_zones_dels_ciclons_tropicals.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nAreas of responsibility, showing the CPHC\'s area, containing part of the Eastern Pacific basin (2) and Western Pacific basin (3).</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>The CPHC\'s area of responsibility is the Central Pacific (CP) region, which is an administrative region, not a meteorological one. It is not a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_basin" class="mw-redirect" title="Tropical cyclone basin">tropical cyclone basin</a> (a distinct area where cyclones form), but is still often referred to as the Central Pacific <i>basin</i> or Central North Pacific <i>basin</i>. The western edge of the area of responsibility, 180\xc2\xb0W, is formally the <a href="/wiki/Antimeridian" class="mw-redirect" title="Antimeridian">antimeridian</a>, though this coincides with the <a href="/wiki/International_Date_Line" title="International Date Line">International Date Line</a> for tropical latitudes, and thus these are often conflated. Meteorologically, this region covers the western part of the <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_basins#Eastern_Pacific" title="Tropical cyclone basins">Eastern Pacific basin</a> and the eastern part of the <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_basins#Western_Pacific" title="Tropical cyclone basins">Western Pacific basin</a>, though administratively the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> is responsible for the Eastern Pacific <i>basin</i> east of 140\xc2\xb0W, and thus the Eastern Pacific <i>region</i> (EP) typically refers to region east of 140\xc2\xb0W, not the whole meteorological basin. The region east of 140\xc2\xb0W was formerly the responsibility of the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Pacific_Hurricane_Center" title="Eastern Pacific Hurricane Center">Eastern Pacific Hurricane Center</a>; like the CPHC, it took responsibility in 1970, but it is now folded into the NHC.</p>\n<p>In this area, the hurricane season lasts from June 1 through November 30. Practically, storms may form in the Eastern Pacific region (east or west of 140\xc2\xb0W) and move west, possibly affecting Hawai\xe2\x80\x98i, or in the Western Pacific basin and move west, possibly affecting Asia. Smaller islands may also be affected, though this region is otherwise very sparsely populated.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hurricane_naming_system">Hurricane naming system</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Central_Pacific_Hurricane_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Hurricane naming system">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/List_of_tropical_cyclone_names" class="mw-redirect" title="List of tropical cyclone names">List of tropical cyclone names</a></div>\n<table class="infobox bordered" style="width: 250px; font-size: 95%; float: right;" cellpadding="2" cellspacing="0">\n<caption><span style="font-size:140%;"><b>Naming List</b></span></caption>\n<tr style="background:#efefef;">\n<th>List 1</th>\n<th>List 2</th>\n<th>List 3</th>\n<th>List 4</th>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td>Akoni</td>\n<td>Aka</td>\n<td>Alika</td>\n<td>Ana</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td>Ema</td>\n<td>Ekeka</td>\n<td>Ele</td>\n<td>Ela</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td>Hone</td>\n<td>Hene</td>\n<td>Huko</td>\n<td>Halola</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td>Iona</td>\n<td>Iolana</td>\n<td>Iopa</td>\n<td>Iune</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td>Keli</td>\n<td>Keoni</td>\n<td>Kika</td>\n<td>Kilo</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td>Lala</td>\n<td>Lino</td>\n<td>Lana</td>\n<td>Loke</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td>Moke</td>\n<td>Mele</td>\n<td>Maka</td>\n<td>Malia</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td>Nolo</td>\n<td>Nona</td>\n<td>Neki</td>\n<td>Niala</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td>Olana</td>\n<td>Oliwa</td>\n<td>Omeka</td>\n<td>Oho</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td>Pena</td>\n<td>Pama</td>\n<td>Pewa</td>\n<td>Pali</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td>Ulana</td>\n<td>Upana</td>\n<td>Unala</td>\n<td>Ulika</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td>Wale</td>\n<td>Wene</td>\n<td>Wali</td>\n<td>Walaka</td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p>The Central Pacific Hurricane Center uses traditional <a href="/wiki/Hawaiian_language" title="Hawaiian language">Hawaiian</a> names for hurricanes that form within its regional sphere of jurisdiction. It has formed four lists of names to choose from. As soon as all the names are exhausted from the first list, it moves on to the second, then third, then fourth, then back to the first and so on. Unlike the name list in the Atlantic and Eastern Pacific, the names do not start at "A" every year. Four names have been retired, <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Iwa" title="Hurricane Iwa">Iwa of 1982</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Iniki" title="Hurricane Iniki">Iniki of 1992</a>, <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Paka" title="Typhoon Paka">Paka of 1997</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Ioke" title="Hurricane Ioke">Ioke of 2006</a>. They were replaced by Io (which was later changed to Iona), Iolana, Pama and Iopa respectively. The next name to be used from the list is \'Walaka\'.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Forecasting_system">Forecasting system</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
