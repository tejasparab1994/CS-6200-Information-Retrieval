classification">K\xc3\xb6ppen climate classification</a> is a non-<a href="/wiki/Arid" title="Arid">arid</a> climate in which all twelve months have mean temperatures of at least 18&#160;\xc2\xb0C (64&#160;\xc2\xb0F). In tropical climates there are often only two seasons: a wet season and a dry season. Tropical climates are frost-free, and changes in the solar angle are small. title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>).</p>\n<p>(<b>Note</b>. The term <a href="/wiki/Season" title="Season">aseasonal</a> refers to the lack in the tropical zone of large differences in daylight hours and mean monthly (or daily) temperature throughout the year. Annual cyclic changes occur in the tropics, but not as predictably as those in the temperate zone, albeit unrelated to temperature, but to water availability whether as rain, mist, soil, or ground water. Plant response (e. g., <a href="/wiki/Phenology" title="Phenology">phenology</a>), animal (feeding, migration, reproduction, etc.), and human activities (plant sowing, harvesting, hunting, fishing, etc.) are tuned to this \'seasonality\'. Indeed, in tropical South America and Central America, the \'rainy season\' (and the \'high water season\') is called <a href="/wiki/Rainy_season" class="mw-redirect" title="Rainy season"><i>invierno</i></a> or <i>inverno</i>, though it could occur in the Northern Hemisphere summer; likewise, the \'dry season\' (and \'low water season\') is called <a href="/wiki/Dry_season" title="Dry season"><i>verano</i></a> or <i>ver\xc3\xa3o</i>, and can occur in the Northern Hemisphere winter).</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tropical_monsoon_climate">Tropical monsoon climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_climate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Tropical monsoon climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Tropical_monsoon_climate" title="Tropical monsoon climate">Tropical monsoon climate</a></div>\n<p>This type of climate results from the <a href="/wiki/Monsoon" title="Monsoon">monsoon</a> winds which change direction according to the seasons. are found at the outer margins of the <a href="/wiki/Tropical" class="mw-redirect" title="Tropical">tropical</a> zone from the low teens to the mid-20s latitudes, but occasionally an inner-tropical location (e.g., <a href="/wiki/San_Marcos,_Antioquia" title="San Marcos, Antioquia">San Marcos, Antioquia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>) also qualifies. Actually, the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_sea" class="mw-redirect" title="Caribbean sea">Caribbean</a> coast, eastward from the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Urab%C3%A1" title="Gulf of Urab\xc3\xa1">Gulf of Urab\xc3\xa1</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>\xe2\x80\x93<a href="/wiki/Panam%C3%A1" class="mw-redirect" title="Panam\xc3\xa1">Panam\xc3\xa1</a> border to the <a href="/wiki/Orinoco_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Orinoco River">Orinoco River</a> delta, on the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> (about 4,000&#160;km), have long dry periods (the extreme is the <i>BSh</i> climate (see below), characterised by very low, unreliable precipitation, present, for instance, in extensive areas in the <a href="https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guajira_Pen%C3%ADnsula" class="extiw" title="es:Guajira Pen\xc3\xadnsula">Guajira</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Coro_region" title="Coro region">Coro</a>, western <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>, the northernmost peninsulas in South America, which receive &lt;300&#160;mm total annual precipitation, practically all in two or three months).</p>\n<p>This condition extends to the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Greater_Antilles" title="Greater Antilles">Greater Antilles</a> forming the circum-Caribbean dry belt. The length and severity of the dry season diminishes inland (southward); at the latitude of the Amazon River\xe2\x80\x94which flows eastward, just south of the <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">equatorial line</a>\xe2\x80\x94the climate is <i>Af</i>. East from the <a href="/wiki/Andes" title="Andes">Andes</a>, between the dry, arid Caribbean and the ever-wet Amazon are the Orinoco River\'s <a href="/wiki/Los_Llanos,_Venezuela" class="mw-redirect" title="Los Llanos, Venezuela">llanos</a> or <a href="/wiki/Savanna" title="Savanna">savannas</a>, from where this climate takes its name.</p>\n<p>Sometimes <i>As</i> is used in place of <i>Aw</i> if the dry season occurs during the time of higher sun and longer days. This is the case in parts of <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a>, northwestern Dominican Republic (<a href="/wiki/Monte_Cristi_(Dominican_Republic)" class="mw-redirect" title="Monte Cristi (Dominican Republic)">Monte Cristi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Villa_V%C3%A1squez" title="Villa V\xc3\xa1squez">Villa V\xc3\xa1squez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luper%C3%B3n,_Puerto_Plata" class="mw-redirect" title="Luper\xc3\xb3n, Puerto Plata">Luper\xc3\xb3n</a>), East Africa (<a href="/wiki/Mombasa" title="Mombasa">Mombasa</a>, Kenya), <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a> (<a href="/wiki/Trincomalee" title="Trincomalee">Trincomalee</a>), and Brazilian Northeastern Coast (<a href="/wiki/Recife" title="Recife">Recife</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jo%C3%A3o_Pessoa,_Para%C3%ADba" title="Jo\xc3\xa3o Pessoa, Para\xc3\xadba">Jo\xc3\xa3o Pessoa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Natal,_Rio_Grande_do_Norte" title="Natal, Rio Grande do Norte">Natal</a>), for instance. In most places that have tropical wet and dry climates, however, the dry season occurs during the time of lower sun and shorter days because of <a href="/wiki/Rain_shadow" title="Rain shadow">rain shadow</a> effects during the \'high-sun\' part of the year.</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Exceptions">Exceptions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_climate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Exceptions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Owing to different causes certain places within the tropics do not have a tropical climate, such as some desert areas and alpine regions.</p>\n<ul>\n<li>Examples:\n<ul>\n<li>Southern part of <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabian Peninsula</a>.</li>\n<li>The <a href="/wiki/Sahara_Desert" class="mw-redirect" title="Sahara Desert">Sahara Desert</a>.</li>\n<li>Alpine regions of the tropics. Mountaintops within the tropics, e.g. <a href="/wiki/Mount_Kenya" title="Mount Kenya">Mount Kenya</a>, can be cold. However, like lowlands in the tropics (and unlike cold winter <a href="/wiki/Temperate_zone" class="mw-redirect" title="Temperate zone">temperate zone</a> regions), there is little seasonal variation of temperature.</li>\n</ul>\n</li>\n</ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Intertropical_Convergence_Zone">Intertropical Convergence Zone</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_climate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Intertropical Convergence Zone">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:352px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Omega-500-july-era40-1979.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Omega-500-july-era40-1979.png/350px-Omega-500-july-era40-1979.png" width="350" height="184" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Omega-500-july-era40-1979.png/525px-Omega-500-july-era40-1979.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Omega-500-july-era40-1979.png/700px-Omega-500-july-era40-1979.png 2x" data-file-width="717" data-file-height="376" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Omega-500-july-era40-1979.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\n<a href="/wiki/Intertropical_Convergence_Zone" title="Intertropical Convergence Zone">Intertropical Convergence Zone</a> vertical velocity at 500 hPa, July average in units of <a href="/wiki/Pascal_(unit)" title="Pascal (unit)">pascals</a> per second. Ascent (negative values) is <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">equator</a>; descent (positive values) is more diffuse.</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Because of the <a href="/wiki/Effect_of_sun_angle_on_climate" class="mw-redirect" title="Effect of sun angle on climate">effect of sun angle on climate</a> most areas within the tropics are hot year-round, with diurnal variations in temperature exceeding seasonal variations. Seasonal variations in tropical climate are dominated by changes in precipitation, which are in turn largely influenced by the <a href="/wiki/Tropical_rain_belt" title="Tropical rain belt">tropical rain belt</a> or <a href="/wiki/Intertropical_Convergence_Zone" title="Intertropical Convergence Zone">Intertropical Convergence Zone</a> (ITCZ), a portion of the <a href="/wiki/Hadley_cell" title="Hadley cell">Hadley cell</a>. The ITCZ is shown, for July average, in the graphic. Areas of ascending air have heavy rainfall; areas of descending air are dry. The ITCZ somewhat follows the solar equator throughout the year, but with geographical variations, and in some areas (India) is heavily influenced by local large-scale <a href="/wiki/Monsoon" title="Monsoon">monsoons</a>.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_climate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Tropical_ecology" title="Tropical ecology">Tropical ecology</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Tropical_vegetation" title="Tropical vegetation">Tropical vegetation</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Tropics" title="Tropics">Tropics</a></li>\n</ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_climate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap">\n<ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-McKnight-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink">^ <a href="#cite_ref-McKnight_1-0"><sup><i><b>a</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-McKnight_1-1"><sup><i><b>b</b></i></sup></a></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation book">McKnight, Tom L; Hess, Darrel (2000).
