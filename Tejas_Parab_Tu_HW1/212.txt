style="display:none">\n<td colspan="2"></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Hurricane Ophelia</b> (known as <b>Storm Ophelia</b> in <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Ireland</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> while <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical</a>) was the easternmost <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_hurricane" title="Atlantic hurricane">Atlantic</a> <a href="/wiki/Major_hurricane" class="mw-redirect" title="Major hurricane">major hurricane</a> on record. The tenth consecutive hurricane and the sixth major hurricane of the <a href="/wiki/2017_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2017 Atlantic hurricane season">2017 Atlantic hurricane season</a>, Ophelia had non-tropical origins from a decaying cold front on 6 October. Located within a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone#Factors" title="Tropical cyclone">favorable environment</a>, the storm steadily strengthened over the next two days, drifting north and then southeastwards before becoming a hurricane on 11 October. After becoming a Category 2 hurricane and fluctuating in intensity for a day, Ophelia unexpectedly intensified into a major hurricane on 14 October south of the <a href="/wiki/Azores" title="Azores">Azores</a>, brushing the archipelago with high winds and heavy rainfall.<sup id="cite_ref-auto1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto1-1">[1]</a></sup> Shortly after achieving peak intensity, Ophelia entered a steady weakening phase as it accelerated to the northeast towards <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>. Completing an extratropical transition early on 16 October, Ophelia became the second storm of the <a href="/wiki/2017%E2%80%9318_UK_and_Ireland_windstorm_season" title="2017\xe2\x80\x9318 UK and Ireland windstorm season">2017\xe2\x80\x9318 UK and Ireland windstorm season</a>. Early on 17 October, the cyclone crossed the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a> and struck <a href="/wiki/Western_Norway" title="Western Norway">western Norway</a>, with wind gusts up to 70 kilometres per hour (43&#160;mph) in <a href="/wiki/Rogaland" title="Rogaland">Rogaland county</a>, before weakening during the evening.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The system made additional landfalls in <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> and <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a> before dissipating over <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>\n<p>As of 21 October, only three deaths can be attributed directly to Ophelia and its remnants, all of which occurred in Ireland, one man and a woman were killed in two separate instances where trees fell onto people\'s cars. Another man was killed in a chainsaw incident while cutting a tree that was blocking a road.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> A further 48 could be attributed very indirectly to an increase in wind by Ophelia tracking over the ocean, East of the Azores, helping fan the <a href="/wiki/October_2017_Iberian_wildfires" title="October 2017 Iberian wildfires">wildfires on the Iberian Peninsula</a> which were set independently from the storm. The total economic losses from the storm are estimated at 1 billion <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">euros</a> ($1.18 billion USD),<sup id="cite_ref-financial_storm_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-financial_storm-6">[6]</a></sup> mostly by the lack of economic activity during the storm as the insured damages are only estimated to be around 111 million euros (US$131&#160;million).<sup id="cite_ref-Insured_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Insured-7">[7]</a></sup></p>\n<p></p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc">\n<div class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ophelia_2017_track.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/Ophelia_2017_track.png/220px-Ophelia_2017_track.png" width="220" height="159" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/Ophelia_2017_track.png/330px-Ophelia_2017_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/Ophelia_2017_track.png/440px-Ophelia_2017_track.png 2x" data-file-width="3000" data-file-height="2162" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ophelia_2017_track.png" class="internal" title="Agrandir"></a></div>\nMap plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>On 6 October, the United States\' <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> (NHC) began monitoring the tail end of a decaying <a href="/wiki/Cold_front" title="Cold front">cold front</a> for possible subtropical or <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclogenesis" title="Tropical cyclogenesis">tropical cyclogenesis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-8">[8]</a></sup> On the same day, a circulation developed on the periphery of this front.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-8">[8]</a></sup> Soon afterwards, a non-tropical low developed within the circulation, and drifted to the southwest before becoming nearly stationary.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The system began to acquire <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_cyclone" title="Subtropical cyclone">subtropical characteristics</a> on the next day, and the chances of development were raised to a high percentage of <a href="/wiki/Cyclogenesis" title="Cyclogenesis">cyclogenesis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> After a brief loss of organization and diminished convection, due to moderate <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> the system continued to steadily organize, and developed a well-defined circulation center early on 9 October, as deep convection began to persist near the center.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> By 09:00&#160;UTC that day, the convection had persisted long enough for the system to be classified as a tropical depression about 875&#160;mi (1,410&#160;km) west-southwest of the <a href="/wiki/Azores" title="Azores">Azores</a>, and the storm was identified as Tropical Depression Seventeen.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> A <a href="/wiki/Rainband" title="Rainband">curved banding feature</a> wrapped around the center as the <a href="/wiki/Weather_satellite" title="Weather satellite">satellite presentation</a> improved, leading to the upgrade to Tropical Storm Ophelia six hours later.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:17L_2017-10-09_1335Z.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/17L_2017-10-09_1335Z.jpg/220px-17L_2017-10-09_1335Z.jpg" width="220" height="173" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/17L_2017-10-09_1335Z.jpg/330px-17L_2017-10-09_1335Z.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/17L_2017-10-09_1335Z.jpg/440px-17L_2017-10-09_1335Z.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2800" data-file-height="2200" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:17L_2017-10-09_1335Z.jpg" class="internal" title="Agrandir"></a></div>\nTropical Depression Seventeen organizing in the Atlantic Ocean on 9 October</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Despite marginally warm <a href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperature" title="Sea surface temperature">sea surface temperatures</a> of around 79&#160;\xc2\xb0F (26&#160;\xc2\xb0C), the effects of below-average temperatures aloft and low wind shear allowed Ophelia to gradually strengthen; with banding features becoming more prominent as it drifted northwards in an area of weak steering currents.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In addition, the large temperature contrast between the abnormally-warm seawater and the extremely cold temperatures in the upper atmosphere provided instability for Ophelia\'s thunderstorms, which allowed the storm to continue strengthening, despite being over marginally-warm ocean temperatures.<sup id="cite_ref-extremely_weird_storm_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-extremely_weird_storm-17">[17]</a></sup> A slight degradation of the structure of the storm resulted in some weakening early on 11 October,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> but this was short-lived as deep convection wrapped around the entire storm. It was upgraded to a hurricane at 21:00&#160;UTC that day after a ragged <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a> developed, becoming the record-tying tenth consecutive hurricane to form during the 2017 hurricane season; Ophelia was the fourth such occurrence since the <a href="/wiki/1878_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1878 Atlantic hurricane season">1878</a>, <a href="/wiki/1887_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1887 Atlantic hurricane season">1887</a>, and <a href="/wiki/1893_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1893 Atlantic hurricane season">1893 seasons</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[nb 1]</a></sup> Afterwards, Ophelia steadily intensified as it became nearly stationary, intensifying into a Category&#160;2 hurricane on the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale</a> on 12 October, as the <a href="/wiki/Central_dense_overcast" title="Central dense overcast">central dense overcast</a> became more symmetric and the eye became better defined.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[21]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The hurricane then weakened slightly the next day as cloud tops warmed,<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[22]</a></sup> only to quickly strengthen as the eye became much more defined and cloud tops cooled significantly due to <a href="/wiki/Outflow_(meteorology)" title="Outflow (meteorology)">outflow</a> from a strong <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a> to the northeast. The NHC upgraded the storm to a major hurricane (Category 3 or higher) at 15:00&#160;UTC on 14 October, the farthest east that a storm of such intensity had been observed in the satellite era.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-RecordEast_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RecordEast-25">[24]</a></sup> At the same time, it attained its peak intensity with winds of 115&#160;mph (185&#160;km/h) and a minimum pressure of 960&#160;millibars (28.35&#160;inHg) while located approximately 235&#160;miles (375&#160;km) southeast of the Azores. After peaking in intensity, increasing shear and dry air from an encroaching frontal boundary began to disrupt the core of the storm, which caused Ophelia to begin weakening early on 15 October as it began its extratropical transition, with the eye collapsing.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[25]</a></sup> The process accelerated as Ophelia became further embedded in the trough, and ultimately, the storm became extratropical at 03:00&#160;UTC on the next day.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[26]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span
