id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sakuhei_Fujiwhara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sakuhei_Fujiwhara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Born in the city of <a href="/wiki/Suwa,_Nagano" title="Suwa, Nagano">Suwa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nagano_Prefecture" title="Nagano Prefecture">Nagano Prefecture</a>, Fujiwhara received his primary education at Takashima Common Elementary School and Suwa Higher Elementary School, where he was in the same class as future army general, <a href="/wiki/Tetsuzan_Nagata" title="Tetsuzan Nagata">Tetsuzan Nagata</a>. He was also close friends with <a href="/wiki/Shigeo_Iwanami" title="Shigeo Iwanami">Shigeo Iwanami</a>, who would go on to found the <a href="/wiki/Iwanami_Shoten_Publishing" class="mw-redirect" title="Iwanami Shoten Publishing">Iwanami Shoten Publishing</a> company. He joined the Central Meteorological Observatory (current <a href="/wiki/Japan_Meteorological_Agency" title="Japan Meteorological Agency">Japan Meteorological Agency</a>) in 1909 after completing undergraduate studies in <a href="/wiki/Theoretical_physics" title="Theoretical physics">theoretical physics</a> at <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Imperial_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Tokyo Imperial University">Tokyo Imperial University</a> (now the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Tokyo" title="University of Tokyo">University of Tokyo</a>).</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Academic_career">Academic career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sakuhei_Fujiwhara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Academic career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Fujiwhara earned his <a href="/wiki/Doctorate" title="Doctorate">doctorate</a> in 1915 through his research work on the abnormal propagation of sound waves, and earned the <a href="/wiki/Japan_Academy_Prize_(academics)" title="Japan Academy Prize (academics)">Japan Academy Prize</a> in 1920 in recognition of his research. He traveled to <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a> in the same year to study <a href="/wiki/Meteorology" title="Meteorology">meteorology</a> under <a href="/wiki/Vilhelm_Bjerknes" title="Vilhelm Bjerknes">Vilhelm Bjerknes</a>.</p>\n<p>He joined the Central Institution for the Training of Meteorologists (current <a href="/w/index.php?title=Meteorological_College_of_Japan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Meteorological College of Japan (page does not exist)">Meteorological College of Japan</a>) as general director after returning to Japan in 1922. He started his tenure as a professor at Tokyo Imperial University in 1924, and succeeded Takematsu Okada as the fifth director of the <a href="/wiki/Japan_Meteorological_Agency" title="Japan Meteorological Agency">Japan Meteorological Agency</a> in 1941.</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life">Later life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sakuhei_Fujiwhara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Later life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Fujiwhara participated in the development of the <a href="/wiki/Fire_balloon" title="Fire balloon">fire balloon</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_War" title="Pacific War">Pacific War</a>, and was purged from his position after the conclusion of the war. He retreated to the countryside afterwards to concentrate on his writing, and devoted his efforts to educating the future generation of meteorologists and researching meteorological phenomena such as vortices, clouds and atmospheric optics. He also spearheaded the study of <a href="/wiki/Glider_aircraft" class="mw-redirect" title="Glider aircraft">gliders</a> in Japan, and became a member of the <a href="/wiki/Japan_Academy" title="Japan Academy">Japan Academy</a> in 1937.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Texts">Texts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sakuhei_Fujiwhara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Texts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul>\n<li><i>Kumo wo Tsukamu Hanashi</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">\xe9\x9b\xb2\xe3\x82\x92\xe6\x8e\xb4\xe3\x82\x80\xe8\xa9\xb1</span>, lit.
