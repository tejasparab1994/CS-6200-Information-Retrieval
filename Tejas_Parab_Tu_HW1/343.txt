colspan="2"></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Super Typhoon Nancy</b>, also known as the <b>2nd Muroto Typhoon</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">\xe7\xac\xac\xe4\xba\x8c\xe5\xae\xa4\xe6\x88\xb8\xe5\x8f\xb0\xe9\xa2\xa8</span><span style="display:none">,</span> <i>Daini-muroto Taif\xc5\xab</i>)</span>, was an extremely powerful tropical cyclone of the <a href="/wiki/1961_Pacific_typhoon_season" title="1961 Pacific typhoon season">1961 Pacific typhoon season</a> and one of <a href="/wiki/List_of_the_most_intense_tropical_cyclones" title="List of the most intense tropical cyclones">the most intense tropical cyclones</a> on record. The system had possibly the strongest winds ever measured in a tropical cyclone, tied with <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Patricia" title="Hurricane Patricia">Hurricane Patricia</a> of 2015. It caused extensive damage and at least 173 deaths and thousands of injuries in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> and elsewhere in September 1961.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div id="toc" class="tocnumber">6</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n<p></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Nancy_(1961)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nancy_1961_track.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Nancy_1961_track.png/220px-Nancy_1961_track.png" width="220" height="220" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Nancy_1961_track.png/330px-Nancy_1961_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Nancy_1961_track.png/440px-Nancy_1961_track.png 2x" data-file-width="2700" data-file-height="2700" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nancy_1961_track.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nMap plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>A tropical depression formed from a <a href="/wiki/Low_pressure_area" class="mw-redirect" title="Low pressure area">low</a> near <a href="/wiki/Kwajalein_Atoll" title="Kwajalein Atoll">Kwajalein Atoll</a> on September 7. It strengthened rapidly; by the time position fixes could be taken, Nancy was nearly a super typhoon. Moving gradually westward, Nancy explosively deepened and reached wind speeds equivalent to a Category&#160;5 (<a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" class="mw-redirect" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson Hurricane Scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson Hurricane Scale</a>) on September 9.<sup id="cite_ref-Unisys_Data_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Unisys_Data-1">[1]</a></sup> It would maintain that intensity for the next several days.</p>\n<p>Shortly after reaching peak intensity, Nancy approached the <a href="/wiki/Ryukyu_Islands" title="Ryukyu Islands">Ry\xc5\xabky\xc5\xab Islands</a> and began turning. It passed near <a href="/wiki/Okinawa" class="mw-redirect" title="Okinawa">Okinawa</a> and over <a href="/w/index.php?title=Haze_(island)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Haze (island) (page does not exist)">Haze</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">ridge</a> steering Nancy broke down, and the typhoon turned sharply and headed towards Japan. Nancy made landfall as a strong typhoon on September 16 as it passed directly over <a href="/wiki/Muroto,_K%C5%8Dchi" title="Muroto, K\xc5\x8dchi">Muroto Zaki</a>. Nancy made a second landfall on <a href="/wiki/Honsh%C5%AB" class="mw-redirect" title="Honsh\xc5\xab">Honsh\xc5\xab</a> near <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">typhoon</a> rapidly traveled up the length of the island as it continued accelerating, eventually reaching a forward speed of 65&#160;mph (100&#160;km/h, 55&#160;<a href="/wiki/Knot_(unit)" title="Knot (unit)">knots</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-JTWC_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JTWC-2">[2]</a></sup> The typhoon quickly crossed over <a href="/wiki/Hokkaid%C5%8D" class="mw-redirect" title="Hokkaid\xc5\x8d">Hokkaid\xc5\x8d</a> before entering the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Okhotsk" title="Sea of Okhotsk">Sea of Okhotsk</a> as a tropical storm. Nancy went <a href="/wiki/Extratropical" class="mw-redirect" title="Extratropical">extratropical</a> on September 17. The extratropical system eventually crossed over <a href="/wiki/Kamchatka" class="mw-redirect" title="Kamchatka">Kamchatka</a> and entered the open ocean.<sup id="cite_ref-Typhoon_List_View_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Typhoon_List_View-3">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Nancy_(1961)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Although no monetary value of all damage is known, damage was "phenomenal"<sup id="cite_ref-JTWC_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JTWC-2">[2]</a></sup> in all areas where Nancy hit. There were at least 173 deaths and 19 people unaccounted for.</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Guam">Guam</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Nancy_(1961)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Guam">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>On <a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">Guam</a>, over half of all crops were destroyed by heavy winds and rain. A total of $40,000 (1961 <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">USD</a>) worth of damage was done to roads on the island. Most of the damage was on the southern end of the island. No deaths were reported on Guam.<sup id="cite_ref-JTWC_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JTWC-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Japan">Japan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Nancy_(1961)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Japan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In Japan, 172 people were killed, 18 were missing, and 3,184 people were injured. These totals made Nancy the sixth-deadliest typhoon to hit Japan at the time. Timely warnings and adequate preparations were probably responsible for the relatively low death toll. The damage was "small" relative to other typhoons that impacted densely populated areas of Japan.<sup id="cite_ref-JTWC_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JTWC-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Hundreds of thousands of people had their lives disrupted. Super typhoon Nancy destroyed 11,539 houses, damaged 32,604 homes, and flooded 280,078 others. Although the exact number may never be known, the <i><a href="/wiki/Stars_and_Stripes_(newspaper)" title="Stars and Stripes (newspaper)">Stars and Stripes</a></i> reported in late September that over 1,056 ships and fishing vessels were sunk or blown ashore and many more were damaged.<sup id="cite_ref-JTWC_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JTWC-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Floodwaters washed away 566 <a href="/wiki/Bridge_(structure)" class="mw-redirect" title="Bridge (structure)">bridges</a> and caused 1146 <a href="/wiki/Landslide" title="Landslide">landslides</a>. Roads were destroyed at a total of 2,053 locations.<sup id="cite_ref-JTWC_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JTWC-2">[2]</a></sup> Damages in <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a> amounted to $500&#160;million (1961&#160;USD).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>\n<p>On <a href="/wiki/Okinawa" class="mw-redirect" title="Okinawa">Okinawa</a>, low-lying areas experienced heavy flooding, which did significant damage to agriculture and structures.<sup id="cite_ref-JTWC_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JTWC-2">[2]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/Amami-o-Shima" class="mw-redirect" title="Amami-o-Shima">Amami-o-Shima</a>, one person was missing and another was badly injured. A ship was sunk. Extensive flooding of crops and houses left 152 people homeless.<sup id="cite_ref-JTWC_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JTWC-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Due to Nancy\'s damage and death toll, the <a href="/wiki/Japan_Meteorological_Agency" title="Japan Meteorological Agency">Japan Meteorological Agency</a> named Nancy the "Second <a href="/wiki/Muroto" class="mw-redirect" title="Muroto">Muroto</a> Typhoon". Nancy is one of only eight typhoons to receive special names in Japan.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Records">Records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Nancy_(1961)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>A reconnaissance aircraft flying into the typhoon near its peak intensity on September 12 determined Nancy\'s one-minute sustained winds to be 185&#160;knots (215&#160;mph; 345&#160;km/h). If these values are reliable, they would be the highest wind speeds ever measured in a tropical cyclone.<sup id="cite_ref-FAQ1_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FAQ1-5">[5]</a></sup> However, it was later determined that measurements and estimations of wind speeds from the 1940s to 1960s were excessive. Thus, Nancy\'s winds may actually be lower than its official best-track value.<sup id="cite_ref-FAQ1_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FAQ1-5">[5]</a></sup> In 2016, reanalysis of <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Patricia" title="Hurricane Patricia">Hurricane Patricia</a> noted that the storm had 215&#160;mph (345&#160;km/h) sustained winds, which ties Nancy and is the highest sustained winds in the <a href="/wiki/Western_Hemisphere" title="Western Hemisphere">Western Hemisphere</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-patriciapressrelease_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-patriciapressrelease-6">[6]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Although the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson hurricane wind scale</a> (SSHWS) didn\'t exist at the time, Nancy would have been a Category&#160;5 equivalent for a total of five and a half days (or 132 hours), assuming the windspeed data is reliable. This is a record for the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Hemisphere" title="Northern Hemisphere">Northern Hemisphere</a> and more than a day longer than the next-highest system, 1962\'s <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Karen" title="Typhoon Karen">Typhoon Karen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FAQ2_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FAQ2-7">[7]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline"
