class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sea_spray&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Sea_salt_aerosol" title="Sea salt aerosol">Sea salt aerosol</a> (SSA) contains both inorganic salts and organic matter from the ocean.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It is thought that the amount of organic matter transferred to SSA depends on microbiological activity.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The organic matter in sea spray can contain <a href="/wiki/Dissolved_organic_carbon" title="Dissolved organic carbon">dissolved organic matter</a><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> or even microbes themselves, like bacteria and viruses.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Effects">Effects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sea_spray&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Effects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Salt spray is largely responsible for <a href="/wiki/Corrosion" title="Corrosion">corrosion</a> of metallic objects near the coastline, as the salts accelerate the corrosion process in the presence of abundant atmospheric <a href="/wiki/Oxygen" title="Oxygen">oxygen</a> and moisture. Salts do not dissolve in air directly, but are suspended as fine <a href="/wiki/Particulates" title="Particulates">particulates</a>, or dissolved in microscopic airborne water droplets.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chemical_resistance">Chemical resistance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sea_spray&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Chemical resistance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Salt_spray_test" title="Salt spray test">salt spray test</a> is the notable measure of material endurance, particularly if the material will be used outdoors and must perform in a <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mechanical_load_bearing&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mechanical load bearing (page does not exist)">mechanical load bearing</a> or otherwise critical role.
