Ocean cyclone season</a></b></td>\n</tr>\n<tr style="display:none">\n<td colspan="2"></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Very Intense Tropical Cyclone Gafilo</b> (<small>French pronunciation:&#160;\xe2\x80\x8b</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/French" title="Help:IPA/French">[\xc9\xa1afil]</a></span>; also known as <b>Cyclone Gafilo</b>) was the <a href="/wiki/List_of_the_most_intense_tropical_cyclones#South-West_Indian_Ocean" title="List of the most intense tropical cyclones">most intense tropical cyclone</a> ever recorded in the <a href="/wiki/South-West_Indian_Ocean_tropical_cyclone_climatology" class="mw-redirect" title="South-West Indian Ocean tropical cyclone climatology">South-West Indian Ocean</a> and the most intense <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> worldwide in 2004. Being unusually large and violent, Gafilo was the deadliest and most destructive cyclone of the <a href="/wiki/2003%E2%80%9304_South-West_Indian_Ocean_cyclone_season" title="2003\xe2\x80\x9304 South-West Indian Ocean cyclone season">2003\xe2\x80\x9304 season</a>. According to the EM-DAT International Disaster Database, Gafilo killed at least 363 people.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The Gafilo cyclone also caused about $250 million (2004 USD) damages in <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a>, which makes it one of most devastating storms to hit the country on reliable record.</p>\n<p>Forming south of <a href="/wiki/Diego_Garcia" title="Diego Garcia">Diego Garcia</a>, it intensified into a moderate tropical storm on 3 March. One day later, Gafilo became a tropical cyclone, and it ultimately intensified into a very intense tropical cyclone on 6 March, prior to making landfall over Madagascar early on the next day. After crossing the island, Gafilo emerged into the <a href="/wiki/Mozambique_Channel" title="Mozambique Channel">Mozambique Channel</a> and made landfall over Madagascar again on 9 March. After a three-day loop overland, the system arrived at the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a> on 13 March, and it transitioned into a <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_cyclone" title="Subtropical cyclone">subtropical depression</a> on 14 March.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc">\n<div href="/w/index.php?title=Cyclone_Gafilo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Gafilo_2004_track.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Gafilo_2004_track.png/220px-Gafilo_2004_track.png" width="220" height="136" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Gafilo_2004_track.png/330px-Gafilo_2004_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Gafilo_2004_track.png/440px-Gafilo_2004_track.png 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="633" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Gafilo_2004_track.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nMap plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>A disturbance formed south of <a href="/wiki/Diego_Garcia" title="Diego Garcia">Diego Garcia</a> on 29 February, and <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A9t%C3%A9o-France" title="M\xc3\xa9t\xc3\xa9o-France">M\xc3\xa9t\xc3\xa9o-France</a> <a href="/wiki/La_R%C3%A9union" class="mw-redirect" title="La R\xc3\xa9union">La R\xc3\xa9union</a> (MFR) upgraded the identified system within the <a href="/wiki/Monsoon_trough" title="Monsoon trough">monsoon trough</a> to a tropical disturbance on the next day. On 2 March, although the centre was exposed due to moderate easterly vertical <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Joint_Typhoon_Warning_Center" title="Joint Typhoon Warning Center">Joint Typhoon Warning Center</a> (JTWC) issued a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Cyclone_Formation_Alert" title="Tropical Cyclone Formation Alert">Tropical Cyclone Formation Alert</a> on the system which was situated north of a <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_ridge" title="Subtropical ridge">subtropical ridge</a>, based on improved organisation of deep convection and moderate poleward <a href="/wiki/Outflow_(meteorology)" title="Outflow (meteorology)">outflow</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> However, its temporary acceleration on that day lessened vertical wind shear.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> As the centre became under organised deep convection on 3 March, the system intensified into a tropical depression, then a moderate tropical storm named <i>Gafilo</i> by the Sub-regional Tropical Cyclone Advisory Centre in <a href="/wiki/Mauritius" title="Mauritius">Mauritius</a>, shortly before MFR even upgraded Gafilo to a severe tropical storm later. When the system slowed down and tracked west-northwestwards along the northern periphery of a building <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_ridge" title="Subtropical ridge">subtropical ridge</a>, it reached category 1 strength on the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale" class="mw-redirect" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson hurricane wind scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson hurricane wind scale</a> late on 3 March.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The complete pattern of the cyclone from March 4 to March 8 as it moved through the south western Indian Ocean has been recorded by the TRMM through its satellite imagery. TRMM (Tropical Rainfall Measuring Mission) is a joint mission between NASA and the Japanese space agency JAXA.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Gafilo_2004-03-03.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Gafilo_2004-03-03.jpg/220px-Gafilo_2004-03-03.jpg" width="220" height="220" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Gafilo_2004-03-03.jpg/330px-Gafilo_2004-03-03.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Gafilo_2004-03-03.jpg/440px-Gafilo_2004-03-03.jpg 2x" data-file-width="6000" data-file-height="6000" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Gafilo_2004-03-03.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nTropical Cyclone Gafilo on 3 March</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Although dry air significantly reduced deep convection on 4 March, MFR upgraded Gafilo to a tropical cyclone because of a banding <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a>, when the system began to expand its clockwise circulation and track west-southwestwards.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> By dual <a href="/wiki/Outflow_(meteorology)" title="Outflow (meteorology)">outflow</a> channels, Gafilo underwent <a href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapid deepening">rapid deepening</a> and reached category 4 strength on the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale" class="mw-redirect" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson hurricane wind scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson hurricane wind scale</a> on 5 March, as MFR also upgraded Gafilo to an intense tropical cyclone late on the same day.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> On 6 March, besides warm <a href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperature" title="Sea surface temperature">sea surface temperature</a> above 29&#160;\xc2\xb0C, the atypical third outflow channel from the west made conditions for the cyclone more favourable.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Therefore, MFR upgraded Gafilo to a very intense tropical cyclone with a very well-defined circular and warm 20 nautical miles (37&#160;km; 23&#160;mi) diameter eye at 06Z, when it also reached category 5 strength on the Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson hurricane wind scale.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> At 12Z, Gafilo attained its peak intensity by the ten-minute maximum sustained winds reaching 125 knots (230&#160;km/h, 145&#160;mph) and the atmospheric pressure decreasing to 895&#160;hPa (26.4&#160;inHg), although microwave imagery showed that the <a href="/wiki/Eyewall_replacement_cycle" title="Eyewall replacement cycle">eyewall replacement cycle</a> had begun.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Shortly after slightly weakening due to land interaction, Gafilo made landfall in the vicinity of <a href="/wiki/Antalaha" title="Antalaha">Antalaha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a> early on 7 March. Unlike other storms, the overland depression just weakened gradually, because of its unusually large circulation making the outer part still at sea.<sup id="cite_ref-meteofrance6869_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-meteofrance6869-11">[11]</a></sup> On that day, Gafilo tracked more southwestwards along the northwestern periphery of a <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_ridge" title="Subtropical ridge">subtropical ridge</a> located to the southeast.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Before noon on 8 March, Gafilo arrived at the <a href="/wiki/Mozambique_Channel" title="Mozambique Channel">Mozambique Channel</a> as a moderate tropical storm whose strongest winds were located in the northern sector.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> On 9 March, Gafilo became almost stationary and then tracked southeastwards along the western periphery of a <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_ridge" title="Subtropical ridge">subtropical ridge</a> located to the east. MFR upgraded the system to a severe tropical storm supported by observed data from Malagasy stations at noon; however, JTWC analysed that the storm became below category 1 strength on the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale" class="mw-redirect" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson hurricane wind scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson hurricane wind scale</a> at the same time in their best track data.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Late on 9 March, Gafilo made landfall north of <a href="/wiki/Morombe" title="Morombe">Morombe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a>, and the system started to make a three-day clockwise loop over the south part of Madagascar.<sup id="cite_ref-meteofrance6869_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-meteofrance6869-11">[11]</a></sup> For the system further weakened after landfall, JTWC analysed that Gafilo dissipated overland late on 11 March, as well as MFR issued a final warning on the overland depression which began to track southeastwards early on 12 March. Surprisingly, MFR began to issue warnings again on the completely disorganised system at noon on 12 March, as the residual centre was expected to go back overseas. Late on the same day, JTWC issued a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Cyclone_Formation_Alert" title="Tropical Cyclone Formation Alert">Tropical Cyclone Formation Alert</a> on the overland remnants, because of improved deep convection over the low-level circulation centre, poleward <a href="/wiki/Outflow_(meteorology)" title="Outflow (meteorology)">outflow</a> and weak vertical <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Early on 13 March, Gafilo arrived at the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a> as a tropical disturbance; JTWC cancelled a Tropical Cyclone Formation Alert on the system because of the less organised low-level circulation centre and moderate vertical wind shear.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Gafilo transferred into a <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_cyclone" title="Subtropical cyclone">subtropical depression</a> south of <a href="/wiki/La_R%C3%A9union" class="mw-redirect" title="La R\xc3\xa9union">La R\xc3\xa9union</a> on 14 March, when the deep convective activity was intensifying with better organisation of a small cluster above the low-level circulation centre due to good upper-level divergence and a warm <a href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperature" title="Sea surface temperature">sea surface temperature</a> of 26&#160;\xc2\xb0C to 29&#160;\xc2\xb0C.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> After attaining minor peak intensity in its subtropical period late on 14 March, Gafilo began to track eastward and transitioned into an <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical depression</a> on 15 March. Owing to a building <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_ridge" title="Subtropical ridge">subtropical ridge</a> located to the southwest, the extratropical depression slowed down and turned northwestwards on 16 March.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> After slowly weakening, the system completely dissipated on 18 March.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyclone_Gafilo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Madagascar">Madagascar</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyclone_Gafilo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Madagascar">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Cyclone_Gafilo_Mahajanga.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Cyclone_Gafilo_Mahajanga.jpg/220px-Cyclone_Gafilo_Mahajanga.jpg" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Cyclone_Gafilo_Mahajanga.jpg/330px-Cyclone_Gafilo_Mahajanga.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Cyclone_Gafilo_Mahajanga.jpg/440px-Cyclone_Gafilo_Mahajanga.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2400" data-file-height="1600" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Cyclone_Gafilo_Mahajanga.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nDestroyed coastline of <a href="/wiki/Mahajanga" title="Mahajanga">Mahajanga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Gafilo caused strong winds and torrential rainfall over <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a>. A half of day after the first landfall, <a href="/wiki/Beaufort_scale" title="Beaufort scale">hurricane-force</a> winds were still observed near <a href="/wiki/Mahajanga" title="Mahajanga">Mahajanga</a> late on 7 March. When Gafilo was reorganising in the <a href="/wiki/Mozambique_Channel" title="Mozambique Channel">Mozambique Channel</a>, 190&#160;mm (7.5&#160;in) of <a href="/wiki/Precipitation_(meteorology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Precipitation (meteorology)">precipitation</a> was recorded at <a href="/wiki/Maintirano" title="Maintirano">Maintirano</a> on 9 March, as well as 242&#160;mm (9.5&#160;in) was recorded at <a href="/wiki/T%C3%B4lanaro" title="T\xc3\xb4lanaro">T\xc3\xb4lanaro</a> on the same day. During its three-day loop overland, Gafilo brought 238&#160;mm (9.4&#160;in) at <a href="/wiki/Morondava" title="Morondava">Morondava</a> on 11 March.<sup id="cite_ref-meteofrance6869_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-meteofrance6869-11">[11]</a></sup></p>\n<p>As <a href="/wiki/Cyclone_Elita" title="Cyclone Elita">Elita</a> has already struck Madagascar one month ago, damage from Gafilo became extremely devastating in the country. The National Rescue Council in <a href="/wiki/Antananarivo" title="Antananarivo">Antananarivo</a> reported 237 dead, 181 missing, 879 injured, and 304,000 homeless (among which 174,000 in <a href="/wiki/Antalaha" title="Antalaha">Antalaha</a> alone). More than 20,000 homes were destroyed, as well as 413 public buildings and 3,400 schools were damaged, including 1,400 completely destroyed ones. The <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> estimated that there were 700,000 disaster-stricken, 280,000 of which needed urgent assistance.<sup id="cite_ref-meteofrance6869_11-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-meteofrance6869-11">[11]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Despite being located 300&#160;km (190&#160;mi) north of Gafilo\xe2\x80\x99s centre, the shipwreck of the ferry \xe2\x80\x98Samson\xe2\x80\x99 still happened at night on 7 March, offshore the northwestern port of <a href="/wiki/Mahajanga" title="Mahajanga">Mahajanga</a>. A narrow but strong band of Gafilo caused very rough conditions at sea, making the ferry undergo an engine failure and tip over almost instantaneously. While the number of fatalities remains undetermined (120 people were officially onboard), only three people drifted, ran aground and survived from the shipwreck.The ferry was heading to Madagascar from the Coromos Islands.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In Antalaha, food prices shot up by 35%, and the harbour was badly damaged and not operational. Roads and bridges connecting the city to the outlying villages were inaccessible, as well as electricity, water and telephone lines were cut.<sup id="cite_ref-ifrc_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ifrc-21">[21]</a></sup> Overall in Madagascar, the main damage caused by Gafilo was the subsequent flooding of vast areas in the north, northwest and southwest, where extensive the flooding had caused serious damage to <a href="/wiki/Vanilla" title="Vanilla">vanilla</a>, rice and banana crops. In many places, the crops were expected to be totally lost.<sup id="cite_ref-meteofrance6869_11-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-meteofrance6869-11">[11]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Moreover, <a href="/wiki/Shrimp" title="Shrimp">shrimp</a> harvesting also suffered. Some of the concerned areas coincided with those affected by <a href="/wiki/Cyclone_Elita" title="Cyclone Elita">Elita</a>, and stocks and resources had therefore been depleted in many areas. In the absence of immediate response, an increase in water borne diseases such as <a href="/wiki/Malaria" title="Malaria">malaria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Diarrhoea" class="mw-redirect" title="Diarrhoea">diarrhoea</a> could have had an immediate occurrence, while outbreaks of <a href="/wiki/Cholera" title="Cholera">cholera</a> were expected to be witnessed within the coming 6\xe2\x80\x938 weeks.<sup id="cite_ref-ifrc_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ifrc-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_islands">Other islands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyclone_Gafilo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Other islands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Damage was minimal to other islands in the South-west Indian Ocean, but <a href="/wiki/Precipitation_(meteorology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Precipitation (meteorology)">precipitation</a> was very heavy. Far from the centre of Gafilo, 393&#160;mm (15.5&#160;in) was recorded at <a href="/wiki/La_R%C3%A9union" class="mw-redirect" title="La R\xc3\xa9union">La R\xc3\xa9union</a> within 48 hours, and 159&#160;mm (6.3&#160;in) was recorded at <a href="/wiki/Seychelles" title="Seychelles">Seychelles</a> on 4 March. Situated 160&#160;km (99&#160;mi) south of Gafilo, ten-minute maximum sustained winds were 49 knots with a peak gust of 66 knots recorded at <a href="/wiki/Tromelin_Island" title="Tromelin Island">Tromelin Island</a>. During 7 and 8 March, precipitation at <a href="/wiki/Mayotte" title="Mayotte">Mayotte</a> was recorded between 100 and 275&#160;mm (3.9 and 10.8&#160;in).<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyclone_Gafilo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Following the passage of Gafilo, the Malagasy government passed Inter-ministerial Order 17939/2004 in September 2004, which cleared the way for export of new and existing stocks of <a href="/wiki/Rosewood" title="Rosewood">rosewood</a> as \xe2\x80\x98salvage\xe2\x80\x99. This created anarchy in the national parks in the <a href="/wiki/Sava_Region" title="Sava Region">Sava Region</a>, with loggers extracting a large amounts of rosewood and <a href="/wiki/Ebony" title="Ebony">ebony</a>, grossly disproportionate to the amount of damage caused by the storm.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GW_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GW-25">[25]</a></sup> During this time, <a href="/wiki/Marojejy_National_Park" title="Marojejy National Park">Marojejy National Park</a> reported that with the granting of export rights, logging in the park had resumed.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> It was not until 2006, with the passing of Inter-ministerial Order 16030/2006, that the export ban was reinstated, nearly two years after the storm, but not before the exporters lobbied the government for an extension \xe2\x80\x98following the grievances expressed by operators\xe2\x80\x99 in October 2005, per Memorandum 923/05.<sup id="cite_ref-GW_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GW-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-WildMadagascar_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WildMadagascar-27">[27]</a></sup> Exports were also authorized following cyclones in 2006 and 2007, encouraging the stockpiling of large quantities of lumber in both legal depots and hidden caches around the ports of <a href="/wiki/Voh%C3%A9mar" class="mw-redirect" title="Voh\xc3\xa9mar">Voh\xc3\xa9mar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Antalaha" title="Antalaha">Antalaha</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WildMadagascar_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WildMadagascar-27">[27]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Records">Records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyclone_Gafilo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>With ten-minute maximum sustained winds of 125 <a href="/wiki/Knot_(unit)" title="Knot (unit)">knots</a> (230&#160;km/h, 145&#160;mph, (later equaled by <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_South-West_Indian_Ocean_cyclone_season#Very_Intense_Tropical_Cyclone_Bruce" title="2013\xe2\x80\x9314 South-West Indian Ocean cyclone season">Bruce</a> in 2013 and <a href="/wiki/Cyclone_Hellen" title="Cyclone Hellen">Hellen</a> in 2014 and surpassed by <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_South-West_Indian_Ocean_cyclone_season#Very_Intense_Tropical_Cyclone_Eunice" title="2014\xe2\x80\x9315 South-West Indian Ocean cyclone season">Eunice</a> in 2015 with 10-min sustained winds of 130 <a href="/wiki/Knot_(unit)" title="Knot (unit)">knots</a> (240&#160;km/h, 150&#160;mph) and <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_South-West_Indian_Ocean_cyclone_season#Very_Intense_Tropical_Cyclone_Fantala" title="2015\xe2\x80\x9316 South-West Indian Ocean cyclone season">Fantala</a> in 2016 with 10-min sustained winds of 135 <a href="/wiki/Knot_(unit)" title="Knot (unit)">knots</a> (250&#160;km/h, 155&#160;mph)) and an <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" title="Atmospheric pressure">atmospheric pressure</a> of 895&#160;hPa (26.4&#160;inHg), Gafilo became the <a href="/wiki/List_of_the_most_intense_tropical_cyclones#South-West_Indian_Ocean" title="List of the most intense tropical cyclones">most intense tropical cyclone</a> in the South-West Indian Ocean since reliable records. In addition, Gafilo was the only tropical cyclone in the basin having the atmospheric pressure below 900&#160;hPa (27&#160;inHg) since <a href="/wiki/1981%E2%80%9382_South-West_Indian_Ocean_cyclone_season#Intense_Tropical_Cyclone_Chris-Damia" title="1981\xe2\x80\x9382 South-West Indian Ocean cyclone season">Chris-Damia</a> in 1982. Gafilo made landfall over Madagascar early on 7 March as a very intense tropical cyclone with ten-minute maximum sustained winds of 120 knots (220&#160;km/h, 140&#160;mph) and an <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" title="Atmospheric pressure">atmospheric pressure</a> of 905&#160;hPa (26.7&#160;inHg), becoming the strongest storm to make landfall over Madagascar and even Africa on record, tie with <a href="/wiki/Cyclone_Hary" title="Cyclone Hary">Hary</a> in 2002.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
