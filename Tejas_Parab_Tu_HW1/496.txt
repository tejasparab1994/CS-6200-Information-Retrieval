2003</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>The <b>Icelandic Low</b> is a semi-permanent centre of low <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" title="Atmospheric pressure">atmospheric pressure</a> found between <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a> and southern <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a> and extending in the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Hemisphere" title="Northern Hemisphere">Northern Hemisphere</a> winter into the <a href="/wiki/Barents_Sea" title="Barents Sea">Barents Sea</a>. In summer it weakens and splits into two centres, one near <a href="/wiki/Davis_Strait" title="Davis Strait">Davis Strait</a> and the other west of Iceland. It is a principal centre of action in the atmosphere circulation of the Northern Hemisphere, associated with frequent <a href="/wiki/Cyclone" title="Cyclone">cyclone</a> activity. It forms one pole of the <a href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_oscillation" title="North Atlantic oscillation">North Atlantic oscillation</a>, the other being the <a href="/wiki/Azores_High" title="Azores High">Azores High</a>.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
