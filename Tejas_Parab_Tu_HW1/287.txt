title="Government of Australia">Australian Government</a> responsible for providing weather services to Australia and surrounding areas. It was established in 1906 under the Meteorology Act, and brought together the <a href="/wiki/States_and_territories_of_Australia" title="States and territories of Australia">state</a> meteorological services that existed before then. The states officially transferred their weather recording responsibilities to the Bureau of Meteorology on 1 January 1908.<sup id="cite_ref-ABC_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ABC-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<p></p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc">\n<div id="Services_and_structure">Services and structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bureau_of_Meteorology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Services and structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Berrimah-radar-darwin-nt.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Berrimah-radar-darwin-nt.jpg/220px-Berrimah-radar-darwin-nt.jpg" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Berrimah-radar-darwin-nt.jpg/330px-Berrimah-radar-darwin-nt.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Berrimah-radar-darwin-nt.jpg/440px-Berrimah-radar-darwin-nt.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3008" data-file-height="2000" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Berrimah-radar-darwin-nt.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nBerrimah radar</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>The Bureau of Meteorology is the main provider of <a href="/wiki/Weather_forecast" class="mw-redirect" title="Weather forecast">weather forecasts</a>, warnings and observations to the Australian public. The Bureau distributes weather images via <a href="/wiki/Radiofax" title="Radiofax">radiofax</a> and is responsible for issuing <a href="/wiki/Flood_alerts" class="mw-redirect" title="Flood alerts">flood alerts</a> in Australia.</p>\n<p>The Bureau\'s head office is in <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_Docklands" class="mw-redirect" title="Melbourne Docklands">Melbourne Docklands</a>, which includes the Bureau\'s Research Centre, the Bureau National Operations Centre, the National Climate Centre, the Victorian Regional Forecasting Centre as well as the Hydrology and Satellite sections.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Regional offices are located in each state and territory capital. Each regional office includes a Regional Forecasting Centre and a Flood Warning Centre, and the <a href="/wiki/Perth,_Western_Australia" class="mw-redirect" title="Perth, Western Australia">Perth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Darwin,_Northern_Territory" title="Darwin, Northern Territory">Darwin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brisbane" title="Brisbane">Brisbane</a> offices also house <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Cyclone_Warning_Centre" class="mw-redirect" title="Tropical Cyclone Warning Centre">Tropical Cyclone Warning Centres</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Adelaide" title="Adelaide">Adelaide</a> office incorporates the <a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Tidal_Centre&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="National Tidal Centre (page does not exist)">National Tidal Centre</a>, while the Darwin office the <a href="/wiki/Volcanic_Ash_Advisory_Centre" class="mw-redirect" title="Volcanic Ash Advisory Centre">Volcanic Ash Advisory Centre</a> and <a href="/wiki/Regional_Specialised_Meteorological_Centre" class="mw-redirect" title="Regional Specialised Meteorological Centre">Regional Specialised Meteorological Centre</a> (Analysis).</p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Darwin_Airport_Met_Office.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/Darwin_Airport_Met_Office.jpg/220px-Darwin_Airport_Met_Office.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/Darwin_Airport_Met_Office.jpg/330px-Darwin_Airport_Met_Office.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/Darwin_Airport_Met_Office.jpg/440px-Darwin_Airport_Met_Office.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1500" data-file-height="1125" /></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Darwin_Airport_Met_Office.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\n<a href="/wiki/Darwin_International_Airport" title="Darwin International Airport">Darwin Airport</a> office</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>The Australian Bureau of Meteorology issues <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">Tropical Cyclone</a> Advices<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and developed the <a href="/wiki/Standard_Emergency_Warning_Signal" title="Standard Emergency Warning Signal">Standard Emergency Warning Signal</a> used for warnings. The Bureau is responsible for <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">tropical cyclone naming</a> for storms in waters surrounding Australia. Three lists of names used to be maintained, one for each of the western, northern and eastern Australian regions.<sup id="cite_ref-BOM_NAMES_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BOM_NAMES-5">[5]</a></sup> However, as of the start of the 2008\xe2\x80\x9309 Tropical Cyclone Year these lists have been rolled into one main national <a href="/wiki/Lists_of_tropical_cyclone_names#Australia" class="mw-redirect" title="Lists of tropical cyclone names">list of tropical cyclone names</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BOM_NAMES_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BOM_NAMES-5">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The regional offices are supported by the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bureau_National_Operations_Centre&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bureau National Operations Centre (page does not exist)">Bureau National Operations Centre</a> (BNOC) which is also located at the head office in <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_Docklands" class="mw-redirect" title="Melbourne Docklands">Melbourne Docklands</a>.</p>\n<p>The Bureau maintains a network of field offices across the continent, on neighbouring islands and in <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctica</a>. There is also a network of some 500 paid co-operative observers and approximately 6,000 voluntary rainfall observers.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Management">Management</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bureau_of_Meteorology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Management">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Director of Meteorology in the Bureau of Meteorology is Andrew Johnson, who commenced the position on 6 September 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Former_Directors">Former Directors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bureau_of_Meteorology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Former Directors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Former Directors of the Bureau of Meteorology are:</p>\n<table class="wikitable">\n<tr bgcolor="lightgrey">\n<th>Director</th>\n<th>Years</th>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Henry_Ambrose_Hunt" title="Henry Ambrose Hunt">Henry Ambrose Hunt</a></td>\n<td align="center">1908\xe2\x80\x9331</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td><a href="/w/index.php?title=William_S_Watt&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="William S Watt (page does not exist)">William S Watt</a></td>\n<td align="center">1931\xe2\x80\x9340</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td><a href="/w/index.php?title=H._Norman_Warren&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="H. id="Technology">Technology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bureau_of_Meteorology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit provides the operational computing capability for weather, climate, ocean and wave numerical prediction and simulation, while other <a href="/wiki/Unix" title="Unix">Unix</a> servers support the id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> on 30 June 2016 and decommissioned their Oracle HPC system in October 2016.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline"
