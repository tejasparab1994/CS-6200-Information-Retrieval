sound in dugout canoes and trapped fish in 1663 albemarle sound was made part of the province of carolina by king charles ii of england and given to eight lords proprietors one of these royal beneficiaries was george monck 1st duke of albemarle for whom the sound is named.3 fishing was a major industry in the albemarle sound in late spring plantation farmers would fish for shad striped bass and herring nets used by these fishermen were sometimes enormous with some more than a mile 1600 m long and were frequently staffed 24 hours a day herring was cut and salted for export to europe while shad was packed in ice and shipped up the chowan river to be sold in northern colonies regional striped bass tournaments attracted sport fishermen to the area and it was considered by many to be
