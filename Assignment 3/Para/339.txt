typhoon tip  contents  meteorological historyedit impactedit heavy rainfall from the typhoon breached a flood-retaining wall at camp fuji a training facility for the united states marine corps near yokosuka.6 marines inside the camp weathered the storm inside huts situated at the base of a hill which housed a fuel farm the breach led to hoses being dislodged from two rubber storage bladders releasing large quantities of fuel the fuel flowed down the hill and was ignited by a heater used to warm one of the huts.789 the resultant fire killed 13 marines injured 681 and caused moderate damage to the facility the facility's barracks were destroyed6 along with fifteen huts and several other structures.710 the barracks were rebuilt6 and a memorial was established for those who lost their lives in the fire.7 records and meteorological statisticsedit despite the typhoon's intensity and damage the name tip was not retired and was reused in 1983 1986 and 1989.3 see alsoedit notesedit referencesedit external linksedit navigation menu personal tools namespaces variants views more search navigation interaction tools print/export in other projects languages
