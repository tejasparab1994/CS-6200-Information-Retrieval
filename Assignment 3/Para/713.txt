canadian hurricane centre the canadian hurricane centre chc is a division of the meteorological service of canada an agency of canada's department of the environment that advises canadians on the threat of tropical cyclones such as hurricanes and tropical storms founded in 1987 chc provides guidance to msc's weather centres in eastern and atlantic canada and is based in dartmouth nova scotia chc frequently consults with its united states counterpart the national hurricane center in miami to coordinate the tracks and positions of storms that pose a threat to canada  contents  historyedit hurricane gloria and its less-than-expected damage prompted environment canada to create the chc.1 its first hurricane warnings were issued in 2008 for hurricane kyle which struck near yarmouth nova scotia on september 28 2008 and quickly became extratropical while maintaining much of its strength into new brunswick and labrador dutiesedit frequencyedit while hurricanes are relatively uncommon in canada they do approach or strike from time to time recent occurrences include also of note due to its rarity and the damage caused is hurricane hazel which hit toronto ontario in 1954 which remained a strong storm even after crossing over the u.s east coast states referencesedit external linksedit navigation menu personal tools namespaces variants views more search navigation interaction tools print/export languages
